# 2026「交織與共構：藝術教育的多元軌跡」國際研討會2026 International Conference on

# “Interweaving and Co-Creation: Diverse Pathways of Arts Education”

## 一、 會議緣起

在當代藝術教育的場域中，教學、創作與研究不再沿著單一線性路徑前

進，而是由無數條軌跡彼此牽引、纏繞與分岔的動態網絡。課室中的課程設

計、偏鄉學校與在地社區的合作計畫、博物館與文化機構的教育推廣、結合輔

導與醫療與公共衛生的藝術介入，或是串連科技、設計、數位人文等領域的跨

域實驗，皆可視為構成藝術教育 DNA的一部分。藝術教育在不同脈絡中被書

寫、改寫與再連結，形塑出多元族群與世代相互牽動的發展路徑。

本研討會以「交織」與「軌跡」為核心意象，關注學校與社區、教育與實

務現場、藝術與其他專業領域之間如何在時間與空間中流變與勾連，形成可被

閱讀、對話與再創造的藝術教育譜系。我們以臺灣為起點，邀集亞洲與全球的

教育者、研究者、藝術家、社區行動者與跨領域實務工作者，分享教學實作、

研究成果、社區計畫與跨域合作，並回應聯合國永續發展目標（SDGs）所強調

的教育品質、公平與夥伴關係等核心精神。期盼在持續變動的社會與科技環境

中，共同重構藝術教育的「基因組」，共構指向未來的多元軌跡。

## 二、目的

「交織與共構：藝術教育的多元軌跡」國際研討會，承接國立彰化師範大

學藝術教育碩士班自 2021 年起持續舉辦藝術教育論壇與研討會的基礎，於

2026 年進一步擴展，促進跨校、跨領域與國際合作。研討會由國立彰化師範

大學美術學系藝術教育碩士班與國立中興大學數位人文與文創產業學士學位學

程暨圖書資訊學研究所共同主辦，並串聯 InSEA 亞洲區委員會、英國

Something Useful Project (SUP) 及相關專業學會與 NGO 等合作夥伴。本研

討會旨在建立國內外藝術教育學術與實踐者的交流平台，探討藝術教育在當代

社會多元脈絡下的發展方向與教育實踐。藉由跨領域對話與研究成果分享，深

化教育者對「多元文化」、「地方實踐」、「跨域融合」、「藝術作為社會行動」等

議題的理解。此外，本研討會提供藝術教育相關領域碩、博士生交流研究成果

與實踐的機會，期能激盪並提升學術素養與研究能力。本研討會主要目的如下：

（1） 促進藝術教育理論與實踐之交流與發展，建構亞洲與全球藝術教育學術網絡。

（2） 鼓勵教育工作者與研究者分享創新教學與研究成果，推動藝術教育專業成長。

（3） 深化跨域合作與文化對話，發展藝術教育的新視野與新方法。

（4） 透過藝術教育的多元軌跡，探討教育中，平等、文化認同與創造性學習的實踐模式。

## 三、 預期效益

（1） 建立國內外藝術教育研究與實務之交流平台，強化學術社群連結。

（2） 促進藝術教育理論與在地文化實踐之整合，提升藝術教育社會影響力。

（3） 鼓勵教師、學生及研究者發表研究成果，提升研究與創作之專業能量。

（4） 推動跨域合作與國際學術對話，促進臺灣藝術教育的全球能見度。

# 2026 International Conference on Interweaving and Co-Creation: Diverse Pathways of Arts Education

## 1. Background

In contemporary arts education, teaching, creative practice, and research no longer follow a single, linear route. They develop instead through many different pathways that cross, overlap, and sometimes move in new directions. Classroom curriculum design, collaborative projects between rural schools and local communities, educational programs in museums and cultural institutions, arts-based projects linked with counseling, healthcare, and public health, as well as interdisciplinary experiments that connect the arts with technology, design, and digital humanities, can all be seen as parts of the “DNA” of arts education. As these practices emerge, change, and reconnect in different contexts, they shape interconnected pathways of development for diverse communities and generations.

This conference is centered on the concepts of “interweaving” and “pathways.” It examines how schools and communities, teaching and professional practice, and the arts and other disciplines are interconnected across time and place, and how these connections form patterns of arts education that can be described, questioned, and reimagined. Starting from Taiwan, we invite educators, researchers, artists, community practitioners, and professionals from different fields across Asia and around the world to share teaching practices, research results, community projects, and cross-disciplinary collaborations, and to engage with key ideas related to educational quality, equity, and partnership as outlined in the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). In rapidly changing social and technological environments, we aim to collaborate in rethinking the “genetic code” of arts education and co-create diverse pathways for its future.

## 2. Objectives

The “Interweaving and Co-Creation: Diverse Pathways of Arts Education” International Conference builds on the experience gained since 2021, when the Master’s Program in Art Education at National Changhua University of Education (NCUE) began hosting annual arts education forums and conferences. In 2026, the event further develops to foster cross-institutional, interdisciplinary, and international collaboration. It is co-organized by the Master’s Program in Art Education, Department of Fine Arts, National Changhua University of Education, and the Program of Digital Humanities and Creative Industries & the Graduate Institute of Library and Information Science at National Chung Hsing University (NCHU), in association with the InSEA World Council and Asia Regional Council (ARC), the UKbased Something Useful Project (SUP), and related professional associations and NGOs.

The conference aims to provide a space for exchange among arts education scholars and practitioners from Taiwan and abroad, and to explore directions and practices of arts education within contemporary, diverse societies. Through interdisciplinary dialogue and the sharing of research and practice, it seeks to deepen educators’ understanding of issues such as multicultural education, local practices, crossdisciplinary collaboration, and art as social action. It also offers opportunities for master’s and doctoral students in arts-education-related fields to present and discuss their research and practice, with the aim of stimulating and strengthening their scholarly competence and research capacity.

## The main objectives are as follows:

1. To promote exchange and development between theory and practice in arts education, enabling scholars, teachers, and graduate students to discuss current directions in the field of arts education.

2. To encourage educators and researchers to share innovative teaching practices and research outcomes, thereby supporting professional growth in arts education.

3. To deepen cross-disciplinary collaboration and cultural dialogue, and to develop new perspectives and approaches in arts education.

4. To explore practices of equality, cultural identity, and creative learning in education through the diverse pathways of arts education.

## 3. Expected Outcomes

1. To establish channels for exchange between arts education research and practice in Taiwan and abroad, and to strengthen connections within the academic and professional community.

2. To promote the integration of arts education theory with local cultural practices, thereby enhancing the social impact of arts education.

3. To encourage teachers, students, and researchers to present their work, and to enhance professional capacity in research and creative practice.

4. To advance cross-disciplinary collaboration and international academic dialogue, and to increase the global visibility of arts education in Taiwan.

## 5. Conference Sub-themes

## 1. Arts Teaching and Learning Practices

Exploring teaching and learning processes in arts education across different levels of schooling and informal learning settings, including:

o Curriculum design, teaching methods, and action research

o Interpretations of learners’ works, learning narratives, and reflections

o Creative arts practices and connections with local communities

o Arts participation and support systems for rural communities, marginalized groups, or specific populations (such as immigrant families and Indigenous communities)

## 2. Research and Knowledge Innovation in Arts Education

Focusing on research and knowledge building related to arts education, including but not limited to:

o Developments in arts education theory, curriculum and instruction, assessment, and analysis of learning outcomes

o Analyses and critiques of education policies, curricula, and institutional structures

o Arts-based approaches and other arts-related or cross-disciplinary research designs and outcomes

## 3. Technological Applications and Interdisciplinary Futures

Exploring innovative possibilities arising from encounters between the arts, technology, and other fields, including:

o Arts × Technology practices (such as AI, STEAM, digital humanities, interaction design, game-based learning, etc.)

o New media and digital creation, and the application and reflection on online and hybrid teaching platforms

o Models of interdisciplinary collaboration linking the arts with science, the humanities, design, medicine, and other fields

## 4. Healing Practices and Social Empathy

Focusing on practices and research that examine how the arts generate healing and empathy at both individual and societal levels, including:

o The arts as a medium for healing, care, and self-awareness (such as art therapy, expressive arts, and creative accompaniment)

o The design and implementation of curricula or programmes that integrate Social and Emotional Learning (SEL) with the arts

o Arts-based interventions related to social issues, multiculturalism, social justice, civic engagement, and University Social Responsibility (USR)
