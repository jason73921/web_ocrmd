一、 密西根大學建校以來，在各學科領域中成就卓著並擁有巨大影響，多項調查顯示該大學超過 70％的專業排在全美前10名，被譽為「公立常春藤」，與加州大學伯克利分校以及威斯康辛大學素有「公立大學典範」之稱，是值得合作的美國大學。

二、 密西根大學迪爾伯恩分校(University of Michigan-Dearborn)簡介

1. 密西根大學（University of Michigan）是美國密西根州的一所世界名校，於 1817 年建校，是美國歷史最悠久的公立大學之一，在全世界享有盛譽，有「中西部哈佛」之美稱。有三個校區，分別是安娜堡（主校區，又譯安阿伯）、迪爾伯恩（Dearborn）和弗林特。其中密西根大學迪爾伯恩分校位於美國及世界的汽車工業中心底特律的迪爾伯恩區，其校區座落在美國汽車工業的發祥地福特莊園之內，與福特汽車公司總部及其研究中心相毗鄰。該分校設有工程與計算機學院、人文學院、管理學院和教育學院，在校生 9000多名。其工程與計算機學院有 14個研究生專業（包括博士、碩士學位），9個本科專業。校園面積為0.8平方公里。

2. 密西根大學於英國泰晤士高等教育（Times Higher Education）中的 2017 世界大學排名為21名，QS 世界大學排名中為 23名。在2017 US News 排名中，密西根大學迪爾伯恩分校在中西區名列第 40名。

Memorandum of Understanding   
Between   
The Regents of the University of Michigan   
On Behalf of

Dearborn, Michigan, USA

Taoyuan, Taiwan

WHEREAS, the University of Michigan-Dearborn (UM-D), located in Dearborn, Michigan, USA, and Yuan Ze University (YZU), located in Taoyuan, Taiwan, wish to undertake academic collaboration, offer educational opportunities for their students, and develop and implement faculty exchange programs;

NOW, THEREFORE, it is hereby agreed by and between the parties as follows:

1. YZU and UM-D wish to establish three separate collaborative education initiatives defined as follows: (a) 3+2 Program, (b) 1+1 Program, and (c) Master’s Program.

a) 3+2 Program: Qualified YZU students, after successfully completing the first three years of a four-year undergraduate program at YZU, can apply for admission to UM-D for two years of study, extendable to three years of study, to study as follows: (i) during year 1, study as undergraduate guest students to finish a required curriculum to qualify for their Bachelor’s degree awarded by YZU, (ii) during year 2 (and year 3, if needed), apply for admission and pursue one of the graduate programs at UM-D listed in Section 2.

b) 1+1 Program: Qualified YZU students, after successfully completing one year of graduate study at YZU (equivalent to at least 24 graduate credit hours), can apply for admission to one of the graduate programs at UM-D listed in Section 2. UM-D will review and consider transfers for up to six credit hours of appropriate graduate coursework not taken at UM-D, provided these credit hours have not been used for a degree elsewhere. Such transfers are at the sole discretion of the college or department governing the relevant program. For CECS programs, Rackham Graduate School at University of Michigan's normal credit transfer policies will also apply.

c) Master’s Program: Qualified YZU students, after receiving their undergraduate degrees at YZU, can apply for admission to one of the graduate programs at UM-D listed in Section 2.

All YZU students who study at UM-D under the terms of this agreement will receive scholarships as outlined in Section 6.

2. UM-D Graduate Programs Included in this Agreement

a) UM-D's College of Business (COB) offers Master of Science (MS) degrees in Business Analytics, Supply Chain Management, Information Systems, Finance, and Accounting. Each program consists of 30 credit hours of approved graduate coursework as prescribed in their respective

curricula. In addition, COB offers a Master of Business Administration (MBA) degree that requires 36-48 credit hours of approved graduate coursework, depending on the student's academic background. Some of the programs may also have pre-requisites that must be satisfied. Students in each of these programs will receive the relevant degree conferred by the Regents of the University of Michigan upon the recommendation of the COB faculty. COB also welcomes students with non-business undergraduate degrees to apply and study for an MBA or MS degree.

b) UM-D's College of Engineering and Computer Science (CECS) offers Master of Science (MS) degrees in Computer Information Science, Engineering Management, Information Systems and Technology, Program and Project Management, and Software Engineering, and Master of Science in Engineering (MSE) degrees in Automotive Systems Engineering, Computer Engineering, Electrical Engineering, Energy Systems Engineering, Industrial and Systems Engineering, Manufacturing System Engineering, and Mechanical Engineering. Each program consists of 30 credit hours of approved graduate coursework as prescribed in their respective curricula. Some of the programs may also have pre-requisites that must be satisfied. Students in each of these programs will receive the relevant degree conferred by the Regents of the University of Michigan upon the recommendation of the Horace H. Rackham School of Graduate Studies and the CECS faculty.

c) UM-D’s College of Arts, Sciences, and Letters (CASL) offers Master of Science (MS) degrees in Applied and Computational Mathematics and in Environmental Science, which require 30 credit hours of approved graduate coursework as prescribed in their respective curricula; in Criminology and Criminal Justice, which requires 30-31 credit hours of approved graduate coursework, depending on whether the student selects the optional thesis; and in Psychology, which requires 39-48 credit hours of approved graduate coursework depending on the student’s field of specialization. In addition, CASL offers a Master of Public Administration (MPA) degree that requires 36 credit hours of approved graduate coursework. Some of the programs may also have pre-requisites that must be satisfied. Students in each of these programs will receive the relevant degree conferred by the Regents of the University of Michigan upon the recommendation of the CASL faculty.

## 3. Admission Criteria

a) All admissions are subject to all applicable UM-D admission policies and standards.

b) English language proficiency is required for the admission of undergraduate international guest students, and may be demonstrated by a score of at least TOEFL 80, IELTS 6.5, or MELAB 76. English language proficiency is also required for admission to graduate programs at UM-D, and may be demonstrated by a score of at least TOEFL 84, IELTS 6.5, or MELAB 80. Note: TOEFL, IELTS, or MELAB scores are not required for admission to most graduate programs at UM-D for all students who had English as their language of instruction for their entire undergraduate program.

c) For admission to a graduate program at UM-D, students must have satisfactorily completed a four-year undergraduate program.

d) Applicants to COB graduate programs are required to take either the GMAT or the GRE and earn a total GMAT score of at least 550 (or equivalent GRE score). However, applicants who satisfy any of the following conditions are not required to submit GMAT or GRE scores:

i. Earned an undergraduate degree from YZU within the previous 5 years with a 3.2 (3.0 for MS applicants) cumulative or post-60 GPA (on a 4-point, US-equivalent scale).

ii. Completed at least 24 graduate hours at YZU within the previous 5 years with a 3.2 (3.0 for MS applicants) graduate GPA (on a 4-point, US-equivalent scale).

iii. Met one of the other GMAT/GRE waiver conditions currently recognized in COB (see “Requirements” at http://umdearborn.edu/cob/graduate-programs/admission).

Notwithstanding any of the above conditions, COB reserves the right to require the GMAT or GRE of any graduate applicant. COB will consider all GMAT and GRE scores submitted to the College, even if the applicant qualifies for a waiver.

e) Applicants to CECS graduate programs are not required to submit GRE/GMAT scores.

f) Applicants to the CASL MS in Psychology are required to take the GRE. However, students who have completed a master’s or other advanced degree from an accredited institution in a field such as psychology, medicine, or health policy, may be eligible to waive the GRE at CASL’s discretion. Graduate degrees that include a thesis or dissertation are preferred.

Applicants to other CASL graduate programs are not required to submit GRE/GMAT scores.

4. The admitted cohort each year will consist of a minimum of 15 YZU students, total over all programs. UM-D and YZU will work out the maximum size of the cohort on a case-by-case basis.

5. For YZU students enrolled in UM-D under this MOU, the following conditions apply:

a) The programs will run each year for twelve (12) consecutive months commencing in September, and consisting of the following semesters: a Fall semester from September through December, a Winter semester from January through April, a Summer I semester in May and June, and a Summer II semester in July and August.

b) Undergraduate guest students are normally expected to complete the required education curriculum for their Bachelor’s degree from YZU within one year (i.e., 12 months).

i. It is the responsibility of each student to ensure that courses taken at UM-D will satisfy the required education curriculum for the Bachelor’s degree at YZU.

ii. It is the responsibility of each student to ensure that they meet the prerequisites for all UM-D classes in which they enroll.

c) Graduate students are normally expected to complete their Master’s degree within one year (i.e., 12 months), but in no more than five consecutive semesters (i.e., 16 months) unless there are compelling situations (e.g., medical emergency) that mandate a sixth semester. The MBA and MS in Psychology may require up to two years (i.e., up to eight semesters).

d) Completed applications (including official transcripts and all official test scores) must be received by March 31 in a given year to facilitate the cohort starting the program that Fall.

e) Students must be physically present on campus at UM-D during the Fall and Winter semesters, and be enrolled in at least 12 credit hours of approved undergraduate coursework or 9 credit hours of approved graduate coursework during each semester, i.e., a total of a minimum of 24 credit hours of undergraduate coursework or 18 credit hours of graduate coursework over the two semesters. The maximum allowed undergraduate credit hours in either semester is 15. The maximum number of graduate credit hours in either semester is 12.

## 6. Scholarship

a) For each YZU student admitted to a program under this agreement with a minimum undergraduate or graduate GPA of 3.0 (based on UM-D's approved grade conversion scale), UM-D will offer a scholarship equal to 80% of the difference between the resident (in-state) and non-resident (out of state) tuition and fees.

b) Students must maintain a minimum GPA of 3.0 (on a 4.0 scale; calculated based only on courses taken at UM-D) while enrolled at UM-D to retain their eligibility for the scholarship.

c) The scholarship is valid for the duration of the program as defined in this agreement.

## 7. Other Expenses

a) Students will be eligible to purchase the health insurance coverage that is available for all full-time students at UM-D. Students must show proof of adequate health insurance coverage during their enrolment at UM-D.

b) Students may sign up for UM-D coordinated housing and student meal plans subject to their availability. There are several off-campus housing options also available.

c) All other costs (e.g., travel, housing, meals, health insurance, transportation, etc.) are the responsibility of the students.

8. This agreement will become effective when both institutions have signed the document, and will be in effect for five years. The agreement may be extended with the mutual consent of the two institutions.

9. This agreement supersedes all preceding agreements between the two institutions.

10. The agreement may be terminated by either institution after giving written notice of 60 days.

For the Regents of the University of Michigan On Behalf of the University of Michigan-Dearborn

On Behalf of Yuan Ze University
