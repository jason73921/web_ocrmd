# Yuan Ze University Regulations Governing Scholarship Awarding for

## Foreign Students

Passed at the 17th Administrative Meeting of 2008 dated June 22, 2009

Regulations Governing Scholarship Awarding for Foreign Students at Yuan Ze University

Article 1: These regulations are formulated to encourage and support outstanding foreign students to study at Yuan Ze University.

Article 2: The term "foreign students" in these regulations referred to in the Regulations refer to degree students as defined in the University’s "Regulations Regarding International Students Undertaking Studies in Taiwan".

Article 3: The content, application conditions, application procedures, and duration of awards for scholarships are as follows:

## 1. Scholarship Content:

(1) Full exemption of tuition, miscellaneous fees, credit fees, computer and internet usage fees, and sports facility maintenance fees.

(2) Fifty percent reduction in tuition, miscellaneous fees, credit fees, computer and internet usage fees, and sports facility maintenance fees.

(3) Twenty percent reduction in tuition, miscellaneous fees, credit fees, computer and internet usage fees, and sports facility maintenance fees.

(4) Stipend.

(5) Exemption from on-campus dormitory fees.

## 2. Application Conditions:

(1) New students:

Upon completion of the individual application stage and registration procedures for the first semester, students are eligible for one or multiple scholarships specified in Article 3(1).

(2) Continuing students:

1. Undergraduate students who have completed one semester of coursework, meet the minimum credit requirements of the university, have a conduct score of 80 or above, and have achieved an academic average score of 65 or above in the previous semester, and:

(a) Ranking in the top 15% of the department or equivalent unit qualifies for the first or fourth objective of Article 3(1).

(b) Ranking in the top 30% of the department or equivalent unit qualifies for the second objective or fourth objective of Article 3(1).

(c) Ranking in the top 50% of the department or equivalent unit qualifies for the third objective or fourth objective of Article 3(1).

2. Master's students who have completed one semester of coursework, taken at least six credits per semester, have a conduct score of 80 or above, an academic average score of 75 or above in the previous semester, and:

(a) Ranking in the top 40% of the department or equivalent unit qualifies for the first or fourth objective of Article 3(1).

(b) Ranking in the top 60% of the department or equivalent unit qualifies for the second objective or fourth objective of Article 3(1).

(c) Ranking in the top 70% of the department or equivalent unit qualifies for the third objective or fourth objective of Article 3(1).

3. Doctoral students who have completed one semester of coursework, taken at least six credits per semester, and have a conduct score of 80 or above qualify for the first or fourth objective of Article 3(1).

4. Students in master's and doctoral programs who have not met the requirement of taking six credits during the thesis writing period may apply with a recommendation letter from their supervisor and a research proposal.

5. Students who do not meet the above conditions may apply through department recommendation.

## (3) Eligibility review for stipend for foreign graduate students:

1. Review after two years for doctoral students who meet the basic requirements of the university.

2. The basic requirement is 2 points for doctoral students, with each college able to raise the review standard according to the nature of the field.

3. Calculation principle:

(a) Important journal papers: SCI, SSCI, AHCI, TSSCI, and THCI, 4 points for each.

(b) Other journal papers: 3 points each.

(c) International conference papers: 2 points each.

(d) Domestic conference papers: 1 point each.

## 3. Application Procedures:

(1) New students: Submit scholarship application forms during the admission application process, which will be reviewed by the department and approved by the Office of Global Affairs.

(2) Continuing students: Submit scholarship application forms to the Office of Global Affairs before the end of each semester, which will be approved based on academic performance ranking and department recommendation by the Office of Global Affairs.

Article 4: The quota and amount of living allowances for foreign student scholarships will be flexibly adjusted according to the budget for each academic year.

Article 5: Students who receive scholarships from other government agencies in Taiwan are not eligible to apply for this scholarship. However, if the amount is lower than this scholarship, they may apply for the objectives specified in Article 3(1) to make up the difference.

Article 6: When scholarship recipients come to Taiwan to study, their scholarship qualifications will be suspended if they study abroad as exchange students or dual (joint) degree students. However, this does not apply to those required to go abroad for internships as stipulated by the university program.

Article 7: Scholarship recipients found to have provided false information in their applications will have their awards revoked, and any received funds must be returned.

Article 8: After enrollment, students must adhere to the relevant regulations of the university. Violations may result in the revocation of their scholarship eligibility upon the recommendation of the department and approval by the Office of Global Affairs.

Article 9: These regulations will be applicable to new students starting from the next semester after approval by the administrative council. Amendments will follow the same procedure.

In the event of discrepancies between the English and Chinese versions, the Chinese version shall prevail.

# Guidelines for Signing Academic Collaboration Agreements between Yuan Ze University and other Academic and Education Institutions

Passed at the 10th Administrative Meeting of 2008 dated Feb. 23, 2009   
Revised and passed at the 16th Administrative Meeting of 2016 dated Mar. 29, 2017   
Revised and passed at the 15th Administrative Meeting of 2019 dated Feb. 26, 2020

Article 1 In order to effectively utilize the limited resources of Yuan Ze University ((hereinafter “the University”), promote academic exchanges with universities and research institutions both domestically and internationally, and enhance the University’s international reputation and academic standards, these operational guidelines are hereby established as the basis for handling such matters.

Article 2 The formulation of cooperative projects should adhere to the following principles, except for cases of special needs-based cooperative relationships:

1. Equality and mutual benefit between both parties.

2. Both parties should have comparable academic status, as well as a cooperative relationship that contributes to both parties' academic developme3. There should be dedicated units or personnel responsible for promoting and executing the cooperative matters.

3. The contents of the cooperative agreement should be specific, clear, and feasible.

Article 3 The signing of cooperative agreements by academic units of our university shall be processed according to the following procedures:

1. Cooperative agreements signed by department (program) units shall be formulated by the organizing unit and the cooperating unit. After approval by the department (program) council and the college council, and endorsement by Academic Affairs, the Office of Research and Development, and the Global Affairs Office, and approval by the president, the cooperative agreement shall be signed by the heads of the equivalent units of both parties.

2. Cooperative agreements signed by colleges (College of General Studies) or research centers shall be formulated by the organizing unit and the cooperating unit. After approval by the college council or its equivalent, and endorsement by the Academic Affairs, the Office of Research and Development, and the Global Affairs Office, and approval by the president, the cooperative agreement shall be signed by the heads of the equivalent units of both parties.

3. For collaborations involving two or more colleges (including College of General Studies) or research centers, agreements shall be signed in the name of the university. The aforementioned agreements shall be formulated by the Global Affairs Office and the cooperating unit, endorsed by the Academic Affairs and the Office of Research and Development, and after approval by the president, shall be signed by the president or the heads of equivalent units of both parties.

4. The content of the agreements shall be written in English. If required by the collaborating school, it may also be written in English and the official language of the collaborating school simultaneously. However, it should be stipulated in the agreement that the English version shall prevail. Agreements with mainland China shall be written in both traditional and simplified Chinese.

5. Agreements with academic institutions in mainland China shall be reported to the Ministry of Education by the Global Affairs Office one month before signing. After review and approval by the Ministry of Education, signing by both parties, and submission to the Ministry of Education for record-keeping, the agreements can be implemented.

Article 4 Six months before the expiration of cooperative agreements of academic units of our university, each unit shall consolidate and evaluate the cooperation performance. The renewal procedure shall follow the provisions of Article 3 of these operational guidelines.

Article 5 Within one week after signing academic cooperation agreements with other academic or research institutions, the executing units of each agreement shall submit copies to the Global Affairs Office for record-keeping. Relevant press releases shall be evaluated and released to the public by the Public Affairs and Alumni Service Center.

Article 6 These operational guidelines shall be implemented upon approval by the

administrative meeting, and the same shall apply to revisions.

In the event of discrepancies between the English and Chinese versions, the Chinese version shall prevail.

# Yuan Ze University Regulations of Study Abroad Scholarship

Approved at the 19th Administrative Meeting of 2002 Academic Year, dated Jul. 14, 2003

Amended at the 5th Administrative Meeting of 2005 Academic Year, dated Nov. 7, 2005

Amended at the 18th Administrative Meeting of 2005 Academic Year, dated May 22, 2006

Amended at the 6th Administrative Meeting of 2009 Academic Year, dated Dec. 7, 2009

Amended at the 9th Administrative Meeting of 2009 Academic Year, dated Mar. 3, 2010

Amended at the 17th Administrative Meeting of 2015 Academic Year, dated Apr. 27, 2016

Article 1 To enhance students' international perspectives and promote mutual benefits through international and cross-strait exchange activities, and to help students grow and increase the visibility of the university on the international stage, this regulation is hereby established to promote mutual benefits.

Article 2 The activities referred to in this regulation are as follows:

1. Taking credit courses abroad or pursuing a dual degree at a foreign university that adheres to the implementation guidelines for YZU students taking courses abroad and submitting an application to the Office of Academic Affairs.

2. International and cross-strait intercollegiate exchange activities, or exchange activities between international and cross-strait universities and institutions, include participation in international workshops and leading a delegation in intercollegiate exchange activities.

Article 3 The scholarship eligibility is as follows:

1. Students who hold the nationality of the Republic of China (Taiwan).

2. Outstanding students recommended by various colleges and administrative units.

Article 4 The funding subsidy principles are as follows:

1. Scholarships shall be prioritized in the following order: dual-degree programs or credit courses at foreign universities, international and cross-strait intercollegiate exchange activities.

2. Pursuing a dual degree or taking formal credit courses should be limited to those with formal contracts or approved by the university's project; international and cross-strait exchange activities should have relevant supporting documents.

3. The principle is that students receive one subsidy per academic year during their period of study.

4. This scholarship is not available to applicants who receive full subsidies from other universities/institutions/agencies regarding the same case. Subsidies will be recovered in full if found.

5. The number of scholarship recipients and the amount of scholarship may be adjusted flexibly according to the budget for the academic year.

Article 5 The application procedure shall include the following materials submitted to the responsible unit:

1. Application form.

2. Specific content of international and cross-strait exchange activities, including activity name, content,

time, location, and expected outcomes.

3. Other supporting information.

Article 6 Scholarship applications shall be reviewed by the responsible unit.

Article 7 If the approved international and cross-strait exchange activities are changed, delayed or canceled due to unforeseen circumstances, students shall inform the responsible unit in advance. If there are any disputes, they will be handled by the unit in charge.

Article 8 This regulation shall be implemented after being passed by the administrative meeting, and shall be amended accordingly.

# Regulations Governing Implementation of Scholarships for Mainland Chinese Students Pursuing Master's and Doctoral Degrees at Yuan Ze University

Passed at the 14th Administrative Meeting of 2011 dated May. 14, 2012   
Revised and passed at the 17th Administrative Meeting of 2015 dated Apr. 27, 2015   
Revised and passed at the 16th Administrative Meeting of 2016 dated Mar. 29, 2017

Article 1: The Regulations Governing Implementation of Scholarships for Mainland Chinese Students Pursuing Master's and Doctoral Degrees at Yuan Ze University (hereinafter referred to as the Regulations) are established to encourage excellent Mainland Chinese students to study at the University, facilitate an internationalized academic environment, and assist students in learning and studying diligently.

Article 2: Eligibility:

1. Applicants must adhere to the "Regulations for Mainland Chinese Students Studying in Taiwan for Higher Education."

2. Students must have completed at least one semester of coursework with a minimum of six credits per semester, achieving a GPA of at least 75 in the previous semester, and be recommended by their department. For Master's and Doctoral students who have not met the six-credit requirement while writing their thesis, they may apply with a recommendation from their advisor.

3. Students must maintain good conduct throughout their academic program, with no record of admonishment or higher disciplinary actions.

4. Students should not have applied for any other internal scholarships during the current semester.

Article 3: Scholarship Content and Duration:

1. Scholarship content includes tuition and miscellaneous fee waivers, as well as a stipend.

2. The maximum duration for Master's students is four semesters, and eight semesters for Doctoral students. Stipend is provided from the actual month of enrollment until the end of the scholarship period, which must be continuous without interruption.

Article 4: Application Procedure and Review:

Recommendations from departments (or equivalent academic units) are submitted before the end of each semester, reviewed and approved by the Global Affairs Office to be eligible for the scholarships outlined in Article 3, Section 1.

Article 5: Suspension and Cancellation of Scholarships:

1. Scholarships are discontinued upon graduation, suspension, withdrawal, transfer, or cancellation of the scholarship.

2. If a student is found to have forged or provided false documents, their scholarship eligibility is revoked, and any funds received must be repaid.

3. Scholarships are suspended if the student is not in the Republic of China (R.O.C) for more than two months during a semester.

Article 6: The allocation of scholarship slots and the amount of stipend for Mainland Chinese students are flexibly adjusted according to the annual budget.

Article 7: After enrollment, Mainland Chinese students are required to adhere to the relevant regulations of the university. Violations may lead to the withdrawal of scholarship privileges after approval by the Global Affairs Office.

Article 8: These regulations are effective upon announcement after approval by the administrative council. Amendments follow the same procedure.

In the event of discrepancies between the English and Chinese versions, the Chinese version shall prevail.

## Article 1 Purpose

The Regulations of Scholarships for International Students (hereinafter referred to as the Regulations) are established to encourage excellent international students to study at the University, facilitate an internationalized academic environment, and assist students in learning and studying diligently.

## Article 2 Subjects Eligible to Apply

The international students referred to in the Regulations refer to degree students as defined in the University’s "Regulations Regarding International Students Undertaking Studies in Taiwan".

## Article 3 Qualifications and Rewards

## I. Scholarship

1) 100% reimbursement of tuition, miscellaneous fees, credit fees, computer and internet fees, and sport facility fees.

2) 50% reimbursement of tuition, miscellaneous fees, credit fees, computer and internet fees, and sport facility fees.

3) 20% reimbursement of tuition, miscellaneous fees, credit fees, computer and internet fees, and sport facility fees.

## 4) Stipend.

## II. Application Requirements

1) Incoming new students: Individual applicants who have completed the registration process in the first semester may be granted scholarships as set out in one of the first three Items, or including Item 4 of Paragraph 1 of this Article.

1. Undergraduate students who have completed one semester of study and have taken the minimum number of credits per semester required by the University, with a conduct score of 80 or higher and an average academic grade of 65 or higher in the previous semester, and:

(1) who ranks in the top 15 percent of the department or unit of the same level may be awarded scholarships as described in Item 1, or including Item 4, Subparagraph 1 of this Article.

(2) who ranks in the top 30 percent of the department or unit of the same level may be awarded scholarships as described in Item 2, or including Item 4, Subparagraph 1 of this Article.

(3) who ranks in the top 50 percent of the department or unit of the same level may be awarded scholarships as described in Item 3, or including Item 4, Subparagraph 1 of this Article.

2. Students in a Master's program who have completed one semester of study, have taken at least six credits in each semester, have a conduct grade of 80 or higher, had an average academic grade of 75 or higher in the previous semester, and:

(1) who ranks in the top 40 percent of the department or unit of the same level may be awarded scholarships as described in Item 1, or including Item 4 of Paragraph 1 of this Article;

(2) who ranks in the top 20 percent of the department or unit of the same level may be awarded scholarships as described in Item 2, or including Item 4, Subparagraph 1 of this Article.

2. Students in a Master's program who have completed one semester of study, have taken at least 6 credits in each semester, have a conduct grade of 80 or higher, had an average academic grade of 80 or higher in the previous semester, and:

(1) who ranks in the top 25 percent of the department or unit of the same level may be awarded scholarships as described in Item 1, or including Item 4 of Paragraph 1 of this Article;

(2) who ranks in the top 50 percent of the department or unit of the same level may be awarded scholarships as described in Item 2, or including Item 4 of Paragraph 1 of this Article;

3. Students in a PhD program who have completed one semester of study, have taken at least 6 credits in each semester, have a conduct grade of 80 or higher, had an average academic grade of 80 or higher may be awarded scholarships as described in Item 1, or including Item 4 of Paragraph 1 of this Article.

5. Those who do not meet the above criteria may apply on the recommendation of the department.
