## 後 COVID-19 時代：社會科學角度的省思與啟示

鑒於新型冠狀病毒（COVID-19）對人類的建康和福祉有著前所未有衝擊與影響，身為社會科學研究人員或高等教育的教師，我們將針對新型冠狀病毒（COVID-19）所引發的社會議題進行探索，例如人際間的溝通交流、遠距教育、以及語言教育等。藉由科學性研究來瞭解此病毒對我們人類社會產生的影響、改變或啟示，期盼在後 COVID-19 時代，社會科學角度的省思與啟示，能累積、傳遞相關研究領域知識、經驗以及創見，增進未來人類社會福祉。鑑於此，本期特刊將就以下主題進行論文摘要徵稿：

一、COVID-19 對電腦輔助語言學習（CALL）或科技增進語言學習（TELL）等理論/實踐的衝擊與啟示

二、COVID-19 對外語教育理論/實踐的衝擊與啟示

三、COVID-19 對跨文化溝通教學或研究理論/實踐的衝擊與啟示

四、COVID-19 對全球或區域政治、經濟、文化的衝擊與啟示

# Journal of Languages and International Studies Special Issue (December, 2020)

## Call for Abstracts

## Post COVID-19: Social Scientific Reflections and Insights

Objective: As we continue to experience the unprecedented scale and impact of coronavirus (COVID-19) pandemic on human beings’ health and wellbeing, we, as social science researchers and/or teachers in higher education, would like shoulder the responsibility and push the boundary of knowledge towards the impacts of COVID-19 on our social lives, for example in the fields of human communication, distance learning/remote teaching and language education. Towards this end, this Special Issue would like to invite research-based manuscripts or theory-based commentary papers that address the following issues:

1. Implications arising from COVID-19 pandemic for Computer-Assisted Language Learning (CALL) or Technology-Enhanced Language Learning (TELL) theory and/or practice

2. Implications arising from COVID-19 pandemic for Intercultural Education theory and/or practice

3. Implications arising from COVID-19 pandemic for teaching and researching Intercultural Communicative Competence and foreign language education theory and/or practice in general

4. Implications arising from COVID-19 pandemic for global political, economic and cultural issues

## Publication Timeline

Abstracts submission date: by May 31st, 2020

Please submit a 300-500 word abstract. Once the abstract is accepted, the final decision on publication will be dependent on the quality of the manuscript itself, as decided by reviewers and editors. Abstracts should include the following information:

1. a general topic under study and the background information for your research

2. the statement of the problem your research addresses

3. research and/or analytical methods

4. possible findings, results, or arguments

5. significance or implications of your research
