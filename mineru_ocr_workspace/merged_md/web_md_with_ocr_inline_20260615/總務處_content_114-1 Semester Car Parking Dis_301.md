## 一、文章基本資料

- **文章ID**: 301
- **文章標題**: 114-1 Semester Car Parking Discount Pass Application Schedule
- **文章網址**: https://www.yzu.edu.tw/admin/ga/index.php/tw/114-1-semester-car-parking-discount-pass-application-schedule
- **文章簡介**: Those intending to apply for the Car Parking Discount Pass for the 114th academic year, first semester (08/01/114–01/31/115), are kindly requested to complete the unified processing within the designated period below. Applicants should bring the completed application form along with copies of all relevant documents and submit them at the Information Center (One-stop Service) located on the 1st floor of Building 5.



Application Procedures:

(1) Fill out the application information in theApplication for Parking Discount Pass, print the application form, sign it, and attach copies of your driver’s license and vehicle registration.

(2) Submit the documents at the Information Center (temporary counter) on the 1st floor of Building 5. The receiving unit will check and register the information.

(3) Applicants must complete the payment.

(4) The processing unit will complete the vehicle identification permit registration, which will take effect 3 days after registration.

On-site payment schedule at the Information Center:



Individuals who are unable to pay during the stated times are requested to contact the Campus Security Guard Captain during regular office hours (Monday to Friday) to arrange an alternative processing time. (Contact: 03-4638800 ext. 2261)

Notes:


The Parking Discount Pass covers the usage period from 08/01/114 to 01/31/115 and will automatically expire after the deadline.

Each student may register one car for a parking permit only. Please prepare the required documents and cash payment: NT$4,400 for regular students; NT$3,000 for part-time master’s programs and extended-study students. You may authorize someone else to pay and submit your application on your behalf.

For related vehicle regulations and fee standards, please refer to the “The Campus Vehicle Management Regulations on Yuan Ze University Headquarters” and “The Parking Fee Standards for Vehicles on the Yuan Ze University Headquarters”.


Office of General Affairs respectfully submits

# The Campus Vehicle Management Regulations on Yuan Ze University Headquarters

March 13, 2024, Approved by the 2nd General Affairs Meeting of the 2023 Academic Year

October 11, 2024, Amended by the 1st General Affairs Meeting of the 2024 Academic Year

March 4, 2026, Amended by the 1st General Affairs Meeting of the 2025 Academic Year

Article 1. To maintain traffic safety, campus tranquility, environmental aesthetics, and parking order within the main campus, these regulations are established. The term "vehicles" as used herein includes cars, motorcycles, bicycles, and electric scooters.

## Article

2. Cars entering the campus are identified by license plate recognition and charged based on hourly rates, with fee standards established separately. Individuals eligible for preferential access, including full-time faculty and staff and on-campus business operators, may apply for up to two cars; others are limited to one car. Vehicles with preferential access may park in the A-Zone parking lot located to the left of the campus entrance (hereinafter referred to as the A-Zone parking lot) without additional hourly charges. General student cars are restricted to the A-Zone parking lot; entry into the main campus is subject to the hourly parking rate.

Article 3. Motorcycles of full-time faculty, staff, on-campus business operators, and students with public hospital-issued disability certifications may enter the campus upon approval. Delivery motorcycles are permitted entry upon obtaining a temporary pass. Other motorcycles are prohibited from entering the campus. Motorcycle identification stickers must be affixed prominently to the front of the motorcycle and must not be loaned or transferred. Delivery motorcycles must adhere to campus-designated delivery times, routes, and locations and are limited to a 30- minute stay within the campus.

Article 4. Official vehicles such as postal vans, telecommunications repair vehicles, fire trucks, ambulances, police cars, garbage trucks, cleaning vehicles, Taiwan Power Company service vehicles, courier trucks, and engineering vehicles are exempt from parking fees after verification by gate security.

Article 5. Vehicles entering the campus must comply with “Regulations Governing Road Traffic Safety” and must not violate any provisions outlined in the “Road Traffic Management and Penalty Act”. Vehicles must be parked in designated parking lots or spaces (racks). Vehicles classified as special under the preceding article may park at work sites during their operation period, provided they do not obstruct traffic, and are not restricted to designated parking spaces or locations. Vehicles parked in designated spaces (e.g., accessible parking or other reserved spots) must display relevant permits or proof in a clearly visible location.

Article 6. During campus-hosted events, vehicles may be allowed free access or charged per entry for the day, subject to specified time limits.

Article 7. Vehicles must observe the campus speed limits: 30 km/h within the main campus and 20 km/h in dormitory areas. Bicycles are prohibited from downhill speeding or riding against traffic on campus downhill sections. Electric scooters are restricted to red brick paths and campus loop roads, riding on the right side of the road. Riders must comply with the regulations for the use of electric scooters, reducing riding speed and yielding to pedestrians. It is strictly prohibited to carry multiple passengers, perform stunts, ride against traffic, or engage in reckless riding. Underground parking in YZU Buildings 6 and 7 is reserved for faculty and staff vehicles, while delivery vehicles may park temporarily for unloading. Dormitory 3 underground parking is for faculty, staff, and on-campus business operators only. Parking lots are closed during extended holidays or typhoons and will not accommodate temporary access during announced closure periods. Car washing is prohibited on campus.

Article 8. Electric-assisted vehicles with government-issued license plates shall be handled in accordance with the relevant regulations for automobiles and motorcycles. If the appearance is similar to that of an ordinary bicycle (with pedals and chains that can be powered by human force), they shall be managed in accordance with the relevant regulations for bicycles. If the appearance is similar to that of an ordinary motorcycle (without pedals and chains that can be powered by human force), they shall be managed in accordance with the relevant regulations for motorcycles.

Article 9. Violating vehicles may be clamped, towed, or ticketed based on photographic or video evidence, with penalties as follows: Cars: NT\$500, Motorcycles: NT\$300, Bicycles: NT\$100. Vehicles with two violations will be blacklisted and denied campus access. Accidents will be referred to the police for handling.

Article 10. Vehicles within the campus that have not been moved for an extended period will be considered abandoned vehicles if, after notifying the owner or issuing a public notice, they remain unmoved during the specified period.

Article 11. These regulations shall take effect upon approval by the General Affairs Meeting and subsequent promulgation. Amendments shall follow the same procedure.

In case of any discrepancy between the Chinese and English versions, the Chinese version shall prevail.

September 25, 2024, Amended by the 3rd Executive Council Meeting of the 2024 Academic Year

Article 1. This standard is formulated in accordance with the “YZU Headquarters Regulations for Campus Vehicle Management”.

Article 2. General hourly parking fee rates for vehicles in the main campus area:

1. Vehicles leaving the campus within half an hour of entering will not be charged. However, if a vehicle stays beyond half an hour, the first half-hour will also be charged. After payment, the vehicle must leave the campus within 30 minutes. The maximum daily fee is NT\$240 (calculated from midnight each day).

2. Monday to Friday (excluding holidays) from 08:00 to 18:00: NT\$15 per half hour. If less than 30 minutes, the fee will be calculated as half an hour.

3. Other time periods (including holidays): NT\$10 per half hour. If less than 30 minutes, the fee will be calculated as half an hour.

4. On days when the university announces a charge based on the number of entries, vehicles leaving within half an hour of entering are free. If exceeding half an hour, a fee of NT\$50 will be charged for each entry on that day.

Article 4. Individuals who do not belong to the aforementioned discount categories and park in A-Zone parking lot should pay the general hourly parking fee. Those who have signed inter-school reciprocal agreements with the university should follow the terms of the agreement.

Alumni, upon approval, and retired faculty members of the university may enjoy free parking within 4 hours per entry into the main campus area. If exceeding 4 hours, additional charges will apply according to the general hourly parking rates, or the vehicle may be parked in A-Zone parking lot without additional time charges. Donors will receive discounts according to the university’s acknowledgment regulations. The Public Affairs and Alumni Service Center, along with the Personnel Office, will handle the registration and application for such privileges.

Retired faculty members who use their office during the courtesy period (within six months after retirement) are entitled to parking according to the full-time faculty/staff discount.

Article 5. If there is any dispute after payment for a discount pass, a refund application may be made within 14 days of payment. No refund will be granted after this period. For refund requests due to force majeure, proof and the original payment invoice should be submitted. The refund will be calculated based on the actual usage period, and the remaining amount will be refunded.

Article 6. If any department invites guests or speakers and provides parking discounts, the inviting department should pay on behalf of the guests or pre-purchase parking vouchers from the security team before the event. Departments hosting campus-wide official events may apply to the security team for guest vehicle registration three days in advance. If approved, the vehicle may

park for free.

On the day of major school events (such as the school anniversary, graduation ceremony, and large-scale events approved by the university), vehicles may park on campus for free.

When the colleges and departments organize academic seminars or independently host graduation ceremonies, alumni gatherings, or parent days, parking vouchers may be purchased in advance for groups of 30 or more vehicles, with a rate of NT\$50 for a half-day (5 hours) and NT\$100 for a full day (10 hours). Post-event settlement will follow regulations, and the voucher is valid only on the event day.

Article 7. All fees collected under the above categories will be included in the university's revenue and specifically used for the improvement of campus traffic and parking management measures.

Article 8. This parking fee standard shall take effect upon approval by the Executive Council Meeting and subsequent promulgation. Amendments shall follow the same procedure.
