## Mic-utumn festial uue

The Mid-Autumn Festival (中秋節zhōng qiū jié) is one of Taiwan's most important traditional holidays. It is celebrated on the 15th day of the eighth lunar month. On this day, families gather to enjoy the full moon and eat mooncakes (月餅yuè bǐng), symbolizing reunion(團圓tuán yuán) and happiness.

## Theriiniv

![](images/774892a363c6afed6dcd7ed733464f83178449050841fdcbb89149925c5aa831.jpg)

Long ago, ten suns (太陽tài yang) appeared in the sky at the same time. The land dried up, and people suffered. A hero named Hou Yi shot down nine suns and saved the world. People made him king, but he became cruel and desire to become immortal

His wife, Chang'e, didn't want him to hurt people. She secretly took the Elixir of Life and floated up to the moon. She stayed in the Moon Palace and became a goddess. People remembered her kindness and started to worship (祭拜jì bài /拜拜bài bài) the moon on the day she flew away—this became the Mid-Autumn Festival.

## Mid-Autumn Traditions in Taiwan

![](images/01f490ece6145c8a84991e09a809895ecc7b2b1387051fa24fcfaa68e4db1f8d.jpg)

•Admire the Moon and mooncakes: Families sit outside or on balconies to enjoy the moon and eat mooncakes. Popular varieties include egg yolk pastry (蛋黄酥dàn huáng sū) and snow skin mooncakes.

•Family barbecues (烤肉kǎo ròu) : Since the 1980s, barbecuing has become a popular activity. Families grill food, chat, and set off fireworks (煙火yān huǒ).

• Lanterns and parties: In some rural areas, people still carry lanterns (燈籠dēng long) and hold evening parties.

• Eating pomelo (柚子yòu zi): Their large, round shape symbolizes reunion. In Chinese, the word pomelo' pronounced as "you zi,"sounds like several auspicious phrases: “wandering child,” expressing longing for family; “blessed child,”symbolizing protection and peace; and “having children,” wishing for fertility and prosperity.
