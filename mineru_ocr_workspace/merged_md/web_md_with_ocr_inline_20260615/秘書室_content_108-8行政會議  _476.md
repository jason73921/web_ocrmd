## 人工智慧跨域創新應用中心規劃書

## 宗旨及具體目標

隨著物聯網帶來海量數據的蒐集、晶片技術成熟及演算法持續優化等因素，人工智慧(ArtificialIntelligence；AI）相關應用備受各界關注，目前政府亦積極推動AI發展，包括科技部將建構雲端服務及大數據運算平台、創新研究中心兩項策略各編列新台幣 50億元，以完善基礎環境，並在發展策略上採用軟硬整合與智慧科技驅動創新應用並重的新思維，將硬體製造優勢與雲端大數據技術融合，積極研發人工智慧技術，並在社會生活、醫療照護、商業金融、綠能環保、農業生技、智慧製造、數位政府智慧城市等領域結合雲端物聯網大數據分析、深度學習等核心技術，創造創新智慧應用，提升國際競爭力。

為配合國家高科技人工智慧發展政策，強化本校於人工智慧科學技術之研發究發展，以及遠東集團關係企業之交流及產學合作，滿足國內外遠東關係企業朝向AI 轉型之需求，擬設立「人工智慧跨域創新應用中心」（以下簡稱「AI中心」），期能整合校內教師在AI領域之研發能量，培育適合人工智慧相關產業之各類人才拓展學校未來在人工智慧技術與產業發展業務及轉型契機，初步規畫研究項目如圖1所示。

## 組織、運作及管理方式

AI中心設置主任一人，綜理全盤業務；另設執行長(或副主任)2至3人、及秘書，配合並協助主任處理各項業務，其中主任、執行長(或副主任)皆為老師兼任並為無給職或由AI中心自籌所需人事費。

AI中心現階段組織架構如下圖2所示，各層級之任務說明如下：

(一)審議委員會：審議委員會由AI中心主任擔任召集人及主持會議。審議委員會置7至11位委員，其中校內至少佔2/5以上為原則，研發長及會計室主任為當然委員。審議委員由主任推薦，經研發長及校長同意後聘任之，其職掌包括：

1.審議AI中心組織章程及法規。

2.訂定AI中心之定位以及發展方針、策略和目標。

3.審議AI中心之營運計畫及預算、結餘之分配。

4.協助AI中心相關業務。

（二）研究團隊：各領域教授、研究人員和學生為主要研發人力，負責對短、中、長程研發計畫之構想形成、申請作業和計畫執行。並保持與國內、外產官學研各界之交流與合作，如人員之互訪、主辦或參與各種學術性或產學合作之研討會。在經費許可下，將積極與國際知名之研發機構建立合作關係。

(三)計算力平台：支援全校師生與遠東集團人員進行 AI運算事務，協助推廣人工智慧相關課程與計畫衍生成果。

（四）行政支援：維持中AI心正常運作與發展，負責項目包含：AI中心內部管理、計畫形成與進度掌控等工作。

## 三、 具體推動工作或業務內容

AI中心主要任務包括：

(一)建立跨領域技術團隊合作，促進跨領域創新。

(二)規劃與打造人工智慧創新環境機制與平台。

(三)逐步與遠東集團建立緊密合作。

（四)規劃與執行有關人工智慧之學術交流、研究發展與產學事宜。

(五)規劃與執行有關人工智慧之推廣服務相關事宜。

(六)進行政府與民間人才培訓計畫。

## 四 預期成效

希望透過AI中心來促進專案團隊間的合作與成果分享，一起在人工智慧應用的領域達成更好的目標，並產生國際級的重要影響。同時在AI中心的引導下，產生許多可能的合作題材，持續追蹤協調，以期讓討論的題材皆能落實到實際研究計劃中。將透過打造之平台讓各種人工智慧在跨領域應用能快速的生成及驗證，引領元智校內研究團隊，共同以這新世代的革命性人工智慧科技帶動。

AI中心一方面致力發展核心技術，將透過整合學校、研究機構和企業等多方研究資源，以跨領域以及跨單位的合作方式，培養關鍵技術的科技領袖人才，同時打造人工智慧創新生態圈，並鼓勵學研機構將研發成果導入公司，解決產業界需求，帶動產業再造，強化與遠東集團之連結。

(四)其他重要成果：AI 中心或研究團隊人員獲校內外學術榮譽，於國內外學術機構或組織擔任重要職務，與校內系所合作概況及跨領域合作成果等。

Yuan Ze University, represented by its President, Prof. Jyh-Yang Wu

Schmalkalden University of Applied Sciences, represented by its Rector, Prof. Dr. Elmar Heinemann.

## Article 1 Purpose

The Yuan Ze University is a private university in Taoyuan City; currently it has five colleges including the Colleges of Engineering, Informatics, Management, Humanities and Social Sciences, and Electrical and Communications Engineering. The Schmalkalden University of Applied Sciences is a public university in the Province Thuringia; it offers undergraduate and graduate education to 3.000 students focussing on engineering and economics.

The present agreement serves the purpose of promoting the contact and the exchange between the students and the academic staff between both institutions. It aims at enhancing mobility in undergraduate programmes. Both institutions will endeavour to implement this cooperation in their teaching and research work.

## Article 2 Organisation

a. The exchange of students can be executed on the basis of this agreement for one or two-semester full time studies. Exchange students do not seek a degree at the host institution. Both institutions endeavour to offer affordable accommodation as well as individual academic assistance for their exchange students; a cultural and a buddy programme help exchange students to phase in. Students participating in an exchange programme enjoy the same rights and obligations as the students of the host institution. Exchange students do not pay tuition fees to the host institution. Each institution agrees to send academic records completed by the participating students to their sending institution. The minimum language requirement is B2 in English. Students are expected to enrol in courses worth approximately 30 ECTS per semester. Whether academic achievements in the host country are recognised by the sending institution must be determined beforehand. Any further details concerning the student exchange programme can be agreed upon between both institutions.

b. The exchange of members of the academic staff should be conducted as visits of approximately one week during which the visitors engage in teaching activities and discover new teaching and research methods in their respective subject area. The guest lecturers are hosted by the inviting institution. Any expenses are carried by the sending institution.

c. Both institutions are committed to providing the necessary funds in their budgets within the legal framework of the respective country and the possibilities of the respective university, in order to implement this agreement successfully. Both parties are specifically encouraged to collect national and international funding to finance exchange projects.

## Article 3 Administration

Each institution provides one contact person at the university’s administration who is responsible for the selection, assignment, and orientation of students as well as further common administrative tasks concerning the programme. At the moment, the person in charge for Yuan Ze University is Prof. Cheng-Chung Wu and the person in charge for Schmalkalden University of Applied Sciences is Dr. Marcus Hornung.

## Article 4 Validity

The present agreement shall remain valid for five years from the date of its issue. Prior to this, it can be terminated only at the end of an academic year in writing within a period of six months beforehand. All exchange staff and students enrolled in the programme at the time of termination shall be permitted to finish their exchange schedule of work or study as specified.

This agreement is signed by the presidents of both institutions and is issued as an original copy to each university. It comes into force only after approval by the relevant organs of both institutions. Nothing in this agreement shall be construed as creating a relationship that permits lawsuits between the parties.

# AGREEMENT REGARDING STUDENT EXCHANGE PROGRAMS BETWEEN YUAN ZE UNIVERSITY AND UNIVERSIDAD DE MONTERREY

This Agreement is made as of November 22nd, 2019 by and between Yuan Ze University, Taoyuan, Taiwan, R.O.C., (hereinafter “YZU”) and Universidad de Monterrey (hereinafter “UDEM”) pursuant to the Cooperation Agreement executed between the two universities.

The purpose of this Agreement is to establish the terms and conditions under which the exchange of students between YZU and UDEM shall take place.

Both parties hereby agree that:

## 1. Program Management

The student exchange program shall be available to students of both universities and shall be administered by YZU and UDEM respectively via the Global Affairs Office of YZU and International Programs Office of UDEM.

## 2. Exchange Student Numbers and Balance

Both universities may send qualified full time undergraduate and/or graduate students to exchange. Up to 4 semester-persons are accepted annually. Special permission is given by mutual agreement between the two institutions.

## 3. Exchange Period

The normal period of exchange for each exchange student shall be one (1) or two (2) consecutive semesters with the consent of both universities.

## 4. Admission Requirements

Exchange candidates must have completed at least one (1) academic year of study at the home university prior to the exchange period, with an overall GPA of 2.8 or higher out of a 4.0 grade scale. Each party will notify the other as quickly as possible if any changes are made to student exchange admission requirements. While it is understood that exchange candidates nominated by the home university will normally be accepted by the host university, the host university reserves the right to review the applications of nominees and make final decisions concerning their admission.

## 5. Language Requirements

Nominees must obtain a TOEFL score of 500 or its equivalent, and present proof of the score when applying. Each party will notify the other as quickly as possible if any changes are made to language requirements.

## 6. Application Documents

The home university will forward the appropriate application documents to the host university, and the host university will inform the home university of its final admission decision within thirty (30) days after the application deadline.

## 7. Visa

The host university will issue appropriate documents to accepted students for visa purposes in accordance with their respective applicable laws and regulations. It is the responsibility of the individual exchange student to obtain a visa in a timely manner.

## 8. Courses

Exchange students will be able to take courses regularly offered at the host university and will have all the rights and privileges enjoyed by other students at that university.

## 9. Credit Transfer

Any academic credit received during the course of the program may be transferred to the home university in accordance with the appropriate regulations of the home university. The host university will inform the home university of exchange students’ grade information in a timely manner upon the request of the exchange student or the home university.

## 10. Tuition and Fees

Exchange students will pay normal tuition and fees to their home university in accordance with the policies of that university. The host university will not charge the home university or the exchange students for application, admission fees and tuition.

## 11. Miscellaneous Fees

Exchange students will be responsible for making their international travel arrangements and shall bear all associated costs, including but not limited to room, board, study materials, local transportation, student health insurance and medical care, personal expenses, and passport/visa application fees. Regarding payment of these expenses, the home university, together with the host university, shall assume responsibility for providing guidance and dealing with situations that may arise so as not to cause undue inconvenience to third parties when exchange students fall into arrears.

## 12. Liability

During their time at the host university, exchange students will be individually liable for any and all incidents, accidents and damage to third parties resulting from the students’ actions where these actions occur in situations beyond the control of the host university and home university. Both parties will not hold each other liable for such incidents.

## 13. Insurance

It is mandatory for exchange students from both institutes to purchase overseas travel and health insurance before departure.

## 14. Accommodation

The host university will make every effort to find suitable accommodation for exchange students. The cost of the accommodation shall be borne by the individual exchange student.

## 15. Student Support

The host university will make every effort to facilitate integration of exchange students into the student life of the host university. The host university will strive to protect the privacy of the student as well as their personal safety in addition to offering orientation sessions and a sufficient support network for everyday life.

## 16. Emergency Procedures

Both the host university and the home university shall provide the other with emergency contact information and will be responsible for carrying out emergency notification in the event of student illness, injury, or other emergencies.

## 17. Appropriate Use of Personal Information

When obtaining personal information from the exchange student, it is necessary for both the host university and the home university to inform the student ahead of time of the

purposes for its use and to keep its use to a minimum. Personal information must be managed carefully to prevent its spread to a third party or use for purposes unauthorized by the exchange student.

## 18. Rules and Regulations

Exchange students shall be subject to the rules and regulations of the host university. Each university reserves the right to dismiss any exchange student at any time for conduct in violation of their established rules and regulations. However, the dismissal of an exchange student shall not adversely affect the agreement and the arrangements regarding other exchange students.

## 19. Length of Stay

Upon completion of the program at the host university, exchange students are required to return to the home university. No extension of stay will be authorized unless otherwise specifically agreed upon by both universities.

## 20. Negotiations

Both parties will strive to resolve any misunderstandings out of or in relation to this agreement that arise in a sincere and respectful manner.

## 21. Agreement Period

This Agreement shall remain in effect from the date of execution for an initial period of five (5) years. Unless either party notifies the other in writing to terminate the Agreement at least six (6) months prior to the date of expiration, the Agreement will be automatically renewed and extended for another five (5) years from the date of expiration. In the case of termination, both universities shall make necessary arrangements to ensure the appropriate completion of ongoing exchanges. This Agreement may be amended by mutual written consent the two of universities through consultation.

## 22. Equity and Non - Discrimation

All participants will be treated in the same non-discriminatory manner in carrying out the provisions of this agreement. Neither party will discriminate on the basis of race, national or ethnic origin, colour, age, gender, sexual orientation, marital status or disability.

## 23. Trademarks and logos

The parties agree not to use the name, logos, emblems and trademarks or any other distinctive sign of the counterpart without their prior written consent. They may only use any distinctive sign of their counterpart in order to identify themselves as collaborators, provided: (i) they are carried out within the framework of this Agreement, and (ii) they do not compromise or adversely affect the name, reputation, image or in general in each part. Otherwise, the affected party may initiate the corresponding legal actions

## 24. Signatures

This agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties. No amendments consent or waiver of terms of this agreement shall bind either party unless in writing and signed by both parties.

## 25. Settlement of disputes

The institutions agree that for the interpretation, execution and compliance of this agreement, as well as for all matters not foreseen in the document, they will take every action to reach an amicable solution and solve any difference that may occur. If an amicable resolution to the dispute is not reached, a mediation committee composed of at least one representative of each partner university will be set up and will attempt to resolve the dispute. Recourse to a court of justice will be made only as a last resort, after having exhausted all other means. In such an event, the dispute will be brought before the judicial authorities of the defendant party.
