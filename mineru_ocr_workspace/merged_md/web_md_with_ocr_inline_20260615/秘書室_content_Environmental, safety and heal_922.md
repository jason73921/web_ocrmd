# Measures for the Establishment of the Environmental Protection and Occupational Safety and Health Committee of Yuan Ze University

2015.12.02 - Adopted at the 9th Executive Council of the 104th academic year   
2023.12.25 - Approved by the 1st meeting of the Committee on the Environment of the 112th school year   
2024.04.24 - Adopted by the 17th Executive Council of the 112th academic year   
2025.03.28 - Approved by the 3rd meeting of the Committee on the Environment of the 113th school year   
2025.04.30 - Adopted by the 15th Executive Council of the 113th academic year

Article 1 To maintain campus environmental quality and ensure the safety of faculty, staff, and students, Yuan Ze University has established the "Yuan Ze University Environmental Protection and Occupational Safety and Health Committee" (hereinafter referred to as the "Committee") in accordance with relevant environmental protection and occupational safety and health laws and regulations.

Article 2 The Committee serves as the leading advisory body for environmental protection and occupational safety and health management at the university. It is composed of several members, including:

1. Chairman: By the President or a designated agent.

2. Ex-officio members:

(1) Heads of Departments: Director of the Environmental Protection and Safety and Health Center. Director of the General Affairs Department. Director of the Personnel Office. Dean of the College of Engineering. Dean of the College of Electrical Engineering and Communication. Director of the Institute of Biotechnology. Director of the Department of Chemical Materials.

(2) Occupational Safety and Health Personnel: One representative from the campus security staff. One representative from the engineering group of the General Affairs Office.

(3) Occupational Safety and Health Professionals: Occupational safety and health affair managers. Occupational safety (and health) management specialist. Occupational safety and health management personnel. Medical personnel engaged in labor health services.

## 3. Elected members:

(1) Faculty Representatives: One person is selected from the current University Council faculty representatives from the College of Engineering

and the College of Electrical Engineering and Communication, respectively.

(2) Technical and Clerical Staff Representative: The first-ranked person from the current University Council staff representatives.

(3) Full-time Staff Representatives: The first-ranked person from the current Labor-Management Conference full-time staff representatives.

(4) University-Appointed Contract Staff Representative: The first-ranked person from the current Labor-Management Conference university-appointed contract staff representatives.

(5) Department-Funded Staff Representative: The first-ranked person from the current Labor-Management Conference unit-funded staff representatives.

(6) Project-Hired Research Assistant Representative: The first-ranked person from the current Labor-Management Conference project-hired full-time assistant representatives.

Members elected by the Committee shall serve a term of one year and shall be eligible for re-election.

Article 3 The Committee shall meet at least once every three months to deliberate, coordinate, and recommend the following matters, keeping records for a period of three years:

1. Continuous improvement and audit of environmental protection, safety, and health management systems.

2. Evaluation and regular review and revision of the promotion plan for environmental protection and safety and health.

3. Implementation of education and training plans on environmental protection and safety and health.

4. Preventive measures against hazards from machinery, equipment, raw materials, and materials.

5. Countermeasures based on the results of the operating environment monitoring plan.

6. Supervision and management of environmental protection, safety and health management accidents, and occupational disaster investigation reports within the school.

7. Health management, occupational disease prevention, and health promotion.

8. Matters related to the environmental protection and safety and health management of contracted businesses.

9. Establishment of a disaster prevention and rescue system to mitigate losses and environmental hazards caused by disasters.

10. Implementation of annual plans, proposals, funds, and budgets, and promotion of tasks by the chairman.

11. Other matters related to environmental protection and safety and health management.

The meetings shall be chaired by the chairman. The Environmental Safety and Health Committee may convene ad hoc meetings when necessary.

Article 4 When the Committee is convened, relevant persons may be invited to attend with the approval of the President.

Article 5 The Committee may establish relevant professional management working groups as necessary.

Article 6 These Measures shall come into force after being adopted by the Executive Council, and the same procedure shall apply when amended.

# Yuan Ze University Environmental Protection and Occupational Safety and Health Management Regulations

Approved at the 6th Administrative Meeting of the 87th Academic Year on November 30, 1998. Revised and approved at the 3rd EHS Committee Meeting of the 89th Academic Year on June 14, 2001. Revised and approved at the 13th Administrative Meeting of the 89th Academic Year on June 18, 2001.   
Revised and approved at the 2nd EHS Committee Meeting of the 101st Academic Year on January 16, 2013. Revised and approved at the 13th Administrative Meeting of the 101st Academic Year on March 6, 2013. Revised and approved at the 4th EHS Committee Meeting of the 101st Academic Year on June 21, 2013. Revised and approved at the 1st Administrative Meeting of the 102nd Academic Year on August 7, 2013.   
Revised and approved at the 9th Administrative Meeting of the 104th Academic Year on December 2, 2015.

## Chapter 1 General Provisions

## Article 1

To promote environmental protection and occupational safety and health, these "Yuan Ze University Environmental Protection and Occupational Safety and Health Management Regulations" (hereinafter referred to as "these Regulations") are established in accordance with relevant laws and regulations.

## Article 2

The term "Environmental Protection and Safety and Health Management" in these Regulations refers to maintaining campus environmental quality and hygiene, conserving energy resources, preventing occupational hazards, and ensuring the safety, health, and comfort of faculty, staff, and students (hereinafter referred to as "EHS Management"). These Regulations apply to all university offices, centers, colleges (departments), departments, institutes, classes, and management units, as well as to all faculty, staff, students, contractors, and temporary workers. The applicable locations referred to in these Regulations include the following locations within the university campus (hereinafter referred to as

"Applicable Locations"):

1. Student laboratories and preparation rooms.

2. Student practice rooms and preparation rooms.

3. Student training workshops.

4. Research rooms equipped with experimental facilities.

5. Other locations designated by the competent authorities.

Persons responsible for management operations in Applicable Locations are referred to as "Responsible Persons."

## Article 3

The EHS management organization and division of responsibilities are as follows:

1. Environmental Protection and Safety and Health Committee (hereinafter referred to as the "EHS Committee"): Responsible for reviewing, coordinating, and advising on EHS management matters.

2. Environmental Protection and Safety and Health Center (hereinafter referred to as the "EHS Center"): Responsible for planning, supervising, and promoting the implementation of EHS management tasks.

3. Supervisory units of offices, centers, colleges (departments), departments, institutes, and classes: Responsible for implementing EHS management tasks related to their respective units and appointing personnel to assist with execution.

## Article 4

Each university office, center, college (department), department, institute, and class should establish EHS-related task groups according to operational needs. The unit supervisor serves as the group leader and should define the operational guidelines.

## Chapter 2 Organization and Division of Responsibilities

The responsibilities of the EHS Committee are as follows:

1. Discuss and propose continuous improvements and audits for the EHS management system.

2. Evaluate and periodically review and revise plans for environmental protection, safety, and energy resource management.

3. Develop and implement educational and training plans for environmental protection, safety, and energy management.

4. Discuss and propose preventive measures for hazards involving machinery, equipment, or materials.

5. Analyze workplace environment monitoring results and propose countermeasures.

6. Supervise and address all types of EHS-related incidents and occupational accident investigations on campus.

7. Develop policies for health management, occupational disease prevention, and health promotion.

8. Discuss matters related to EHS management for contracted services.

9. Establish disaster prevention and rescue systems to reduce losses and environmental damage.

10. Discuss and propose annual plans, proposals, budgets, and tasks assigned by the committee chairperson.

11. Address other matters related to environmental protection and safety management.

## Article 6

The responsibilities of the EHS Center are as follows:

1. Develop occupational hazard prevention plans and supervise their implementation in applicable locations.

2. Plan and oversee EHS management tasks for all units.

3. Supervise inspections and reviews of EHS facilities.

4. Guide and oversee units in conducting patrols, regular checks, focused inspections, and workplace environment assessments.

5. Plan and supervise EHS education and training programs.

6. Plan and implement occupational health checks and health management.

7. Supervise occupational accident investigations and compile statistical analyses.

8. Manage other EHS-related tasks.

## Article 7

Deans of colleges (departments) shall direct and supervise the implementation of EHS management tasks within their jurisdiction and coordinate representatives to execute relevant activities. Their responsibilities for EHS management include:

1. Oversee EHS management matters related to the college (department).

2. Require all units under the college (department) to execute EHS management tasks and conduct regular evaluations.

3. Ensure all units within the college (department) cooperate in carrying out EHS management tasks.

4. Promptly address and resolve workplace hazards reported by units within the college (department).

5. Approve and implement the EHS management work plan for the college (department) and evaluate statistics related to occupational injuries.

## Article 8

The responsibilities of department, institute, and class supervisors in EHS management are as follows:

1. Direct, supervise, and execute EHS management and occupational hazard prevention for the department, institute, or class.

2. Assign EHS personnel to conduct regular inspections, focused inspections, and other related checks and guidance.

3. Conduct patrols and assessments of EHS management matters in their units.

4. Research occupational hazard prevention strategies and plan and implement EHS training programs.

5. Supervise occupational accident investigations and compile statistics for the unit.

6. Manage and investigate incidents, identify causes, and report immediately to higher authorities.

7. Execute EHS-related tasks assigned by superiors.

## Article 9

Supervisors of laboratories, experimental rooms, training workshops, testing sites, and other high-risk locations shall direct and supervise the execution of EHS management tasks within their jurisdiction and coordinate personnel accordingly. Their responsibilities include:

1. Analyze and evaluate potential hazards in the workplace, establish safety protocols, and provide environmental protection, radiation safety, and EHS-related training for personnel. Supervisors should coordinate laboratory operations and ensure adherence to procedures for environmental protection, campus safety, personal health, and work rules.

2. Manage laboratory materials and equipment, ensure proper maintenance and inspections, and report potential issues to higher authorities.

3. Conduct regular inspections, correct unsafe actions, and enforce safety protocols.

4. Provide suitable protective equipment and supervise its proper use.

5. Halt operations and evacuate personnel to safety when imminent danger arises.

6. Ensure proper labeling and documentation (e.g., Safety Data Sheets) for hazardous substances.

7. Respond promptly to incidents, follow the disaster response reporting process, provide first aid, and investigate the causes. Propose and report corrective measures.

8. Recommend workplace safety and health improvements to higher authorities.

9. Collaborate with contractors to establish and enforce EHS standards for specialized equipment.

10. Ensure that laboratory users obtain permission and comply with operational rules. Supervisors should monitor user activities and enforce regulations.

11. Promote and implement EHS management and communication responsibilities in the supervised locations.

## Chapter 3 Implementation of Self-Inspections

## Article 10

Vehicles, machinery, and equipment in applicable locations of the university, including their structure, performance, and protective measures, must comply with the safety standards stipulated in Article 7 of the Occupational Safety and Health Act.

For vehicles, machinery, and equipment in applicable locations, supervisors must conduct regular inspections in accordance with Articles 13 to 44, focused inspections per Articles 45 to 49, and operational checks of machinery and workplace environments per Articles 50 to 78 of the Occupational Safety and Health Management Regulations.

When conducting regular or focused inspections as stated above, supervisors should document inspection dates, methods, results, the name of the inspector, and methods for addressing any issues identified. Supervisors must coordinate with unit heads and EHS personnel for follow-up audits.

The university must provide necessary safety and health facilities and implement protective measures for workers and students using chemicals, as stipulated in Articles 10, 13, and 14 of the Occupational Safety and Health Act.

For regulated and priority-managed chemicals designated by central authorities, the university should regularly apply for permits from labor authorities, submit operational data for record-keeping, and comply with hierarchical management guidelines.

## Article 12

The university must plan and implement necessary safety and health measures for the following matters, as per Article 6, Paragraph 2 of the Occupational Safety and Health Act:

1. Prevention of musculoskeletal disorders caused by repetitive tasks.

2. Prevention of illnesses caused by abnormal workloads, such as shift work, night work, or prolonged working hours.

3. Prevention of physical or mental harm caused by others' actions while performing duties.

4. Provision of measures for evacuation, first aid, rest, or other protections for the physical and mental well-being of workers and students.

5. Prevention of hazards in the workplace that may affect maternal health, fetal development, pregnancy, or breastfeeding.

## Article 13

For students entering laboratories, experimental rooms, training workshops, or testing sites for learning purposes, the university must develop and implement training plans based on the characteristics of each location, as stipulated in Article 32 of the Occupational Safety and Health Act.

## Article 14

The university must ensure that occupational exposure levels in workplaces comply with permissible exposure limits set by labor authorities, as stipulated in Article 12 of the Occupational Safety and Health Act.

The EHS Center must plan and conduct workplace environment monitoring for applicable locations in accordance with the Labor Workplace Environment Monitoring Implementation Guidelines.

Personnel in each unit must perform inspections and checks, and upon identifying abnormalities or potential hazards, immediately undertake repairs, implement necessary measures, and report to higher authorities.

## Chapter 4: Incident Reporting

## Article 15

When an occupational accident occurs in the university workplace, immediate emergency response and rescue measures must be taken. A joint investigation and analysis with labor representatives should be conducted, and an "Incident Analysis Report" must be completed.

If any of the following occupational accidents occur in the university's workplace, they must be reported to the EHS Center within two hours, to the university president within four hours, and to the labor inspection agency and the Ministry of Education's Campus Safety Reporting Center within eight hours:

1. Fatal accidents.

2. Accidents resulting in injuries to three or more people.

3. Accidents causing injury to one or more people requiring hospitalization.

4. Other accidents designated and announced by central authorities.

For major occupational accidents described in items 1 to 4 above, the scene must not be disturbed or altered except for emergency and rescue measures, unless authorized by judicial authorities or labor inspection agencies.

## Chapter 5: Supplementary Provisions

## Article 16

Personnel working in applicable university locations must strictly adhere to the following:

1. Comply with the university's EHS work rules.

2. Participate in necessary health examinations.

3. Participate in required EHS-related education and training.

Violators of the above provisions may be penalized in accordance with Articles 20 and 32-34 of the Occupational Safety and Health Act, with fines of up to NT\$3,000 or referred for disciplinary action by the university.

Individuals, laboratory supervisors, or administrators who achieve significant contributions may be publicly recognized, have their accomplishments recorded as a reference for evaluations, or receive other appropriate rewards upon recommendation by the EHS Committee.

## Article 17

If a legally established laboratory operates in violation of regulations or is involved in an accident, the supervisory unit may immediately order operations to cease and temporarily place the laboratory (including equipment) under university control. Responsible parties may appeal to the EHS Committee if they disagree.

In such cases, or in instances where individuals, laboratory supervisors, or managers fail to fulfill their responsibilities, the EHS Committee may recommend public warnings, suspension or reduction of funding, documentation of poor performance, closure of non-compliant laboratories, or other appropriate penalties.

Once penalties have been fulfilled and the laboratory passes reinspection according to these Regulations, the temporary control over the laboratory (including equipment) will be lifted.

## Article 18

University construction projects must comply with the Occupational Safety and Health Act, the Guidelines for Strengthening Occupational Safety and Health in Public Works, and other relevant university regulations.

## Article 19

These Regulations shall be promulgated and implemented upon approval by the Administrative Meeting, and the same applies to any amendments.

# Yuan Ze University Tobacco Hazard Prevention and Control Measures

Adopted at the 2nd Executive Council of the 1999 academic year on September 20, 1999. Adopted by the 21st Executive Council of the 96th school year on June 30, 2008. Adopted as amended by the 8th Executive Council for the 2008 school year on January 5, 2009. Adopted by the 14th Executive Council of the 2022 school year on February 22, 2023. Amended and adopted at the 21st Executive Council of the 2022 school year on June 7, 2023.

Article 1 To enhance the prevention and control of tobacco-related harm within the university and to safeguard the health of faculty and students, the "Measures for the Prevention and Control of Tobacco Hazards at Yuan Ze University" (referred to as the "Measures") are hereby established.

Article 2 In accordance with the revisions to the Tobacco Hazard Prevention Law, the university enforces a complete tobacco ban, increases the legal smoking age to 20 years, and delineates other relevant norms and associated responsibilities of the university. The contracts with partner manufacturers of the university must explicitly state their commitment to comply with the "Tobacco Hazard Control Act." Those entering the university premises from external sources must also adhere to the provisions of the Tobacco Hazard Control Act.

Article 3 The university strictly prohibits the sale, supply, or promotion of tobacco products, including heated cigarettes and e-cigarettes, on its premises.

## Article 4 Regulations on non-smoking places in schools:

Smoking (including heated cigarettes and e-cigarettes) is prohibited in indoor and outdoor places on campus, and obvious non-smoking signs are set up at the school gate and all entrances and exits of each building to implement the comprehensive non-smoking norms on campus.

Article 5 The joint efforts to enforce the campus smoking ban involve the delegation of responsibilities and rights as follows:

1. Health Education and Advocacy: Handled by administrative units, including the Health and Security Group, Personnel Office, Environmental Safety and Health Center, Extracurricular Activities Group, Global Office, and other administrative and teaching units.

2. Environment Creation: This responsibility falls on the General Affairs Office, Student Affairs Office, and the Environmental Safety and Health Center.

3. Anti-Smoking Education and Counseling: Managed by units such as the Health Care Group, Student Support Group, Counseling Group, and involves personnel like tutors and nurses.

4. Campus Inspections: The responsibility of conducting campus inspections is entrusted to units such as the Security Team, School Security Center, Student Auxiliary Group, and Dormitory Group, among others.

5. Tobacco Hazard Notification Center: Ensuring notifications regarding tobacco hazards are addressed is the responsibility of units including the Environmental Safety and Health Center, School Safety Center, Student Auxiliary Group, Health Protection Group, Guard Team, Dormitory Group, and other related units.

6. Punishment of Violators: This responsibility falls on units like the Personnel Office, Student Auxiliary Group, Dormitory Group, General Affairs Office, and other relevant units.

7. Course Teaching: Handled by units such as the Academic Affairs Office, General Education Department, and Student Affairs Office.

Article 6 Individuals in charge of units, locations, or university staff and students are responsible for discouraging individuals who smoke in non-smoking areas on campus.

Article 7 The Personnel Office and the Student Affairs Office are responsible for conducting tobacco hazard prevention education and awareness campaigns for both faculty and students. They will also provide smoking cessation counseling services.

Article 8 Violations of these measures within the university will be addressed as follows:

1. The reporting party must provide evidence and complete the "Yuan Ze University Violation Report Form for Tobacco Hazard Prevention and Control Measures" (as an attached document). This form should be submitted to the Environmental Safety and Health Center, which will then assign the case to the relevant administrative unit (Personnel Office, Academic Affairs Office, Student Affairs Office) for assistance in identifying the involved personnel.

2. In alignment with the university's educational objectives, smoking cessation counseling and support, as well as smoking cessation resources, will be offered to help students adopt smoke-free and healthy behaviors.

3. If a student is found violating the regulations and continues to smoke, they will be referred to the Student Evaluation Committee for compulsory smoking cessation education. Should the student persist in smoking in violation of the rules or refuse to cooperate with smoking cessation education or counseling, they will be reported to the Health Bureau for legal action. For other individuals who do not cooperate after being discouraged, the Health Bureau will be notified to impose fines in accordance with the law.

Article 9 These measures will come into effect upon approval by the Executive Council and will apply to future amendments.

# Measures for the Establishment of the Environmental Protection and Safety and Health Center of Yuan Ze University

2004.07.02 Approved by the 2nd School Board Meeting of the 92 School Year   
2005.06.21 Ministry of Education Taihuan Zi No. 0940084278 Letter for verification   
2013.11.06 Amended and approved by the first school board meeting of the 2013 school year   
2015.11.04 Amended and approved by the first school board meeting of the 2015 school year

Article 1: To effectively manage environmental protection and occupational safety and health in compliance with government regulations and relevant laws, Yuan Ze University has established the Environmental Protection and Safety and Health Center, hereinafter referred to as "the Center," in accordance with the provisions of Article 23 of the Occupational Safety and Health Law.

Article 2: The Center is tasked with formulating, planning, promoting, and overseeing campuswide management of environmental protection, occupational safety and health, energy and resource conservation, disaster prevention and response, and environmental safety and health education. It is also responsible for monitoring and supervising the effectiveness of implementation within each unit. The Center's responsibilities include:

1. Environmental Protection Management

(1) Planning, supervising, and implementing campus environmental quality improvement plans.

(2) Establishing an environmental management system and promoting environmental protection policies and measures.

(3) Supervising and monitoring hazardous substances and controlled poisons in laboratories and practice sites.

(4) Overseeing and supervising the disposal of hazardous waste in laboratories and practice sites.

(5) Planning, testing, and providing guidance for the prevention and control of water pollution, air pollution, and noise pollution on campus.

(6) Managing other activities related to environmental protection.

## 2: Occupational Safety and Health Management

(1) Establishing an occupational safety and health management system and promoting related policies and measures.

(2) Formulating, implementing, and promoting occupational accident prevention and safety management plans, conducting regular key inspections, making improvements, and supervising compliance.

(3) Supervising and providing guidance for the inspection and management of hazardous machinery, equipment, and chemicals in laboratories and practice sites, as well as overseeing biosafety and health standards and radiation safety.

(4) Managing contractor operations, focusing on occupational accident prevention, rescue efforts, investigation, analysis, and record-keeping, and handling statistics related to occupational incidents.

(5) Managing the health and well-being of relevant personnel in laboratories and

practice sites.

(6) Ensuring compliance with other regulations pertaining to occupational safety and health laws.

## 3. Energy and Resource Management

(1) Overseeing policies and measures for sustainable energy use on the green campus.

(2) Establishing an energy resource management system.

## Chapter 1 General Provisions

Article 1 In accordance with the Occupational Safety and Health Act, relevant environmental protection regulations, and other applicable laws, this regulation is established to define the rights and obligations of contractors regarding environmental protection and occupational safety and health. It serves as the basis for contractor management and is hereby enacted as the Yuan Ze University Contractor Occupational Safety and Health Management Regulations (hereinafter referred to as "this Regulation").

Article 2 This Regulation applies to the following contractors engaged in work within all areas of the university:

I. Contractors who have signed construction contracts.

II. Contractors responsible for landscaping and maintenance of campus greenery.

III. Contractors providing outsourced environmental cleaning services and outsourced catering services.

IV. Contractors engaged in maintenance and servicing of various facilities and equipment.

V. Contractors and relevant personnel providing food supply and merchandise sales.

VI. Other contractors whose personnel must perform hazardous operations within the university premises, including but not limited to hot work, excavation, elevated work, hoisting, confined space operations, or other dangerous tasks.

Article 3 For the purposes of this Regulation, the term "contractor" refers to the responsible entity (company or business owner) undertaking the aforementioned operations within the university. The term "workers" refers to personnel employed by the contractor to perform on-site work. The list of personnel under contractor management is provided in Appendix 1.

Article 4 Contractors shall bear the employer responsibilities stipulated in the Occupational Safety and Health Act for the contracted work. The same responsibilities apply when subcontracting.

Article 5 Contractors shall establish a "Contractor Safety and Health Coordination Organization" with their independently recruited subcontractors and shall serve as the responsible party.

## Article 6 Hazardous Operations refer to:

Hot Work Operations:

Work that may generate ignition sources in areas containing combustible or flammable materials, such as flame cutting, electric welding, the use of gas torches, grinding machines, or other equipment capable of producing sparks. Such activities fall under the scope of hot work permit control.

Excavation Operations:

Any excavation work, whether performed manually or mechanically, in open spaces or indoors, falls under the scope of excavation permit control.

Elevated Work Operations:

Any excavation work, whether performed manually or mechanically, in open spaces or indoors, falls under the scope of excavation permit control.

Hoisting Operations:

Any lifting and hoisting activities performed by outsourced contractors using cranes or similar equipment.

Confined Space Operations:

Work performed in poorly ventilated confined spaces that may result in oxygen deficiency, poisoning, or other hazardous conditions (e.g., fire water tanks, sewage pits).

## Chapter 4 Work Safety Measures

Article 28 Work Precautions:

(1) Workers must wear work uniforms, safety helmets, and work shoes.

(2) Warning signs should be placed in hazardous areas.

(3) Flammable materials should be marked and stored separately.

(4) Personnel are not allowed to ride forklifts or other lifting equipment.

(5) It is strictly forbidden to use forklifts as work platforms.

(6) Gas cylinders must be placed on designated racks, not inverted, and classified for management.

(7) Before each day's work, a safety gear inspection and equipment check must be carried out, and records should be kept.

(8) Materials or waste should not be thrown from heights to prevent injury to personnel.

(9) Unauthorized activation of machinery switches belonging to other units is prohibited.

(10) Regular maintenance and inspection of machinery should be performed, with records available for review.

(11) Proper use of protective equipment is required, and the equipment should be inspected regularly.

## Article 29

Elevated Work Precautions:

(1) Elevated work above 2 meters should use work platforms or scaffolds.

(2) Safety measures should be in place for elevated work, such as guardrails, ladders for personnel to climb, and scaffolding with appropriate strength specifications in compliance with national safety standards.

(3) Contractors should check the health status of personnel before employing them for elevated work, and workers restricted by safety standards for elevated work should not be employed.

(4) Warning signs and safety fences should be placed around elevated work areas to prevent unauthorized personnel from entering the danger zone.

(5) For elevated work near overhead power lines, appropriate insulation and protective measures, such as shields and warning signs, should be implemented to prevent electric shock hazards.

(6) The setup, connection, support materials, and safety devices of scaffolding must comply with relevant regulations.

(7) For areas with a falling hazard, guardrails of sufficient strength should be installed, and warning signs should be posted.

(8) Elevated workers must wear safety belts or lanyards, ensuring proper length based on the height of the work area, and securely attach the lanyard to a solid structure above waist level. If necessary, a safety lifeline should be set up for attaching the safety belt.

(9) Before each day's work, safety measures should be inspected, and workers should conduct self-checks.

## Article 30

Electrical Work Safety Precautions:

(1) Do not contact electrical circuits unless it is confirmed that the circuit has been de-energized.

(2) When maintaining or de-energizing electrical equipment, a "Under Maintenance, Do Not Energize" sign should be placed, and electrical switches should be locked.

(3) Use testing equipment to check for electricity in circuits.

(4) Do not use wires or other metal conductors as replacements for fuses.

(5) Do not hang objects from wires.

(6) Do not overload electrical circuits by connecting too many devices.

(7) Electrical connections should be made according to the designated switchboard; when electrical wires are damaged, use should be stopped immediately.

(8) Electrical fires should be extinguished using dry powder fire extinguishers.

(9) Elevated welding work should have fire shielding properly installed.

(10) Welding machines should be equipped with electric shock protection devices.

(11) For portable or mobile electric equipment, ensure that suitable residual current circuit breakers (RCCBs) with high sensitivity and fast action are installed on the electrical connections.

## Article 31

Confined Space Work Precautions:

(1) Confined spaces refer to areas not regularly used for work, with limited entry or exit, and where natural ventilation cannot maintain clean, sufficient air.

(2) Before conducting work in a confined space, an oxygen deficiency work supervisor must be designated, and the related protective measures must be confirmed by safety personnel or work supervisors. Designated personnel should monitor the space before entry.

(3) Workers entering confined spaces must be registered.

(4) Before workers are employed for confined space work (with oxygen deficiency hazards), the contractor should measure the oxygen, hydrogen sulfide, carbon monoxide, methane, and other gas concentrations in the air. If gas concentrations exceed permissible levels or if harmful gases are present, entry should be prohibited.

(5) When welding, cutting, burning, or heating work is performed in confined spaces, a designated person must confirm the absence of hazards, and the work supervisor or responsible person must approve and sign a hot work permit before proceeding.

(6) Unauthorized personnel should be prohibited from entering confined spaces, with clear signage at the entrance.

(7) To prevent oxygen deficiency hazards in confined spaces, contractors should prepare ventilation equipment, gas detectors, air respirators, safety harnesses, fall arrest devices, etc.

## Article 32: Hoisting Operations Precautions:

(1) Personnel are strictly prohibited from walking or lingering beneath hoisted loads.

(2) Hoisting operations must have designated safety boundaries and personnel assigned to monitor and direct traffic.

(3) Before use, lifting equipment and slings (ropes, chains, clamps) must undergo self-inspection in accordance with regulations, with records retained for three years for reference.

## Article 33

Catering and Goods Sales Operations Precautions:

(1) Electrical appliances used for catering and sales must comply with the relevant provisions of Article 30.

(2) Contractors may not modify, install, remove, or damage kitchen gas hoses, stoves, electrical wiring, etc., without prior approval from the school's Dormitory Services Section of the Student Affairs Office.

(3) Since gas is a flammable and hazardous substance, workplaces using gas must have gas warning signs and Material Safety Data Sheets (MSDS). Contractors must educate and inform their employees about gas safety, keep records, and retain them for future reference.

(4) Before daily operations, contractors must inspect gas valves, connections, hoses, stoves, and burners for leaks or unusual odors. If any abnormalities are detected, the issue must be investigated immediately, personnel must be evacuated as needed, and the incident must be reported to the Maintenance Section and the General Affairs Section for repairs. The contractor shall bear all repair costs. For safety reasons, gas and electrical appliances must not be used until leaks or odors have been fully addressed.

(5) Before leaving at the end of the workday, contractors must inspect and ensure that all gas and electrical appliances are properly turned off.

Article 50 If hot work is conducted without prior approval of a hazardous work permit: For the first offense, a fine of NT\$1,000 will be imposed on the contractor’s responsible person. For the second offense, a fine of NT\$2,000 will be imposed on the contractor’s responsible person. For the third offense, a fine of NT\$3,000 will be imposed on the contractor’s responsible person. For the fourth offense and beyond, a fine of NT\$5,000 will be imposed, and the individual will be prohibited from working on campus for one month.

Article 51 If a hazardous work permit (excluding hot work) has not been applied for, or if a safety work permit is not displayed at the work site: For the first offense, a fine of NT\$2,000 will be imposed on the contractor’s responsible person. For the second offense, a fine of NT\$4,000 will be imposed on the contractor’s responsible person. For the third offense, a fine of NT\$5,000 will be imposed on the contractor’s responsible person. For the fourth offense and beyond, a fine of NT\$6,000 will be imposed on the contractor’s responsible person.

Article 52 If the work site is not cleaned up at the end of each workday during construction, a fine of NT\$2,000 will be imposed.

Article 53 If a contractor uses vehicles to transport waste, rubble, sand, or gravel

without proper protection, and it causes pollution:   
For the first offense, a fine of NT\$5,000 will be imposed on the   
contractor’s responsible person.   
For the second offense, a fine of NT\$10,000 will be imposed on the contractor’s responsible person.   
For the third offense and beyond, a fine of NT\$20,000 will be imposed on the contractor’s responsible person.

Article 54 If a crane enters the campus without obtaining approval from the supervising unit, it will be prohibited from entering. If entry is made without permission, a fine of NT\$3,000 will be imposed on the contractor’s responsible person.

Article 55 If, after the commencement of a contracted project, the contractor fails to submit the "Occupational Safety and Health Management Personnel Registration Form" to the labor inspection agency to complete the reporting procedures, a fine of NT\$5,000 will be imposed on the contractor’s responsible person.

Article 56 If the contractor’s safety personnel do not arrive at the work site to perform their duties during construction: For the first offense, a fine of NT\$3,000 will be imposed on the contractor’s responsible person. For the second offense, a fine of NT\$5,000 will be imposed on the contractor’s responsible person For the third offense, a fine of NT\$7,000 will be imposed on the contractor’s responsible person. For the fourth offense and beyond, a fine of NT\$10,000 will be imposed on the contractor’s responsible person.

# Yuan Ze University Regulations for Handling Unlawful Infringement Incidents Arising from the Performance of

Duties

Approved at the Occupational Safety and Health Center Meeting on March 24, 2025

Approved at the 14th Administrative Meeting of Academic Year 2024 on April 16, 2025

## Article 1

These Regulations (hereinafter referred to as “the Regulations”) are enacted to address incidents where faculty or staff members of Yuan Ze University suffer unlawful infringement while performing their duties at the University.

## Article 2

The Regulations apply to situations where University faculty or staff, during the course of duty at the workplace, are subject to inappropriate conduct by employers, supervisors, colleagues, service recipients, or other third parties. Such conduct may include speech, writing, bodily actions, electronic communication, the Internet, or other means, resulting in unlawful infringement upon the individual’s physical or mental well-being. Examples include workplace violence, bullying, sexual harassment, or employment discrimination.

## Article 3

Reporting procedures shall be carried out in accordance with the flowchart (Annex 1):

1. When faculty and staff become aware of, or experience, suspected unlawful workplace conduct, the concerned party or reporter shall complete the “Yuan Ze University Suspected Workplace Unlawful Infringement Reporting Form” (Annex 2) and submit it to the Occupational Safety and Health Center (hereinafter “the Center”).

2. In emergency situations, or when the suspected unlawful infringement is committed by an external party, oral, telephone, fax, or instant messaging reports may first be made to the immediate workplace supervisor, the Security Guard Unit, the Campus Security Center, or other relevant supervisors, to facilitate victim assistance, medical care, or referral to police authorities as necessary. A written report shall be submitted to the Center within 14 days.

3. Cases concerning sexual harassment or gender equality shall be processed through the Personnel Office in accordance with the University’s gender equity complaint procedures.

4. Reports shall be submitted within one year of the unlawful act. For ongoing incidents, the one-year limit shall be calculated from the day after the last occurrence.

5. During the review process, if the complainant wishes to withdraw, they shall complete the “Yuan Ze University Suspected Workplace Unlawful Infringement Withdrawal Application Form” (Annex 3). Once withdrawn, the case cannot be refiled unless the withdrawal was due to reasons beyond the complainant’s control.

6. If the accused is the University President, the report shall be submitted directly to the Ministry of Education.

7. Within three working days of receiving a workplace violence report, the Director of the Center shall submit the evidence and documents to the President, who shall assign appropriate personnel to mediate or investigate. The complainant or reporter shall be notified in writing or by email within 20 days regarding whether the case will be accepted.

8. Once accepted, mediation may be conducted, requiring both complainant and respondent to attend and complete the “Yuan Ze University Suspected Workplace Unlawful Infringement Disposition Form” (Annex 4). If mediation is unsuccessfu or rejected, the case shall be referred to the Investigation Committee.

9. The Investigation Committee shall complete its investigation within two months of being convened. If necessary, one extension of up to two months may be granted with reasons provided to the complainant.

10. The Investigation Committee shall clarify the facts, deliberate, and prepare a report. Upon approval by the President, the deliberation report shall be provided in writing to both complainant and respondent.

11. If either party is dissatisfied with the investigation findings, they may, within 14 working days of receipt, submit the “Yuan Ze University Appeal Form for Unlawful Infringement of Duties” (Annex 5) along with the original investigation response to the Center. The President shall appoint new committee members to reinvestigate. Only one appeal is permitted. If the appellant remains dissatisfied, they may seek recourse from higher supervisory authorities such as labor inspection agencies, police, or legal institutions.

## Article 4

Mediation Committee, Investigation Committee composition and operation:

1. For mediation, the President shall appoint 1–2 faculty/staff members as Mediation Committee members to facilitate discussions and submit records of mediation results for approval.

2. The Investigation Committee shall be chaired by a Vice President. If absent, the chair may appoint another member to preside. Members shall include one faculty/staff representative and two experts with backgrounds in law, dispute resolution, counseling, policing, occupational safety, or legal affairs.

Appointments require Presidential approval, are for a two-year term (renewable), and vacancies shall be filled for the remainder of the term.

3. Any member with a conflict of interest in a case must recuse themselves, and the President shall appoint a replacement.

4. Committee meetings require attendance by more than half of members, including both professional experts, for a quorum.

5. When necessary, relevant personnel, special education professionals, or external scholars/experts may be invited to attend for assistance.

## Article 5

Reports of unlawful infringement shall not be accepted in the following cases:

1. The complaint fails to meet requirements, cannot be corrected, or remains uncorrected within 14 days of notice.

2. The same matter has already been resolved, withdrawn, or reviewed, unless new evidence is presented.

3. The matter does not fall within the scope of unlawful workplace infringement.

4. The complaint lacks specific facts, a real name, or service unit.

5. The complaint is submitted after the reporting deadline.

## Article 6

Procedures for investigation:

1. Both parties shall be given the opportunity to present their statements. If either refuses to attend, the committee shall deliberate based on available evidence.

2. Direct confrontation between complainant and respondent shall be avoided.

3. The identities of complainants, reporters, witnesses, and those assisting in the investigation shall remain confidential.

## Article 7

The purpose of complaints is to resolve University issues. Investigations must be conducted objectively, fairly, and impartially, while protecting the privacy and rights of reporters. Exceptions may apply when disclosure is necessary for investigation or public interest. To avoid undue pressure, committee member lists shall not be made public.

## Article 8

Faculty or staff who cease work and withdraw to a safe location due to imminent danger to life or health while performing duties shall not be subject to adverse disciplinary actions.

## Article 9

Perpetrators shall not retaliate against complainants. If relations between parties deteriorate to the extent that working together is no longer possible, with mutual consent, adjustments in duties or reassignment may be made to protect the victim from further harm.

## Article 10

If a case enters judicial proceedings, the Investigation Committee may decide to suspend its own investigation and deliberation.

## Article 11

These Regulations shall be implemented upon approval by the Administrative Meeting. Amendments shall follow the same procedure.
