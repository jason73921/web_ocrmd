NTU x ECCT Women in ESG Leadership: Shattering the Ceiling with Impact

財務報告是過往評量企業發展最主要的依據，然而在21世紀全球化與永續議題的挑戰下，有更多非財務因子影響企業的中長程發展。聯合國遂於2004年提出「環境保護、社會責任與公司治理（ESG）」的概念，漸成為用來評估企業能否永續經營的重要面向與指標，除了許多企業每年會自主發表ESG永續報告書外，金管會也要求今（2023）年起資本額達20億元的上市櫃公司，皆須編製和申報永續報告書。

研究顯示，女性進入領導階層能提升企業在ESG面向上的表現。台大因此與歐洲在台商務協會低碳倡議行動合作，特別邀請來自資訊、醫療、美妝、能源與汽車等五大產業的女性領袖，分享自身的學思歷程，以及如何運用所學，促進公司在ESG面向上的表現，進而啟發青年學習與探索志趣，在未來進入職場後，能投入ESG發展。

NTU x ECCT Women in ESG Leadership Shattering the Ceiling with Impact

Financial reports have been the primary basis for evaluating a company’s development in the past. However, facing the challenges of globalization and sustainability in the 21st century, more and more non-financial factors are influencing the mid-to-long term development of businesses. In 2004, the United Nations put forth the idea of “environment, social, and corporate governance (ESG),” which gradually became an important indicator for evaluating whether a company can operate sustainably. Not only do many businesses voluntarily publish an annual ESG sustainability report, but starting this year (2023) the Financial Supervisory Commission also requires listed and OTC-traded companies with a capital of more than 2 billion NTD to submit a sustainability report.

Research shows that women leaders are especially adept at improving a company’s ESG performance. In partnership with the European Chamber of Commerce Taiwan Low Carbon Initiative, NTU has invited women leaders from the information, medical, beauty, energy, and automotive industries to share their learning process and applications for promoting their company’s performance in ESG related aspects. This impressive panel of leaders will inspire local and international young talents to learn and explore their interests, so that they can contribute to ESG development when they enter the workforce.
