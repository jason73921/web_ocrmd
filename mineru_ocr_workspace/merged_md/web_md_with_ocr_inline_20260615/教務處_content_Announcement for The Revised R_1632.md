# **Yuan Ze University**

# **Regulations on the Inter-University Course-Select Program**

Passed at the 4th Academic Affairs meeting, Academic Year 1995, on November 12, 1995

Amendments passed at the 3rd Academic Affairs meeting, Academic Year 1999, on November 17, 1999

Amendments passed at the 2nd Academic Affairs meeting, Academic Year 2004, on December 1, 2004

MOE Reference NO. 0930177882 , on January 12, 2005

Amended by the 1st Academic Affairs Meeting, Academic Year 2023, on September 27, 2023

Amended by the 6th Academic Affairs Meeting, Academic Year 2023, on January 3, 2024

Amended by the 7th Academic Affairs Meeting, Academic Year 2024, on March 20, 2024

1. The regulations on the inter-University course-select program, established based on the University Regulations Code Session 26, aim to make full use of teaching resources as well as enhance inter-university cooperation.
2. The number of credits earned from the inter-university course-select program is nomore than one-third of the total credits received for courses taken during the semester. For Bachelor’s degree students born after 2005, particularly conscripted soldiers applying for flexible study programs, they were restricted to one-half(inclusive) of the total credits taken that semester. Senior 5<sup>th</sup>-year undergraduates, as well as master’s and Ph.D. graduates, are exempt from the rule above. The conflicts between off-campus courses and on-campus courses should be avoided. The course conflict will receive no marks without objection.
3. The conflicts between off-campus courses and on-campus courses should beavoided. The course conflicted will receive no marks without objection.
4. To select off-campus courses, YZU students should submit an inter-University course-select application form followed by obtaining the approval of the chair ofthe department. Required courses must also obtain the approval of the departmentwhere the courses are offered.
5. Students enrolled at another University intending to select courses offered by YZUmust submit a written consent issued by their University before the announced deadline of course select. Late submission will not be acceptable.
6. Students enrolled at another University intending to select courses offered by YZUmust pay tuition fees based on the University regulations on tuition fees. Practical training fee is required for a course with practical training in addition to course fees.
7. The University students enrolled in a course get the priority over off-campus students enrolled in the same course when the course reaches its enrollment limit.
8. No refund is provided after the enrollment process is completed unless the course isnot available due to insufficient students required for course opening.
9. The assessment of academic performance and the calculation of grades received from the inter- University courses must be implemented in accordance with the regulations set by the University

at which the courses are delivered. The students’ home University will receive the grades from the University where the course are completed.

10. The Regulation shall be announced and implemented after adopted at the Academic Affairs Council. The same procedures shall apply to revision.

**If any controversies or disputes occurred regarding clauses of the contract, it shall always refer to its Chinese version.**

# **Yuan Ze University Regulations on Summer Courses**

Passed at the 13th Academic Affairs meeting in Academic Year 1996, May 14, 1997

Amendments passed at the 14th Academic Affairs meeting in Academic Year 1996, July 2, 1997

Amendments passed at the 10th Academic Affairs meeting in Academic Year 1997, May 27, 1998

Amendments passed at the 5th Academic Affairs meeting in Academic Year 2000, March 26,2001

MOE Reference No. 90053624, April 27, 2001

MOE Reference No. 0940014667, February 14, 2005

Amendments passed at the 5th Academic Affairs meeting in Academic Year 2004, June 19, 2005

Amendments passed at the 6th Academic Affairs meeting in Academic Year 2006, July 4, 2007

MOE Reference No. 0960114336, August 8, 2007

Amended by the 2nd Academic Affairs Meeting, Academic Year 2022, on November 16, 2022

Amended by the 3rd Academic Affairs Meeting, Academic Year 2022, on December 28, 2022

Amended by the 1st Academic Affairs Meeting, Academic Year 2023, on September 27, 2023

Amended by the 6th Academic Affairs Meeting, Academic Year 2023, on January 3, 2024

Amended by the 7th Academic Affairs Meeting, Academic Year 2024, on March 20, 2024

# 1 The University offers two types of summer courses as follows:

## 1.1 Academic Course

The academic courses are offered at two terms per academic year in accordance with the academic requirements. There are 18 lecture hours including midterm and final per credit except for the practical training course which requires 36 lecture hours per credit.

## 1.2 Prerequisites

Certain academic courses require that students have prerequisites in order to add that course to their regular academic study schedule. Prerequisite credits will compute into the credits earned in the following academic year. The lecture hours for each credit are offeredin accordance with the University Lecture Regulations.

# 2 All current enrolled students are eligible for the summer courses except for students suspending from school. The priorities given from top to bottom to students for enrollment in the summer courses are as follows:

## 2.1 Graduating students who must repeat or make up required courses in order to graduate from the University

## 2.2 Students who fail courses and are required to repeat the failed courses

## 2.3 Transfer students, Bachelor's degree students born in or after the year 2005 applying for flexible study programs who are conscripted soldiers.

## 2.4 Students who currently declare a minor/a double major

## 2.5 Students who withdraw from courses

## 2.6 Others such as transfer students with the approval of the chairperson of their department of their chairperson of the department

# 3 Students who qualify for the students as described in Regulation 2 above can enroll in summer courses offered by the University or by other Universities. Bachelor's degree students born in or after the year 2005 applying for flexible study programs, particularly conscripted soldiers, are not subject to this limitation.

# 4 There must be at least fifteen students for the summer course opening. The minimum number of students for a perquisite opening must conform to the number described in the academic regulation.

If the enrollment for summer courses does not meet the standard, but there is a special circumstance that necessitates the opening of the class, it may proceed with the consent of enrolled students agreeing to share the course credit fees. For bachelor's degrees, students born in or after the year 2005 applying for flexible study programs, especially conscripted soldiers, the regulations for the minimum number of students required for summer classes at this university will be applied for the collection of credit fees.

For bachelor's degree students born after 2005 who apply for the flexible study program and who need summer courses, our school can match students with cross-university summer course selection, or cross-university cooperation to jointly offer summer courses.

# 5 Summer classes will be delivered at two consecutive terms with a period of six weeks per term based on the announcement of the Office of Academic Affairs. The teaching assistant numbers available for each class are decided based on the conditions below. One teaching assistant will be assigned to a summer class of no less than 25 students. Two teaching assistants will be assigned to a summer class of no less than 55 students. The number of teaching assistants is adjustable according to the total student numbers enrolled in all available courses. The course instructor or the chairperson of the department will select qualified teaching assistants (TAs) among candidates, and inform the Office of the Academic Affairs of the selected TAs afterwards. The salary rates for TAs serving are set based on the Table of Standard Hourly PayRate, and the fee for each course's teaching assistant is calculated over a four-month period.

# 6 Student enrolling in summer courses must complete the procedure of registration and pay tuition fees according to the University academic regulations. Tuition fees paid are

non-refundable after courses begin. Students can apply for courses drop without fees refunded within 9 course hours

# 7 Summer Session Tuition Fees Policy

## 7.1 Summer Course:

Fees and other relative fees are charged based on the tuition policy announced in the same academic year.

## 7.2 Prerequisite Course:

No fees are charged for the undergraduate and postgraduate courses. Fees are charged for those students who require extending study.

# 8 Faculty hourly wage

## 8.1 Summer Course:

Except for specific circumstances, faculty hourly wage is subject to the hourly pay rate set for the Summer Course.

## 8.2 Prerequisite Course:

The Faculty hourly wage setting is based on the standard of Regulations Governing Yuan Ze University Instructor Teaching Hours and Buy-Out.

# 9 Assessment of Academic Performance for the Summer Session

## 9.1 All grades either passing or failing will be kept in the academic records.

## 9.2 Summer Course:

All credits earned in the summer session will not be computed into academic credits earned in the following semester.

Grades earned in the summer session will not be included in the GPA calculation in the following semester.

## 9.3 Prerequisite Course:

All credits earned in the summer session will be added into the total credits earned in the following semester.

Grades earned in summer courses will be included in the GPA calculation in the following semester.

## 9.4 Students cannot make up for courses receiving failing grades.

# 10 Eligibility

YZU Summer courses are available for the on-campus students only. Off-campus students must produce a written consent of their head of relevant academic units to enroll in the YZU summer courses

# 11 Issues without being declared in the regulations stated herein must be processed based on the University Academic Regulations.

# 12 The Regulation shall be announced and implemented after adopted at the Academic Affairs Council. The same procedures shall apply to revision.

**If any controversies or disputes occurred regarding clauses of the contract, it shall always refer to its Chinese version.**

# Yuan Ze University

# Regulations for Establishing Academic Credit Programs

Passed by the 5th Academic Affairs Meeting, Academic Year 2004, on April 28, 2004

Amended by the 1st Academic Affairs Meeting, Academic Year 2006, on September 20, 2006

Amended by the 2nd Academic Affairs Meeting, Academic Year 2007, on November 7, 2007

Amended by the 2nd Academic Affairs Meeting, Academic Year 2009, on November 4, 2009

Amended by the 4th Academic Affairs meeting, Academic Year 2012, on March 7, 2012

Amended by the 2nd Academic Affairs meeting, Academic Year 2017, on November 15, 2017

Amended by the 7nd Academic Affairs meeting, Academic Year 2023, on May 31, 2023

Amended by the 7nd Academic Affairs meeting, Academic Year 2024, on March 20,2024

1. The regulations established aim to provide a standard guideline when setting up academic credit programs in academic units. The course programs stated in the regulations are referred to the design and combination of cross department/school/college curriculum in all professional learning fields and are offered as an interdisciplinary field that combines courses from two or more degree programs or academic credit programs. Academic credit programs should invite an Assistant Professor, Associate Professor, or Professor, as the director of the academic credit programs, to be in charge of establishing academic credit programs, handling promotion activities and issuing certificate; besides, the academic unit which the director of academic credit programs belongs to should take charge of the general administrative affairs of academic credit programs. Academic credit programs can set up an Academic Credit Program Committee if needed.

2. The Academic Credit Programs of the school are divided into Normal Program and Micro-Program. For the Normal Program, the student shall select at least 12 credits, and for the Micro-Program shall select at least 8 credits. To establish the academic credit programs, the relevant regulations on matters—objectives, program title, curriculum plan, required subjects and credits thereof, director of academic credit programs, eligibility, student numbers, approval process, application deadline and certificates of academic credit programs and standard thereof must be established by academic units in charge and must be adopted by the Curriculum Committee Meeting and the Academic Affairs Meeting for ratification and implementation before announcement, as shall amendments when they are made.

Yuan Ze University Industry-Academia Programs are divided into "Enterprise Programs" and "Industry Programs". The names, content, and implementation methods of these programs are conducted in accordance with the regulations stipulated in the "Yuan Ze University Regulations for the Establishment of Industry-Academia Programs ".

3. The minimum credits required for graduation will not be increased due to studying academic credit programs. Moreover, students cannot apply for extending study period due to studying academic credit programs except as provided in other regulations.

4. Students who intend to study the academic credit programs should meet all required conditions, register online for studying academic credit programs in the application period and receive the approval of the application from academic units in charge of academic credit programs. Then, students can select courses of academic credit programs online.

5. In order to obtain the certificates of the academic credit programs, students who complete all required subjects and receive all credits for those courses can file an application with their official transcripts enclosed to colleges at which the academic credit programs are offered and receive the approval of the application afterwards. The certificates won’t be available for students who do not register for studying the course programs in advance.

6. All applications for academic credit programs must be filed online. The format for the certificate of academic credit programs is designed and set up by Academic Affairs Office except as provided in other regulations.

7. If the academic credit programs cannot be offered due to the change of educational objectives, the transition of industrial development or other factors, the units in charge of these programs can alter the programs’ contents or cancel the programs based on the original approved procedures.

If the number of students enrolled in the academic credit programs is less than 10 in a consecutive of 3 academic years, the Office of Academic Affairs can call a meeting to reevaluate the establishment of these programs. The programs can be called off after the re-evaluation being approved by the Curriculum Committee Meeting and passed at the Academic Affairs Meeting afterwards.

8. These regulations will be adopted by the Academic Affairs Meeting before implementation, as shall amendments when they are made.

**If any controversies or disputes occurred regarding clauses of the contract, it shall always refer to its Chinese version**
