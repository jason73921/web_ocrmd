- **文章簡介**: Protect every step you take in the online world! Office of Library and Information Services will hold a series of cybersecurity seminars in August. Learn how to protect personal data, avoid online scams, and understand the latest cybersecurity trends. With a range of topics closely related to daily life, these seminars will help you feel more secure while navigating the internet!
- **文章全文**: 1. Participants: Faculty and staff interested in the topic.

2. Series Course Content:

(1) 8/15 (Thursday) 10:00-12:00: "Information Security and Teaching Innovation: Cryptography in Movies" by 林熙中老師

(2) 8/20 (Tuesday) 10:00-12:00: "Fundamentals of Information Security and the Challenges of the AI Era" by 周兆龍老師

(3) 8/22 (Thursday) 10:00-12:00: "Privacy-Enhancing Technologies: Theory and Practice of Differential Privacy" by 周兆龍老師

(4) 8/27 (Tuesday) 12:00-14:00: "Enhancing Personal Information Security Awareness and Preventing Social Engineering Fraud" by 周兆龍老師

(5) 8/28 (Wednesday) 12:00-15:00: "Privacy and Security Issues of Cookies" by 許富皓老師

3. Organizers: Guided by the Ministry of Education; Office of Library and Information Services 

4. Notes:

(1) Lunch will be provided at noon; for afternoon activities, lunch boxes will be available.

(2) Participants who attend the entire session of each course can receive 2 or 3 hours of faculty training credit (depending on the announcement for each session). If participants do not stay for the entire session, they will not receive certification for the hours.

(3) Contact Person: Mr. Hu from the Media Services Group, Extension 3109.
