This agreement is an addendum to the Memorandum of Understanding that hereinafter referred to as Memorandum of Agreement (MoA) that contains the terms and conditions governing a joint bachelor degree completion program in which students from undergraduate program of Accounting, Business and Management, Information System (Computer Science), and Electrical Engineering who have completed minimum 50% of credits or two years of undergraduate study at UIB, may complete a bachelor degree within another two years at YZU and reciprocally. It is signed:

## BETWEEN:

Yuan Ze University, a private Taiwan university, with the principal office at 135 Yuan-Tung Road, Chung-Li, Taoyuan City, Taiwan, hereinafter referred to as “YZU”, and represented in this instrument by its President, Prof. Jyh-Yang Wu.

## AND

Universitas Internasional Batam, a private university in Indonesia, with the principal office at Jl. Gajah Mada, Baloi-Sei Ladi, Batam, 29442, Indonesia, hereinafter referred to as “UIB”, and represented in this instrument by its President, Prof. Dr. Handoko Karjantoro, CPA.

Hereinafter is referred to singularly as “the Party” and collectively as “the Parties”.

## WHEREAS:

a. YZU is the first bilingual university in Taiwan which aims to offer a professional education for skills required by the working professionals, and higher education for the national development based on the educational ideals of the Republic of China (Taiwan) and founding spirits of this school foundation.

b. UIB is an established University, which strives to enhance its education and research excellence to participate in national development process focuses in educating the nation’s people. UIB has some collaborative arrangements with other parties and always strengthens its national and or international linkages.

c. The Parties are desirous of entering into this MoA to declare their respective intentions and to establish a basis of co-operation and collaboration between the Parties upon the terms as contained herein.

## HAVE REACHED AN UNDERSTANDING as follow:

## ARTICLE I. SCOPE OF PARTNERSHIP

## 1.1. Objective

The purpose of this MoA is to establish Joint Bachelor's Degree Program to provide students of UIB or students of YZU with curriculum based on study an accredited program in UIB or in YZU, and to provide an opportunity for students to enroll at YZU or at UIB to get a Joint Bachelor's degree in Accounting, Business and Management, Information System (Computer Science), and Electrical Engineering in Taiwan and in Indonesia. The program may extend to other graduate majors in the future.

## 1.2. Program Name

For the purpose of this document the joint program is called “2+2 Joint Degree Program in Accounting, Business and Management, Information System (Computer Science), and Electrical Engineering” hereinafter referred to as the “Program”.

## 1.3. Graduation and Degree

The enrolled student must be able to fulfill all graduation requirements for Bachelor degree in YZU and UIB at undergraduate program of Accounting, Business and Management, Information System (Computer Science) and Electrical Engineering. Both universities agree upon arrangement of the program in which the students meet the requirements of the studies to obtain a degree from each university. Upon completion of the program, students will receive a Bachelor Degree Certificate issued by YZU and UIB respectively. The certificate of Degree Program of the students enrolled should be sent officially to UIB or YZU office. This degree is valid in Taiwan or Indonesia for continuing their study.

## 1.4. Approval

It is understood that authority to enter into this Agreement must be granted by the approval authorities of both universities.

## ARTICLE II. PROGRAM OF STUDY

## 2.1. Period of Study

Standard period of study is 2+2. Students who participate will complete two (2) years (the first and the second year) at their home university, then they will continue their study in YZU or UIB for two (2) year2 (the third and the fourth year).

## 2.2. Areas of Program

Between YZU and UIB program will focus initially on the 2+2 Joint Bachelor's Degree in Taiwan or Indonesia. For the next, the Parties will send their students under this MoA.

## 2.3. Curriculum

UIB or YZU will adjust their curriculum in accordance with content of the curriculum of the Bachelor’s Degree Program.

## 2.4. Required Credits

The total credits to accomplish undergraduate of bachelor degree in Indonesia is minimum 144 credits with a minimum GPA of 3.00/4.00 including mini thesis (skripsi), Indonesian language, Religion, The state philosophy (Pancasila) subjects, while in Taiwan is 128 credits with a minimum GPA of 3.00/4.00. Participating students who complete minimum of 72 credits at UIB and 60 credits at YZU and fulfill the requirements of both YZU and UIB will earn the Bachelor degrees from both institutions. In the event that students only fulfill the requirement of either home or host university, they will be granted with only one Bachelor degree from home or host university.

Therefore, the credits of students before joining the 2+2 program are minimum of 50% credits of degree requirements at UIB for two years and minimum of 60 credits of degree requirements at YZU for two years. The detail of curriculum mapping is as an attachment 1 of the MOA.

## 2.5. Undergraduate Thesis and Details of Supervision

The undergraduate thesis (skripsi) is written in the last semester for UIB students. It must be completed in Indonesian or English. UIB Students will have advisor(s) from UIB and/or YZU. Oral examination will be conducted in UIB upon the completion of the program or any other way that is agreed by both parties. UIB is the party which shall publish research findings.

## ARTICLE III. ADMINISTRATION AND TIMEFRAME

## 3.1. Admission procedure

Both parties guarantee that participants of the Program will be selected according to their academic, personal, and their linguistic qualifications. To enter the program, students first have to be admitted in the Undergraduate Program at UIB or at YZU. Students of UIB or YZU will apply for admission to YZU or UIB in accordance with the international student application process. YZU or UIB will provide participating students with the necessary documents to obtain visas in preparation for their temporary stay in Taiwan or Indonesia.

UIB and YZU will admit qualified students from undergraduate program of Accounting, Business and Management, Information System (Computer Science), and Electrical Engineering, if they satisfy the following admission requirements: (A) Good Academic Standing, and (B) Language Proficiency.

## 3.2. Good Academic Standing

The applicant should have completed minimum 50% of credits or two years of study at undergraduate program of Accounting, Business and Management, Information System (Computer Science), and Electrical Engineering in UIB or in YZU or should be in their forth semester. Also, the applicant should be in good academic standing with a cumulative grade point average of at least 3.00/4.00 (for students from UIB), 3.0/4.5 (for students from YZU). A student may need to extend his/her matriculation beyond two full years if satisfactory progress has not been made in the two full academic years at YZU or UIB.

## 3.3. Language Proficiency

During their first two years at home university, students admitted to this Program must develop their English proficiency or TOCFL for UIB students and English proficiency or Indonesian Language Proficiency for YZU Students.

## a. English Language Proficiency for UIB and YZU Students

The applicants from both UIB and YZU are required to have TOEIC score of minimum 550 for Engineering college, 600 for informatic college and 650 for management college.

b. TOCFL for UIB students whose TOEIC score lower than the requirement

The applicant should have TOCFL level 3 for students whose TOEIC score lower than the requirement.

The applicant should have SEMENJANA or above level of Indonesian Language Proficiency Test (UKBI). For students who do not have Indonesian Language proficiency can study of Indonesian Language for Foreigner (BIPA) at UIB Language Center and Culture (UCLC) to enhance their language proficiency. In this case, students can study for one-year as an exchange student; for exchange students, the BIPA tuition will be waived for 1 year (even and odd semester). If the student fails to get SEMENJANA level or higher within 1 year, scholarship benefit for the exchange student will be terminated and the student will have to pay the tuition for the extended period.

## 3.4. Number of Students

The number of students participating in the Program will be decided upon through mutual discussions between the two universities.

## 3.5. Timeframe for Administration

a. The parties should send the applications by mid of November for the spring semester, and by mid of April for the fall semester.

b. The parties should send the letter of acceptance and the visa application document by beginning of January for the spring semester, and by beginning of June for the fall semester.

e. The certificate of degree program of the students should be sent officially to UIB or YZU office within one month after the completion of program.

The parties reciprocally oblige to:

c. Oversee and assist in the admission application and the visa application processes.

d. Provide the applicants with the appropriate departure orientation.

# STUDENT EXCHANGE PROGRAMS AGREEMENT BETWEEN UNIVERSITAS INTERNASIONAL BATAM, INDONESIA No. 001/REK/MoA-UIB/VI/2017 AND YUAN ZE UNIVERSITY, TAIWAN

This Agreement is made as of June 19, 2017 by and between Yuan Ze University, Chung-Li, Taiwan, R.O.C., (hereinafter “YZU”) and Universitas Internasional Batam (hereinafter “UIB”) pursuant to the Cooperation Agreement executed between the two universities.

The purpose of this Agreement is to establish the terms and conditions under which the exchange of students between YZU and UIB shall take place.

## Both parties hereby agree that:

## 1. Program Management

The student exchange program shall be available to students of both universities and shall be administered by YZU and UIB respectively via the Office of International Affairs of YZU and UIB.

## 2. Exchange Student Numbers and Balance

Both universities may send qualified full time undergraduate and/or graduate students to exchange annually. Up to 2 for one academic year or 2 for one semester are accepted. Special permission is given by mutual agreement between two institutions.

## 3. Exchange Period

The normal period of exchange for each exchange student shall be one (1) or two (2) consecutive semesters with the consent of both universities.

## 4. Admission Requirements

Exchange candidates must have completed at least one (1) academic year of study at the home university prior to the exchange period, with an overall GPA of 2.5 or higher out of a 4.0 grade scale. Each party will notify the other as quickly as possible if any changes are made to student exchange admission requirements. While it is understood that exchange candidates nominated by the home university will normally be accepted by the host university, the host university reserves the right to review the applications of nominees and make final decisions concerning their admission.

## 5. Language Requirements

Nominees must obtain a TOEFL score of 500 and present proof of the score when applying. Each party will notify the other as quickly as possible if any changes are made to language requirements.

## 6. Application Documents

YZU will forward the appropriate application documents to International Relation Office (IRO) of UIB. and IRO of UIB will inform YZU of its final admission decision within thirty (30) days after the application deadline. IRO of UIB will forward the appropriate application documents to YZU and YZU will inform IRO of UIB of its final admission decision within thirty (30) days after the application deadline.

## 7. Visa

The host university will issue appropriate documents to accepted students for visa purposes in accordance with their respective applicable laws and regulations. It is the responsibility of the individual

exchange student to obtain a visa in a timely manner.

## 8. Courses

Exchange students will be able to take courses regularly offered at the host university and will have all the rights and privileges enjoyed by other students at that university.

## 9. Credit Transfer

Any academic credit received during the course of the program may be transferred to the home university in accordance with the appropriate regulations of the home university. The host university will inform the home university of exchange students’ grade information in a timely manner upon the request of the exchange student or the home university.

## 10. Tuition and Fees

Exchange students will pay normal tuition and fees to their home university in accordance with the policies of that university. The host university will not charge the home university or the exchange students for application, admission fees and tuition. Among exchange students, two of them from the host university (UIB or YZU) will have tuition free for one-semester at the partner university (YZU or UIB), and the rest exchange students will pay regular tuition.

## 11. Miscellaneous Fees

Exchange students will be responsible for making their international travel arrangements and shall bear all associated costs, including but not limited to, room, board, study materials, local transportation, student health insurance and medical care, personal expenses, and passport/visa application fees. Regarding payment of these expenses, the home university, together with the host university, shall assume responsibility for providing guidance and dealing with situations that may arise so as not to cause undue inconvenience to third parties when exchange students fall into arrears.

## 12. Liability

During their time at the host university, exchange students will be individually liable for any and all incidents, accidents and damage to third parties resulting from the students’ actions where these actions occur in situations beyond the control of the host university and home university. Both parties will not hold each other liable for such incidents.

## 13. Insurance

It is mandatory for exchange students from both institutes to purchase overseas travel insurance before departure.

## 14. Accommodation

The host university will assume responsibility for finding suitable accommodation for exchange students. The cost of the accommodation shall be borne by the individual exchange student and shall not exceed the amount paid by students at the home university in the case that the student is living in a university dormitory or other university affiliated accommodations.

## 15. Student Support

The host university will make every effort to facilitate integration of exchange students into the student life of the host university. The host university will strive to protect the privacy of the student as well as their personal safety in addition to offering orientation sessions and a sufficient support network for everyday life.

## 16. Emergency Procedures

Both the host university and the home university shall provide the other with emergency contact information and will be responsible for carrying out emergency notification in the event of student illness, injury, or other emergencies.

## 17. Appropriate Use of Personal Information

When obtaining personal information from the exchange student, it is necessary for both the host university and the home university to inform the student ahead of time of the purposes for its use and to

keep its use to a minimum. Personal information must be managed carefully to prevent its spread to a third party or use for purposes unauthorized by the exchange student.

## 18. Rules and Regulations

Exchange students shall be subject to the rules and regulations of the host university. Each university reserves the right to dismiss any exchange student at any time for conduct in violation of their established rules and regulations. However, the dismissal of an exchange student shall not adversely affect the agreement and the arrangements regarding other exchange students.

## 19. Length of Stay

Upon completion of the program at the host university, exchange students are required to return to the home university. No extension of stay will be authorized unless otherwise specifically agreed upon by both universities.

## 20. Negotiations

Both parties will strive to resolve any misunderstandings out of or in relation to this agreement that arise in a sincere and respectful manner.

## 21. Agreement Period

This Agreement shall remain in effect from the date of execution for an initial period of five (5) years. Unless either party notifies the other in writing to terminate the Agreement at least six (6) months prior to the date of expiration, the Agreement will be automatically renewed and extended for another five (5) years from the date of expiration. In the case of termination, both universities shall make necessary arrangements to ensure the appropriate completion of ongoing exchanges. This Agreement may be amended by mutual written consent the two of universities through consultation.
