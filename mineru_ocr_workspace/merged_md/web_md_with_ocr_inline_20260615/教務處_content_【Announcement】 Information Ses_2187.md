The Advantages of Interdisciplinary Learning through Minors and Double Majors at Yuan Ze University

Yuan Ze University"s unique minors and double majors provide students with opportunities to explore multiple disciplines, emphasizing not only academic depth but also fostering comprehensive skills, innovative thinking, and interdisciplinary abilities.

By exposing students to knowledge from various fields, it enhances their innovation capabilities and problem-solving skills, making them more competitive and adaptable. It also broadens their global perspective and cultural understanding, fostering diverse talents.

We hope that more students can participate in these enriching and challenging academic experiences, exploring the limitless possibilities of knowledge.

# Yuan-Ze University Regulations for Students Taking Minor

Passed by the 9th Academic Affairs Meeting, Academic Year 1994, on March 22,1995

Article 1 These regulations are provided pursuant to Article 25 of University Act and Article 30 of Yuan Ze University Study Regulations.

Article 2 The university’s undergraduate students may apply for a minor study at a different department beginning from their second semester of study; however,each departments can be the minor for each other.

Article 3 Students seeking to pursue a minor shall file an application with their department within the application deadline specified for each semester. Those found eligible to pursue a minor shall have their applications forwarded to the department chairman in which the student seeks to pursue a minor for initial approval and to Academic Dean for final approval.

Article 4 The course requirements for all minors offered at the university shall comply with the respective course requirement lists for the respective departments. In addition,the subjects and number of credits to be studied are determined by each department offering a minor,and courses of at least 20 credits must be taken.

Article 5 Credit hours of the minor shall be completed in addition to the minimum credits required for graduation by the original department.

Article 6 The credits and the academic scores of the courses completed by students taking a minor, both in the original major and in the minor, must be merged and computed on the whole; relevant matters shall be processed in accordance with the university’s study regulations as well.

Article 7 Students taking a minor may include the name of the minor they have completed on the certificate of study while transferring to other universities.

Article 8 After the completion of all course and credit requirements for the minor, students’ diploma shall be noted with the department name of the minor. Students’ diploma shall not be noted with the department name of the minor if they do not complete the course and credit requirements for their

minor, and they shall not request for additional extension of study beyond the limited period of extension to finish these requirements. For students who entered in the second semester of the academic year 1982, those who have completed or intend to pursue a minor shall follow the old regulations. The accumulated extension of the study period shall not exceed three years (including three years). Article 9 Students who have not completed the course and credit requirements for their minor within the required period of study shall not request for any certificate or proof relating to the minor study. Article 10 Students pursuing a minor that requires the university to offer additional classes within the required period of study shall pay tuition according to the number of credit hours. Students applying for an extended term of study in order to pursue a minor shall pay the credit fee only for courses under 9 credits but full tuition and fees for courses of 10 or more credits. Article 11 These regulations will be adopted by the Academic Affairs Meeting before implementation, as shall amendments when they are made.

If any controversies or disputes occurred regarding clauses of the contract, it shall always refer to its Chinese version.

# Yuan-Ze University Regulations for Students Taking Double Major

Passed by the 9th Academic Affairs Meeting, Academic Year 1994, on March 22, 1995   
Approved for future reference by Ministry of Education in the Letter Tai-(84)-Kao-Tzu No.017868, on April 20, 1995   
Amended by the 4th Academic Affairs Meeting, Academic Year 1998, on February 04, 1999   
Approved for future reference by Ministry of Education in the Letter Tai-(88)-Kao-2-Tzu No.88066422, on June 09, 1999   
Amended by the 1st Academic Affairs Meeting, Academic Year 1999, on September 01, 1999   
Approved for future reference by Ministry of Education in the Letter Tai-(88)-Kao-2-Tzu No.88112982, on September 17, 1999   
Amended by the 5th Academic Affairs Meeting, Academic Year 2004, on June 19, 2005   
Amended by the 5th Academic Affairs Meeting, Academic Year 2006, on April 25, 2007   
Approved for future reference by Ministry of Education in the Letter Tai-Kao-2-Tzu No.0960114336, on August 08, 2007   
Amended by the 3th Academic Affairs Meeting, Academic Year 2014, on January 14, 2015   
Approved for future reference by Ministry of Education in the Letter Tai-Kao-2-Tzu No. 1040032005, on March 16, 2015   
Amended by the 2nd Academic Affairs Meeting, Academic Year 2020, on November 11, 2020   
Approved for future reference by Ministry of Education in the Letter Tai-Kao-2-Tzu No. 1090186176, on December 30, 2020   
Amended by the 3th Academic Affairs Meeting, Academic Year 2021, on January 05, 2022   
Approved for future reference by Ministry of Education in the Letter Tai-Kao-2-Tzu No. 1110002661, on January 19, 2022   
Amended by the 4th Academic Affairs Meeting, Academic Year 2022, on March 01, 2023   
Approved for future reference by Ministry of Education in the Letter Tai-Kao-2-Tzu No. 1120053229, on June 17, 2023

Article 1 These regulations are provided pursuant to Article 28 of University Act, Article 25 of University Act and its EnforcementRules, Article 14 of Degree Conferral Law, and Article 30 of the University’s Study Regulations.

Article 2 The University’s undergraduate students may apply for a double major of different nature beginning from their second semester of study until the first semester of their final year of study (including extension of study). The University’s master and doctoral students may apply to take the second major beginning from their second semester of study until the first semester of their final year of study (including extension of study). Students undertaking a master program shall not be allowed to double major in an on-job master program.

Article 3 Students who intend to apply for a double major shall submit their applications to both the original department (graduate institute or degree program) and the department (graduate institute or degree program) of the second major within the application period prescribed on the school calendar. After being approved by the chairmen of both departments, the applications will be submitted to Office of Academic Affairs for final approval.

Article 4 In addition to the minimum course credit requirements for graduation designated by the original department, undergraduate students undertaking a double major shall complete all the required courses designated by the department of the second major, to be conferred along with a bachelor’s degree for each program.

In addition to the minimum course credit requirements for graduation designated by the original program, master and doctoral students undertaking a double major of other departments (degree programs) shall submit one completed degree thesis/dissertation to each program respectively. The theses/dissertations shall differ from each other in terms of title and contents. If the department of the second major has a separate “Joint Supervising of Thesis/Dissertation

Agreement” with the original department, after jointly handling the degree examination and graduating with the double-major qualification with a joint thesis, the restrictions in the preceding paragraph will not apply.

Even if both programs have established their specific requirements for graduation or qualifying regulations, students undertaking a double major shall comply with the preceding rules for course credit completion and thesis/dissertation submission. The department of the second major may designate required courses or the minimum credits for graduation, which shall be more than two-thirds of the course credits required for graduation designated for the original major.

Each department (degree program) shall establish the regulations for taking a double major, which shall be approved by department/college meetings and the Curriculum Committee of School before taking effect.

Article 5 In cases where courses in the two departments are substantially similar, it is up to the department of the second major to decide whether such courses taken in the original major can be recognized and counted as courses of the same nature for the second major. If the completed required course or general education course in the second major is similar to that of the original major, the original major may decide to accept that course as an elective to fulfill credit requirement for graduation stipulated by the original department.

Article 6 If the second major puts restrictions on the order of taking courses, students shall comply with all the regulations in completing the requirements. The course credits earned from the departments of both majors shall be jointly calculated, which shall not exceed the maximum or below the minimum credits required for each semester.

Article 7 Courses, credits, and grades for courses in both majors shall be jointly calculated each semester. Course credits of the second major shall be counted into the total credits of a semester, total credits of passed courses, academic average scores, and credits of failed courses reaching the standard for being dismissed by the University.

Article 8 Undergraduate students undertaking a double major who have completed the course and credit requirements for their original major but have not yet completed those for the second major after two years of their extended study ends may request additional extension of study again for one more year or relinquish the pursuit of double major and graduate with their original major. However, students who have completed the course and credit requirements for the second major but have failed to complete those for their original major within the two years of their extended study shall be dismissed by the University and their graduation qualifications for the second major shall not be recognized.

Master and doctoral students undertaking a double major who have completed the course and credit requirements for the original major (department, degree program) but have not yet completed those for the second major after their extended study ends, may request additional extension of study again for one more year for master students and two years for doctoral students. Students who relinquish the pursuit of double major shall graduate with their original major; they are not allowed to complete their double major program after graduation or be issued

any double major program-related certificate.

Article 9 Students taking double major may include the name of the double major they have completed on the certificate of study while transferring to other universities.

Article 10 After the completion of all course and credit requirements for both the original and second majors, the student’s graduation record, transcripts, and diploma shall be recorded with the name of both departments. Students who have not completed the course and credit requirements for their second major but have met the credit requirements of a minor program, the student’s graduation record, transcripts, and diploma shall be recorded with the department name of the minor.

Article 11 Students undertaking a double major program that requires the University to offer additional classes within the required period of study shall pay credit fees according to the number of credit hours. Students applying for an extended study to complete their double major studies shall pay credit fees for enrolled courses with no more than nine credits, or pay full tuition for enrolled courses with more than ten credits

Article 12 Matters not covered by the University's Study Regulations shall be conducted in accordance with “University Act and its Enforcement Rules”, “Degree Conferral Law”, and “Yuan Ze University Study Regulations”.

Article 13 These regulations will be adopted by the Academic Affairs Meeting for implementation, as shall amendments when theyare made.

The English translation is for reference only. In case of any discrepancy between Chinese version and English version, the Chinese version shall prevail.
