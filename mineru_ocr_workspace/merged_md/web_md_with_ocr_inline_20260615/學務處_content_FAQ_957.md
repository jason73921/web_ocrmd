- **文章簡介**: 1. Is the dormitory bed-space guaranteed for all freshmen?

a. Priority is first given to freshmen whose household are not registered in Taoyuan City, followed by the distance between their household and YZU. Due to the limited space, bed-spaces are not guaranteed for all freshmen.

b. If the bed-spaces are insufficient to freshmen whose household are registered in Taoyuan City, it will be allocated by the Student Housing Service Section (SHSS) based on the distance between students’ household and YZU and whether it is convenient when travel to YZU.

 

2. What should freshmen who aren’t allocated bed-spaces do?

a. Apply for reserve bed-space from SHSS. Freshmen will be informed when vacancy is available, and can move in once the application is done and the accommodation bill is paid.

b. Check https://house.nfu.edu.tw/YZU to search for nearby accommodation.

4. Can sophomores or above continue to stay in the dorm?

Sophomores or above have to participate in the bed-space draw. For more information about the procedures and time of the draw, pay attention to the announcement by the Student Housing Service Section. The application for participating in the draw usually starts around March - April in the spring semester.

8. Where is the Office of Student Housing Service Section located?

9. I have paid the NT$ 2,000 deposit. When can I get it back?

Paying the dormitory deposit means that students will stay in the dorm for the whole academic year (2 semesters). The deposit will only be returned after a student has been staying in the dorm for 1 academic year (two semesters) and has decided not to stay in the dorm anymore.  Otherwise, the deposit will not be refunded. However, under circumstances such as graduation, suspension, transfer to another school, dropping-out, long-term off-campus internship, participating in exchange program, students may apply for a refund by bringing the receipt to the Student Housing Service Section.

13. How do I request a room change?

Room change requests generally are not accepted. Please inform the resident assistant on the floor of your room to solve any problem before the SHSS evaluates and make the final decision regarding your request.

 

14. My Student ID card is lost or malfunctioned, and I am unable to scan for the lift or the entrance to the dorm. What should I do?

You may borrow a temporary card for 2 weeks from SHSS after signing a letter of consent and paying a deposit of NT$ 500. The deposit will be returned to you after the written consent and the card has been returned.

 

15. Due to some circumstances, I plan to move out of the dorm. How may I apply for dormitory withdrawal? Can I get any refund?

[Dormitory Withdrawal-Personal Reasons]

If you do not wish to stay in the dorm due to personal reasons except graduation, suspension, transfer to another school, dropping-out, long-term off-campus internship, participating in exchange program, etc., the paid deposit and accommodation fee would be non-refundable. You need to: 
(1) Fill in and sign the voluntary dormitory withdrawal declaration form at SHSS 
(2) Return the key to the front desk and sign the check-out form in the front desk.

[Dormitory Withdrawal-Legitimate Reasons]

Students having legitimate reasons, such as graduation, suspension, transfer to another school, drop-out, long-term off-campus internship, or participating in an exchange program, may get a refund for the deposit and part of the accommodation fees. The amount of accommodation fees refunded is based on the formula set by the Ministry of Education. 

The procedure for applying for a refund is as follows:
(1) Fill in and sign the voluntary dormitory withdrawal declaration and accommodation fee refund forms at SHSS along with relevant documents and the receipt for deposit. 
(2) Return the key to the front desk in the dorm and sign the check-out form in the front desk.

 

16. Can I only live in a dormitory for one semester?

No!  If you want to live in a dormitory, you must live in a dormitory for one academic year (two semester).

19. Do Master & PHD students stay at the off-campus graduate dormitory?

No. Female Master & PHD students live in Female Dorm #1 (4-bed room) and Master & PHD graduate students live in Male Dorm #1 (4-bed room). 

 

20. I am a new graduate student. May I apply for short-term accommodation during summer break before I successfully apply for a dorm bed-space?

Yes. You may apply for a short-term stay in the dorm during summer break at SHSS. Please pay attention to the announcement. 

 

21. Important notice 

(1) High-power electrical appliances such as microwaves, induction cookers, etc., are prohibited in the dormitory. Pets are also not allowed.

(2) Pets are not allowed in the dorm.

(3) Smoking, excessive noise, fighting, drug use, and bringing anyone of the opposite sex into the dormitory are strictly prohibited.

(4) Hair dryers are not allowed to be used in the dorm rooms to prevent power outages caused by electrical overload.

(5) Residents are responsible for keeping the dorm rooms clean.

(6) There is no Wi-Fi or wireless internet connection available in the dorm rooms. A cable is needed for using the internet and can be purchased at the Family Mart located in the dormitory area. To connect, plug one end of the LAN cable into the internet port located in front of the desk, and the other end into your computer. After a few moments, the internet connection should be established automatically.

(7) The prepaid card for air conditioners can be purchased at the Family Mart in the dormitory area.

(8) Check-in: New students should check in at the 1st floor of their assigned dormitory. During the semester, all check-in procedures should be conducted at the Management Center (1st floor of Female Dorm 1).

(9) Check-out: Please follow the official procedure and return your room key to avoid deductions from your dormitory deposit.

(10) For any dormitory-related issues, please contact the Floor Leader(s) on your floor or Counselors at the Management Center (Female Dorm 1,1F) for assistance.



22.    When is the time for dorm room draw?

The dorm room draw will be hold in the Spring semester. Those who are selected can live in the dorm for a year after paying the NT$2,000 deposit and signing YZU Student Hosuing Agreement.
