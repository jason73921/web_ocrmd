```markdown
## Yuan Ze University Spokesperson System Regulations

Approved by the 6th Executive Council Meeting of the 1998 Academic Year on November 30, 1998   
Amended by the 19th Executive Council Meeting of the 2002 Academic Year on July 14, 2003

Article 1 These Yuan Ze University Spokesperson System Regulations (hereinafter referred to as the "Regulations") are established to promptly and appropriately address significant and urgent matters within or outside the campus and to represent the University’s position officially.

Article 2 The statutory spokesperson shall represent the University in expressing official opinions and principles of action, with the objectives of disseminating key information and clarifying internal and external concerns.

Article 3 The statutory spokesperson, who represents the University in official communications, shall be the Vice President. The designated proxy for the statutory spokesperson shall be the Secretary General.

Article 4 Supervisors of relevant units shall cooperate with the statutory spokesperson to execute the key points of announcements and provide detailed supplementary explanations regarding their respective operations as outlined below.

1. Meeting information, except for the University Affairs Meeting and Executive Council Meetings compiled by the Secretariat Office, shall be promptly provided by the convening units for timely external dissemination by the spokesperson.

The respective organizing or responsible units shall proactively provide relevant information to the spokesperson.

2. Student Organization Information: The Office of Student Affairs shall coordinate and provide information on student activities to the spokesperson on time.

3. Emergency Information: In the event of any significant or urgent incident on campus, the responsible unit shall immediately report the matter verbally to the spokesperson.

4. For any specific information or achievements within the University, the respective unit shall proactively provide them to the spokesperson.

Article 5 Opinions expressed by individuals other than the statutory spokesperson of the University shall be deemed personal opinions for which the University assumes no responsibility.

Article 6 The Public Affairs and Alumni Service Center handles media relations and inquiries, coordinates communications among the media, the statutory spokesperson, executive units, and relevant personnel, and disseminates related news.

Article 7 These Regulations take effect upon approval by the Executive Council Meeting and ratification by the University President. The same procedure applies to any amendments.

In case of any discrepancy between the Chinese and English versions, the Chinese version shall prevail.
```

```markdown
# Yuan Ze University Educational Resource Development Fund Management Regulations

Approved by the 10th Executive Council Meeting of the 2000 Academic Year on May 14, 2001   
Amended and Approved by the 19th Executive Council Meeting of the 2001 Academic Year on June 10, 2002   
Amended and Approved by the 7th Executive Council Meeting of the 2013 Academic Year on October 30, 2013   
Amended and Approved by the 5th Executive Council Meeting of the 2017 Academic Year on October 11, 2017   
Amended and Approved by the 14th Executive Council Meeting of the 2019 Academic Year on February 12, 2020   
Amended and Approved by the 24th Executive Council Meeting of the 2021 Academic Year on June 8, 2022

## Article 1 Purpose

These Regulations are formulated to appropriately manage and utilize donations to promote the development of the University’s affairs.

## Article 2 Authority and Responsibilities

A committee of 7-15 members shall be established to handle, supervise, and approve various donation-related matters. The President of the university shall serve as the Chairperson of the committee.

## Article 3 Types of Donations Received

1.University Development Fund (including donations with no specified unit or purpose).

2.Development funds for colleges and departments (institutes).

3.Designated donations for administrative units.

4. Scholarships.

5. Financial aid for underprivileged students.

6. Other designated-purpose funds.

7. Other in-kind donations, securities, or assets.

## Article 4 Management Principles

All donations shall be deposited into the Educational Resource Development Fund’s dedicated account. The Accounting Office shall compile statistics on the income, expenditures, and balance of donations received by each unit for the previous academic year. Each recipient unit must submit an annual expenditure report for review by the committee and appropriate public disclosure.

1. The committee shall manage donations with no specified purpose. Donations with specified purposes shall be used accordingly, provided such use aligns with university affairs and does not violate academic ethics, harm the university’s reputation, or serve illegal purposes.

2.When donations are designated for individual teaching or research purposes, the donor (including the responsible person if the donor is a legal entity) and the recipient (including individuals, supervisors, project leaders, or researchers) must not be the same person, spouses, or relatives within the second degree of kinship.

3.Donations must not be associated with improper benefits to the donor.

Recipient units must establish guidelines for managing and using donations and inform the Public Affairs and Alumni Services Center. Expenditures shall follow internal accounting procedures. If no guidelines are established, the use of donations must be approved by the college or department affair meeting.

## Article 5 Utilization Principles for Non-Designated Donations

Non-designated donations shall be utilized by the university as follows:

1. Establish academic chairs.

2. Establish student scholarships and financial aid.

3. Support teaching and research activities.

4. Support internationalization efforts.

5. Fund lectures, seminars, and academic exchanges.

6. Renovate teaching and research buildings.

7. Purchase equipment, instruments, or supplies.

8. Support administrative expenses related to university affairs.

9. Fund expenses related to university development.

10. Cover expenses for fundraising and related activities.

Expenditures exceeding NT\$1 million must be approved by at least two-thirds of the attending committee members. For projects under NT\$1 million with time constraints, the Chairperson may approve the expenditure, subject to ratification by the committee at its next meeting.

## Article 6 Utilization Principles for Designated Donations

Designated donations may be reallocated under the following circumstances, with committee approval:

1. The original purpose has been achieved, or the purpose no longer exists.

2. The designated funds remain unused for over three years.

3. Donated in-kind items no longer serve their original purpose.

Any other changes to the designated purpose require the donor’s written consent.

## Article 7 Donations Utilizing Interest

If donations are to be utilized based on interest generated, the principles outlined in Article 6 shall apply.

## Article 8 Management of Annual Surplus Funds

1. Surplus from non-designated donations shall be managed by the Chairperson in accordance with the needs of university development, referencing Article 46 of the Private School Act.

2. Surplus from designated donations shall be carried forward and used according to the original purpose.

3. If the remaining balance of a designated donation is less than NT\$10,000, the executing unit may utilize it according to Article 6.

## Article 9 Disposal of In-Kind Donations

The disposal of non-cash donations (including movable and immovable property and securities) shall be conducted in accordance with the university’s Property Management Regulations and relevant provisions of the Private School Act.

## Article 10 Incentive Measures

When a recipient unit’s cash donations for an academic year total NT\$1 million or more, and the donations are designated for university development, the university shall allocate 10% of the donation amount as an incentive for the unit. After the incentive is allocated, the fundraising total is reset.

Article 11 Donation Receipts and Disclosure The University shall issue receipts for donations for tax purposes. Donor names and donation amounts shall be published periodically on the University’s website. Donation records and receipts shall be sequentially numbered and managed by designated personnel.

Article 12 Donor Appreciation Donor appreciation measures shall be conducted in accordance with the Yuan Ze University Donor Appreciation Regulations.

Article 13 Implementation These Regulations shall take effect upon approval by the Executive Council Meeting. Amendments shall follow the same process.

In case of any discrepancy between the Chinese and English versions, the Chinese version shall prevail.
```

```markdown
# Yuan Ze University Donor Appreciation Regulations

Approved by the 40th Executive Council Meeting of the1993 Academic Year on May 30, 1994

Amended and Approved by the 5th Executive Council Meeting of the 1996 Academic Year on December 16, 1996

Amended and Approved by the 1st Fundraising Committee Meeting of the 1996 Academic Year on March 24, 1997

Amended and Approved by the 5th Executive Council Meeting of the 1997 Academic Year on November 2, 1998

Amended and Approved by the 3rd School Development Fund Committee Meeting of the 1998 Academic Year on April 21, 1999

Amended and Approved by the 12th Executive Council Meeting of the1998 Academic Year on April 26, 1999

Amended and Approved by the 8th Meeting of the 4th Board of Directors on April 29, 1999

Amended and Approved by the 14th Executive Council Meeting of the 2017 Academic Year on March 7, 2018

Amended and Approved by the 5th Meeting of the 10th Board of Directors on May 30, 2018

Article 1 These Regulations are formulated to express gratitude to donors who contribute to the University's development.

Article 2 The names of donors or donor organizations and donation details shall be published regularly on the University's website and relevant publications. A letter of appreciation will be issued to donors.

Article 3 A record of all donations shall be displayed in the University History Galley. Additionally, an annual donor honor roll shall be published. For individual donations exceeding NT\$1 million, the donor's name shall be permanently listed.

Article 4 Donors shall be thanked according to the following methods:

1. All donors shall be acknowledged on the University's website as part of the donor list.

2. Donors contributing NT\$10,000 or more shall receive a letter of appreciation from the President.

3. Donors contributing NT\$100,000 or more shall receive a certificate of appreciation and a letter from the President.

4. Donors contributing NT\$1 million or more shall receive a commemorative plaque and a letter of appreciation from the President.

5. Donors contributing NT\$3 million or more shall receive a commemorative plaque and a letter of appreciation from the Chairperson of the Board.

6. Donors contributing NT\$10 million or more shall receive a commemorative plaque, a letter of appreciation from the Chairperson of the Board, and a commemorative plaque displayed in the University History Galley and honored as Distinguished Alumni.

7. Donors contributing NT\$30 million or more shall receive special recognition under a dedicated plan.

Article 5 Donors contributing to the construction of buildings or facilities shall be honored as Distinguished Alumni and may have the building or facility named after them for perpetuity. The naming of such buildings or facilities shall be approved by the University Council and the Board of trustee. The specific donation amount required for naming rights shall be determined separately.

Article 6 Donors shall receive recognition for cumulative donations that meet the thresholds specified in the preceding articles by these Regulations.

Article 7 Donors contributing equipment related to teaching or research shall have their donation acknowledged by a label on the donated equipment. Recognition shall be given according to the value of the donation as specified in these Regulations. If the donation meets the reward standards under the Ministry of Education's Regulations on Rewards for Educational Donations, the University shall report it to the government for commendation.

Article 8 Donor recognition shall respect the donor's wishes. If a donor prefers anonymity, the University shall maintain strict confidentiality.

Article 9 These Regulations shall take effect upon, approval by the Executive Council Meeting, and ratification by the Chairperson of the Board. The same procedure applies to amendments.
```

```markdown
# Yuan Ze University Regulations Governing the Selection of Outstanding Alumni

Approved by the 6th Executive Council Meeting of the 2008 Academic Year on November 24, 2008.   
Amended and Approved at the 11th Executive Council Meeting of the 2013 Academic Year on December 25, 2013   
Amended and Approved at the 10th Executive Council Meeting of the 2017 Academic Year on December 20, 2017   
Amended and Approved at the 5th Executive Council Meeting of the 2018 Academic Year on October 17, 2018   
Amended and Approved at the 5th Executive Council Meeting of the 2022 Academic Year on September 28, 2022

Article 1 The "Yuan Ze University Regulations Governing the Selection of Outstanding Alumni" (hereinafter referred to as "the Regulations") are enacted to recognize outstanding alumni for their contributions to the University and society, enhancing the University's reputation and inspiring future generations.

## Article 2 Graduates or attendees of the University meeting any of the following qualifications are eligible for nomination as Outstanding Alumni candidates:

1. Exceptional achievements in their profession or business.

2. Significant contributions to society.

3. Major contributions to the construction or development of this University.

## Article 3 Nomination Methods:

1. Nominations by the Alumni Association, regional alumni associations, or departmental alumni associations.

2. Nominations by colleges or departments (institutes).

3. Nominations by members of the Selection Committee.

4. Nominations jointly endorsed by five or more alumni.

## Article 4 Selection Procedure:

1. The "Outstanding Alumni Selection Committee" of 9 - 15 members are appointed by the President, comprising internal and external individuals and alumni representatives. The Vice President serve as the Chairperson. Any Selection Committee member nominated as a candidate for that year shall recuse themselves from the process.

2. The selection requires a quorum of over half the committee members, with candidates needing two-thirds approval from those present to be recognized as Outstanding Alumni.

3. No more than five alumni may be selected as Outstanding Alumni each year.

## Article 5 The selection committee will announce the schedule for the selection process. Nominating units must submit nomination forms within the designated timeframe for processing by the Selection Committee. The list of candidates and the nomination and evaluation process shall remain confidential.

Article 6 Outstanding Alumni shall be publicly honored by the President at significant University events, where they will be presented with an Outstanding Alumni award and certificate. Their achievements shall be published in the university’s publications and on social media platforms to highlight their contributions.

Article 7 Outstanding Alumni are expected to actively promote the University’s educational philosophy and achievements and contribute to its advancement.

Article 8 An Outstanding Alumni who engages in conduct detrimental to the reputation of the University may have their status revoked upon resolution by the Selection Committee.

Article 9 These Regulations take effect upon approval by the Executive Council Meeting. The same procedure applies to any amendments.

In case of any discrepancy between the Chinese and English versions, the Chinese version shall prevail.
```

```markdown
# Yuan Ze University Intellectual Property Rights Protection and Implementation Team Establishment Guidelines

Approved by the 3rd Executive Council Meeting of the 2010 Academic Year on October 4, 2010

## Article 1 Purpose

To effectively promote and actively advocate measures for the protection of intellectual property rights and to enhance the awareness of respecting intellectual property rights, the university has established the "Intellectual Property Rights Promotion and Implementation Team" (hereinafter referred to as the Team).

## Article 2 Organization

The Team consists of the University President, Vice President, Deans of Academic Affairs, Student Affairs, General Affairs, Research and Development, Library and Information Services, Secretary General, Director of the Personnel Office, Director of the Public Affairs and Alumni Service Center, Deans of all colleges, Director of the College of General Studies Department, and one representative from the Student Association.

The University President serves as the convener, presiding over meetings and supervising the implementation of annual plans. The Secretary General concurrently serves as the Executive Secretary, handling administrative tasks.

## Article 3 Duties

1. Plan and promote courses and activities related to intellectual property rights protection.

2. Ensure the lawful use of campus networks, software, and various types of works.

3. Establish principles and standard operating procedures for respecting and protecting intellectual property rights.

4. Assist university faculty, staff, and students in resolving intellectual property rights disputes.

5. Plan and promote other measures related to the protection of intellectual property rights.

## Article 4 Meetings

1. The Team holds regular meetings once each semester and may convene interim meetings as needed, inviting relevant units to attend.

2. If the convener cannot attend, they may designate a committee member to preside over the meeting.

3. Meetings require the attendance of more than half of the members to proceed, and resolutions must be approved by more than half of the attending members.

Article 5 These guidelines take effect upon approval by the Executive Council Meeting. The same procedure applies to any amendments.

In case of any discrepancy between the Chinese and English versions, the Chinese version shall prevail.
```

```markdown
# Yuan Ze University Gender Equity Education Committee Establishment Guidelines

Amended and approved by the 5th Executive Council Meeting of the 2005 Academic Year on November 7, 2005   
Amended and approved by the 3rd Executive Council Meeting of the 2007 Academic Year on October 8, 2007   
Amended and approved by the 18th Executive Council Meeting of the 2008 Academic Year on July 13, 2009   
Amended and approved by the 2nd Executive Council Meeting of the 2010 Academic Year on September 20, 2010   
Approved by the 6th Executive Council Meeting of the 2012 Academic Year on October 31, 2012   
Approved by the 1st University Affairs Meeting of the 2012 Academic Year on November 14, 2012   
Approved by the 1st University Affairs Meeting of the 2024 Academic Year on October 30, 2024

Article 1 To promote substantive gender equality, eliminate gender discrimination, uphold personal dignity, and strengthen and establish resources and environments for gender equality education at this University, the “Guidelines for the Establishment of the Gender Equality Education Committee” (hereinafter referred to as "These Guidelines") are formulated by Article 6 of the Gender Equality Education Act.

Article 2 The tasks of the Committee are as follows:

1. Integrate resources from various units within the University, draft and implement gender equity education plans, and evaluate their outcomes.

2. Plan or organize activities related to gender equity education for students, faculty, staff, and parents.

3. Develop and promote courses, teaching methods, and assessments on gender equity education.

4. Formulate regulations for the implementation of gender equity education and the prevention of gender-related incidents on campus, establish mechanisms, and coordinate and integrate relevant resources.

5. Investigate and address cases related to the Gender Equity Education Act.

6. Plan and establish a safe, gender-equal campus environment.

7. Promote family and community education on gender equity.

8. Handle other matters related to gender equity education at the University or in the community.

Article 3 The Committee of 17-19 members includes the President as the Chairperson, Deans of Academic Affairs, Student Affairs, and General Affairs, the Secretary General, and the Director of Personnel as ex-officio members. Representatives: one faculty member from each college and the General Education Center, two staff representatives elected by a university-wide vote, two student representatives selected by the Student Association, one parent representative, experts, and scholars in gender equity education fields may also be appointed as members. At least half of the Committee members must be women, and all members must have gender equity awareness and be free of any genderdiscriminatory behavior.

Members serve a two-year term and may be reappointed once.

The Committee have an Executive Secretary responsible for administrative tasks concurrently held by the Secretary General.

## Article 4

The Committee shall convene at least once per semester, with meetings requiring the attendance of at least half the members. Special meetings may be held as necessary.

Article 5 To implement tasks related to gender equality education, this Committee is organized into the following groups according to mission needs:

1. Policy Planning Group: Convened by the Secretary General, with the Secretariat Office as the primary responsible unit. Its tasks are as follows:

(1) Integrate resources from various university units, draft and implement the gender equality education plan, and review its implementation results.

(2) Develop regulations for implementing gender equality education and preventing campus sexual assault and harassment, establish mechanisms, coordinate and integrate relevant resources, and handle administrative matters related to cases governed by this act.

2. Curriculum and Instruction Group: Convened by the Dean of Academic Affairs, with the Office of Academic Affairs and College of General Studies as the primary responsible units. Its tasks are as follows:

(1) Coordinate, develop, and implement research on courses and teaching for gender equality education.

3. Environment and Resources Group: Convened by the Dean of General Affairs, with the Office of General Affairs as the primary responsible unit. Its task is to plan, maintain, and establish a gender-equal, friendly, and safe campus environment.

4. Guidance and Promotion Group: Convened by the Dean of Student Affairs, with the Office of Student Affairs and the Personnel Office as the primary responsible units. Its tasks are as follows:

(1) Plan and organize activities related to gender equality education for students, faculty, staff, and other gender equality education matters related to the University or community.

(2) Serve as the reception point for applications or reports concerning campus gender-related incidents, providing counseling and resource referral assistance as needed.

(3) Actively promote preventive education on campus gender-related incidents to enhance the awareness and respect for others' and one's own sexual or bodily autonomy among faculty, staff, workers, and students. Organize regular annual educational campaigns on the prevention of campus gender-related incidents and evaluate their effectiveness.

The tasks and budgets for each group should be collectively planned within the gender equality education implementation plan and managed by the respective groups.

Each group must proactively plan annual work items and budgets and report their annual implementation outcomes to the Committee.

Article 6 Any matters not specified in these Guidelines shall be addressed in accordance with the regulations of the Gender Equity Education Act.
```

```markdown
3. They are graduates of the University.

2. Units with a “Conditionally Passed” evaluation result must submit a self-improvement plan within three months of the evaluation, complete the improvements within one year, and undergo a follow-up evaluation before the conditional pass period ends.

3. Units with a “Failed (re-evaluation required)” result must restart the evaluation process, re-examine the evaluation items, reorganize the data within one year, and re-evaluate after completing the improvements.

4. Improvement plans and implementation outcomes submitted by evaluated units are continuously tracked by various levels of selfevaluation execution committees and included in the following evaluation cycle.
```

```markdown
According to Article 46 of the Copyright Act, schools and teachers may photocopy books within a reasonable scope for use as teaching materials if needed for classroom instruction. The "reasonable scope" must be related to the teaching content and generally refers to photocopying a portion of a book. Photocopying an entire book exceeds the scope of reasonable use and constitutes copyright infringement.

2. Pursuant to Articles 48 and 51 of the Copyright Act, individuals may reproduce publicly published works using library or non-public machines for personal or non-profit family purposes, provided it is within reasonable limits. "Reasonable limits" include reproducing (photocopying) parts of publicly published works at the request of readers for personal research or single articles from journals, limited to one copy per person.

3. Under Article 65 of the Copyright Act, if teachers and students use a small portion of a copyrighted book and the usage has minimal market impact, it may be deemed reasonable use. Photocopying a part of a book for classroom use does not constitute copyright infringement. However, photocopying an entire book exceeds the reasonable use scope and constitutes copyright infringement.

4. Photocopying is a method of reproduction. Unauthorized photocopying of books that does not meet the criteria for reasonable use constitutes an act of illegal reproduction and infringement of others copyright.
```

```markdown
# Yuan Ze University Regulations for Archives Management

Approved by the 16th Executive Council Meeting of the 2015 Academic Year on April 13, 2016   
Amended and Approved by the 13th Executive Council Meeting of the 2020 Academic Year on January 27, 2021

Article 1 To establish a sound archival management system, preserve official archives, promote accessibility and utilization of archives, and enhance their functional value, these Regulations for Archives Management (hereinafter referred to as "the Regulations") are enacted.

## Article 2 Classification of Archives:

Archives are classified based on the organizational structure and the nature of operations and are categorized into class, series, and file levels.

## Article 3 Classification of Confidential Archives:

1. Confidential Archives:

(1) Base prices for equipment or engineering projects prior to finalizing budgets or awards.

(2) Reports or Petition Cases.

(3) Personnel appointment, evaluation, and salary information.

(4) Information that is legally or contractually bound to confidentiality.

(5) Other matters are designated as confidential.

2. Restricted Archives: Determined independently by the responsible unit.

3. The levels of archive confidentiality are categorized as "Top Secret," "Highly Secret," "Confidential," "Restricted," and "Ordinary." Determination, declassification, or extension of confidentiality periods shall be approved by the President (or authorized representative).

## Article 4 Retention Periods for Archives:

1. Archives are retained permanently or for a fixed period based on their nature and value, serving as guidelines for determining retention periods.

(1) Permanent Retention:

A. Establishment, adjustment, or dissolution of units.

B. Major institutional development plans and reports.

C. Resolutions and records of significant meetings.

D. Major awards and appointments of senior officials.

E. Official seal activation and related documents.

F. Presidential handover records.

G. Other documents of historical value.

(2) Fixed-Term Retention: Based on actual needs.

2. Retention periods shall comply with the University's "Archive Classification and Retention Schedule," which requires approval from the President.

## Article 5 Transmission of Confidential Documents:

Confidential documents must be delivered by the responsible staff member personally or sealed and labeled with the confidentiality level for delivery by designated personnel. Receipt records must be retained.

## Article 6 Archive Storage:

1. Archives shall be categorized and managed based on media types, including paper, photographic, video/audio tapes, and electronic media.

2. Archives must be regularly maintained to ensure document integrity.

3. Storage environments must include proper security measures (e.g., access control, fire prevention, dehumidification, and firewall systems) to safeguard archives and prevent damage.

4. Confidential archives must be stored in secure, lockable cabinets with designated personnel responsible for management.

## Article 7 Archive Clearance:

1. Each unit shall conduct archive clearance at least once annually.

2. Archive clearance procedures:

(1) Permanent Retention by Unit: Archives of permanent value not yet designated as institutional history archives shall be permanently retained by the unit.

(2) Appraisal After Expiration: If retention value is unclear, archives are tentatively assigned a retention period and appraised upon expiration for extension or disposal.

(3) Disposal by Procedure: Upon expiration, important archives shall be destroyed in the presence of relevant personnel, with complete records maintained.

(4) Transfer to University History Gallery: Archives deemed historically significant shall be transferred according to relevant procedures.
```

```markdown
# Yuan Ze University Regulations for the Collection of University Historical Archives and Artifacts

Approved by the 6th Executive Council Meeting of the 2016 Academic Year on October 19, 2016,

## Article 1

To preserve Yuan Ze University’s (hereinafter referred to as "the University") cultural heritage, document the historical evolution of the University, and systematically collect and organize various historical resources, the Regulations for the Collection of University Historical Archives and Artifacts (hereinafter referred to as "the Regulations") are enacted. This aims to enhance the sense of identity and unity among current members and alumni, and to foster awareness of the University's origins, development, and future vision.

## Article 2 Scope of Collection:

1. Historical Artifacts: Items of historical or commemorative significance, such as school emblems, flags, trophies from significant competitions, gifts from foreign visitors, and commemorative teaching instruments or equipment.

2. University Development Records: Documents related to changes in university leadership, amendments to regulations, organizational changes, establishment of departments, and institutional development plans.

3. Official Documents: Non-current official documents and manuscripts of historical value.

4. Publications:

(1) Official Publications: Brochures, university regulations, institutional development plans, and other official publications.

(2) Historical Record Publications: Commemorative publications, special issues, yearbooks, etc.

(3) Digital Media Publications: Videos of significant events, oral history interviews, past university introduction videos, and other multimedia materials.

(4) Academic Publications: Major research outcomes, journal articles.

(5) Special Publications: Newsletters for alumni or other special publications.

5. Architectural Records: Design plans, campus models, groundbreaking tools, images and records of construction completion, and documentation of campus building restorations.

6. Significant Reports and Events: Media reports highlighting university achievements, such as newspaper or magazine articles.

7. Meeting Records: Records and documents of significant university affairs and administrative management meetings.

8. Other significant historical documents from university units.

## Article 3 Collection Methods:

The University prioritizes the collection of original artifacts and employs the following methods:

1. Solicitation: Requesting donations from external units through formal correspondence.

2. Transfer: Units shall transfer eligible documents to the University History Gallery after the retention period expires at the end of each academic term.

3. Donation: Individuals are encouraged to donate items that fall within the scope of the collection.

4. Duplication: If original artifacts cannot be obtained, the university may negotiate to obtain photographic or scanned duplicates for collection and use.

5. Purchase: Artifacts of special value may be acquired through the University's procurement procedures.

For artifacts involving ownership rights, the "Yuan Ze University Historical Artifacts Authorization Form" must be completed. Donations or solicitations require completing the "Yuan Ze University Historical Artifacts Collection and Donation Agreement."
```

```markdown
## Yuan Ze University Implementation Regulations for Internal Auditing

May 9, 2018, Approved by the 2nd University Affairs Meeting of the 2017 Academic Year February 24, 2023, Amended and approved by the 3rd Internal Audit Committee Meeting of the 2022 Academic Year March 22, 2023, Amended and approved by the 16th Administrative Meeting of the 2022 Academic Year May 10, 2023, Amended and approved by the 2nd University Affairs Meeting of the 2022 Academic Year April 30, 2025, Amended and approved at the 15th Administrative Meeting of the 2024 Academic Year May 21, 2025, Amended and approved at the 2nd University Affairs Meeting of the 2024 Academic Year

Article 1 Yuan Ze University (hereinafter referred to as “the University”) establishes these Regulations to assist the President in evaluating the effectiveness of the internal control system, assessing the efficiency and effectiveness of operations, the reliability, timeliness, and transparency of reporting, and compliance with relevant laws and regulations. These Regulations aim to provide timely recommendations for improvement and ensure the continuous and effective implementation of the internal control system.

Article 2 The University shall establish an Internal Audit Committee consisting of 21 to 23 members (including one full-time audit staff member) to carry out internal audit tasks. The Committee is a specialized audit organization under the President and is composed as follows. Half of the elected members are replaced in each election cycle. Members serve a two-year term aligned with the academic year and may be reappointed. The Chairperson is elected by the Committee members.

1、 President-appointed members (7–9): Selected from current or former second-level administrators, senior faculty with audit experience, or those with backgrounds in finance, accounting, or management. 3–5 members shall be full-time faculty, and 3–5 shall be full-time technical staff.

2、 Faculty representatives (8): Nominated by academic units from full-time faculty not serving as first-level administrators and elected by all full-time faculty. Each college nominates 2 candidates; the General Education Division and Physical Education Office each nominate 1.

(1) Office of Academic Affairs, Office of Student Affairs, Office of General Affairs, Office of Library and Information Services, and each college (excluding the College of Medicine and Nursing): 2 each.

(2) Office of Research and Development, Personnel Office, General Accounting Office, and Secretariat Office: 1 each.

(3) Office of Global Affairs and Environmental Protection and Occupational Safety and Health Center: 1 combined.

(4) College of General Studies (including the International Language and Culture Center), Physical Education Office, and College of Medicine and Nursing: 1 combined.

If a member vacates their position during the term, the President shall appoint a replacement for appointed members, and elected members shall be replaced based on the next highest vote count. The replacement term shall match the original term unless the remaining term is less than four months and the vacancy does not exceed one-tenth of the total membership, in which case no replacement is made.

The Committee shall convene at least once per semester and may hold special meetings as needed. Meetings require attendance by at least two-thirds of members, and relevant units may send representatives to attend. Resolutions require approval by more than half of attending members.

The Committee shall include one full-time audit staff member and several audit clerks. Audit clerks shall be staff from the Secretariat.

## Article 3 The Committee shall conduct audits of internal controls to assess the effectiveness and compliance of policies and procedures related to personnel, finance, and operations, without infringing upon accounting responsibilities.

Members shall maintain independence and impartiality, executing duties objectively and fairly. To ensure audit objectivity, members shall not audit their own or related parties’ operations.

If a major violation or potential significant harm to the University or its legal entity is discovered, an audit report shall be immediately submitted to the President for review. Committee responsibilities include:

1 Post-audit of personnel, financial, and operational activities.

2 Post-audit of cash handling.

3 Audits and recommendations to improve efficiency and reduce uneconomical expenditures.

4 Special audit projects.

5 Review of annual audit plans and comprehensive audit reports.

Article 4 Responsibilities of the full-time audit staff include:

1. Developing audit plans based on risk assessments.

2. Conducting internal audits and preparing audit reports. Reports shall be reviewed by the President and presented at the University Affairs Meeting.

3. Disclosing deficiencies and recommendations found during audits and providing opinions, with regular follow-up until improvements are made.

4. Inventory of cash, bank deposits, and marketable securities.

5. Post-audit of transactions involving the University Fund.

6. Joint audits of projects funded by the National Science and Technology Council and other grants with Committee members.

## Article 5 The University shall establish internal audit procedures specifying audit items, timing, processes, and execution methods.

Article 7 These Regulations shall be implemented upon approval by the University Affairs Meeting. Amendments shall follow the same procedure.
```

```markdown
# Yuan Ze University Regulations for the Application and Use of Institutional Research Data

Approved by the 22nd Executive Council Meeting of the 2016 Academic Year on July 4, 2017.

Article 1 The Institutional Research Center (hereinafter referred to as "the Center") is responsible for collecting and analyzing data related to university operations, as well as enhancing the value-added application of institutional data for decision-making in order to improve academic research capabilities and support the overall development of the university. In response to the need for information transparency and to establish guidelines for the use and external publication of data, these "Regulations on the Application and Use of Institutional Research Data " (hereinafter referred to as "the Regulations") are formulated in accordance with government regulations on information disclosure and Personal Data Protection Act.

Article 2 The "institutional research data" referred to these Regulations primarily refers to data currently stored in the Institutional Research Data Platform at the Center. In principle, data related to the operations of various administrative and academic units within the university may be considered institutional research data and can be requested through the Center. Such data must undergo de-identification processing before being placed on the Institutional Research Data Platform. Access permissions will be granted to relevant personnel for utilization, and the data will be managed under applicable information security regulations.

## Article 3 Data Application Procedures

## 1.Eligibility for Application

Applicants for data must primarily be faculty or staff members of the university who are engaged in institutional research-related topics. They apply data to the Center for their research in accordance with these Regulations.

## 2.Application Procedure

To apply for the use of data required for institutional research, applicants must fill out "Yuan Ze University Institutional Research Data Usage Application Form" and submit it along with the relevant supporting documents in writing to the Center.

(1) If the requested data is already available at the Center, the applicant may use the data from the "Institutional Research Data Platform" after receiving approval from the Center within the authorized usage period.

(2) If the requested data is unavailable at the Center, the Center will obtain consent from the relevant administrative units through the "Yuan Ze University Special Use Application Form for Large-scale Personal Data." Once consent is granted, the applicant will be authorized to use the data.

(3) The Center reserves the right to review the reasons for the application and the supporting documents provided to approve or deny the application.

## Article 4 Obligations of Data Users

1.Data users must primarily be faculty or staff members of the university engaged in institutional research-related topics.

2.Users are prohibited from modifying, altering, falsifying, or concealing the data. Any such actions are the sole responsibility of the user and will be considered a violation of the regulations. In such cases, the user’s access to the data will be terminated, and in severe cases, future access privileges may be revoked.

3.Users are prohibited from re-authorizing, transferring, or otherwise allowing third parties to access or use the data. If the data is used in a way that harms the university or the rights of others, the user will be held liable for damages and other legal responsibilities.

## Article 5 Dissemination of Research Results

1.The primary use of the institutional research database is for institutional research purposes.

2.Personnel involved in the handling, processing, applying, or storing institutional research data must comply with the Personal Data Protection Act and relevant regulations and interpretations. Violations will result in civil and criminal liabilities, as well as penalties in accordance with university policies. This applies even after the individual leaves the university. Data for academic research and publication must also adhere to relevant academic and research ethics guidelines.

3.When research data users publish their research results, they must acknowledge the source of the data or credit the "Institutional Research Center of Yuan Ze University." The related research reports must be submitted to the Center for archival purposes.

Article 6 If identifiable data is discovered, it must be immediately reported to the Center, and the relevant data must not be publicly disclosed.

Article 7 If any violation of these Regulations occurs, the use of the data shall be suspended, and the matter shall be referred to the Institutional Research Advisory Committee for further action.

Article 8 If the research results derived from the use of the Center's database involve improper use or infringement of personal privacy, in addition to facing administrative penalties in accordance with relevant regulations, the matter shall be referred to the judicial authorities for investigation under the Criminal Code, the Personal Data Protection Act, and other applicable laws.

Article 9 Any matters not addressed in these Regulations shall be handled in accordance with relevant laws and regulations.

Article 10 The Regulations take effect upon approval by the Executive Council Meeting. The same procedure applies to any amendments.
```

```markdown
# Yuan Ze University Implementation Guidelines for Institutional Research

Approved by the 1st Executive Council Meeting of the 2022 Academic Year on August 3, 2022.

## Article 1

In response to the long-term development of the university and to establish a professional management framework for university operations, the university actively promotes institutional research. The goal is to provide decision-making support for university administration through empirical analysis results, thereby enhancing the overall quality of teaching and research and improving institutional governance effectiveness. To this end, these "Implementation Guidelines for Institutional Research " (hereinafter referred to as "these Guidelines") are formulated.

Article 2 To promote institutional research, the university establishes the Institutional Research Center, which is responsible for planning, coordinating, and managing the institutional research data platform. The center’s director, appointed by the university president, oversees the center’s operations. An "Institutional Research Advisory Committee" has been established to guide matters related to promoting institutional research.

Article 3 The Institutional Research Advisory Committee is convened by the Vice President as the chairperson. The Executive Secretary of the committee is the Director of the Institutional Research Center. The following individuals serve as ex officio members: Deans of Academic Affairs, Student Affairs, General Affairs, Research and Development, Library and Information Services, Dean of Global Affairs Office, Deans of each college, Secretary General, the Directors of College of General Studies, School of Lifelong Education, the General Accounting Office, Personnel Office, and Environmental Protection and Occupational Safety and Health Center. Additionally, the University President shall appoint 2 to 5 full-time faculty members within the university to join the committee, whose term is one year, with the possibility of reappointment or renewal.

Article 4 The Institutional Research Advisory Committee is convened at least once per semester. Ad hoc meetings may be convened as necessary, and relevant university personnel may be invited to attend as needed. The main responsibilities of the committee are as follows:

1. To formulate University’s policies and management guidelines related to institutional research.

2. To promote institutional research on major issues and review internal applications for funding related to institutional research projects.

3. To review the results of institutional research, compile recommendations for institutional improvement, and provide decision-making support for university administration, including teaching, guidance, and resource allocation. The committee will also monitor the application of these recommendations.

4. To provide consultation and guidance on institutional research matters.

5. To address any other matters related to institutional research.

Article 5 Institutional research proposals are categorized into three types based on their nature: "University-wide Planning Proposals," "Unit-Self-Proposed Proposals," and "Task-Driven Proposals."

1. University-wide Planning Proposals: These are research projects executed by university-wide administrative and academic units, planned in accordance with the university’s medium- to long-term development plans, Ministry of Education competitive research programs, and other related initiatives. These proposals are developed in collaboration with the responsible executing units.

2. Unit-Self-Proposed Proposals: To encourage participation in institutional research, the university publicly invites faculty, staff, and units to propose research topics each academic year. Faculty or units select their research topics for study.

3. Task-Driven Proposals: These are research projects developed in response to school policies or the urgent needs of individual units. These proposals are not subject to a fixed submission schedule and may be proposed and applied for at any time.

## Article 6 Target Participants

1. All administrative and academic units within the university are required to propose and participate in institutional research each academic year.

2. All faculty and staff members of the university may freely propose and participate in research based on the open call for "Unit-Self-Proposed Proposals."

Article 7 All institutional research proposals for each academic year must be approved by the Institutional Research Advisory Committee. The approved proposals will receive research funding based on the available budget for the year, and the funds will be utilized in accordance with relevant financial regulations.

## Article 8 Evaluation Methods and Results

1. During the implementation of each research topic, the responsible units must participate in the Institutional Research Working Group. The effectiveness of each research topic must be reported in both mid-term and final reports. These reports will serve as references for improving the research and will be incorporated into the university's administrative self-assessment process.

2. Each year, the Institutional Research Advisory Committee, appointed by the university president, will conduct a preliminary evaluation of the institutional research topics completed in the previous academic year. The evaluation will be based on the design and research outcomes of each topic. After the preliminary evaluation, the proposals will be ranked according to their scores and recommended as the university's "Highlight Research Projects" for that year. These recommendations will be presented for further review at the Institutional Research Advisory Committee meeting.

3. The team members responsible for the selected "Highlight Research Projects" will be nominated for awards by the Secretariat Office and may be supported to present their research at relevant domestic and international conferences.

Article 9 Follow-up on Research Outcomes

Each academic year, follow-up will be conducted on the topics completed in the previous academic year, as well as on those topics that have not yet been concluded after previous follow-up. This is to assess the implementation and application of institutional research results in the policy adjustments of various units. The follow-up results will be submitted to the Institutional Research Advisory Committee for review and approval.

Article 10 These guidelines take effect upon approval by the Executive Council Meeting. The same procedure applies to any amendments.

In case of any discrepancy between the Chinese and English versions, the Chinese version shall prevail.
```

```markdown
# Yuan Ze University Regulations for Establishment of Sustainable Development Promotion Committee

October 26, 2022, Approved by the 7th Administrative Meeting of the 2022 Academic Year December 13, 2023, Approved by the 10th Administrative Meeting of the 2023 Academic Year August 27, 2025, Approved by the 2nd Administrative Meeting of the 2025 Academic Year

## Article 1

In order to effectively integrate sustainability-related affairs across administrative and academic units of the University, promote sustainability development and energy management policies, and enhance the University’s social impact externally, Yuan Ze University establishes the Sustainable Development Promotion Committee (hereinafter referred to as "the Committee") in accordance with Articles 7 and 12 of the University Organizational Charter, and hereby promulgates these Regulations.

## Article 2 The missions of the Committee are as follows:

1. To deliberate on the formulation or revision of sustainability development policies, targets, and related strategies.

2. To review sustainability action plans and assess their implementation performance.

3. To integrate internal and external sustainability resources and cross-unit task forces.

4. To promote the integration of teaching, research, and services with the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).

5. To foster a campus culture of sustainability and enhance sustainability-related competencies among faculty, staff, and students.

6. To promote the University's participation in sustainability initiatives with domestic and international organizations.

7. To approve annual sustainability-related reports.

8. To supervise, monitor, and review the implementation status and performance evaluation of social responsibility-related projects.

9. To formulate greenhouse gas and energy auditing mechanisms and implement related policies.

10. To handle other matters related to the promotion of sustainability development.

## Article 3

The Committee shall consist of the President, Vice President, Dean of Academic Affairs, Dean of Student Affairs, Dean of General Affairs, Dean of Research and Development, Dean of Information Officer, Chief Global Officer, Secretary General, Director of the Personnel Office, Director of the General Accounting Office, Director of the Environmental Protection and Occupational Safety and Health Center, Deans of all colleges, Director of General Studies, Director of the School of Lifelong Education, Director of the Center for Sustainable Development and Social Responsibility (who shall concurrently serve as the Executive Secretary), and one student representative. The President may, as needed, appoint several internal or external experts and scholars as committee members. The term of office for members is one year, and they may be renewed upon reappointment.

## Article 4

The President serves as the Chair of the Committee and designates one Vice President to serve concurrently as Vice Chair and Chief Sustainability Officer. The Committee shall convene at least once each semester, and may convene extraordinary meetings when necessary. A resolution of the meeting shall be adopted only when more than one-half of the committee members are present, and with the approval of a majority of the members present. Administrative affairs of the Committee shall be coordinated by the Center for Sustainable Development and Social Responsibility.

Article 5 Based on operational needs, the Committee may establish cross-unit Sustainability Working Groups. The composition and responsibilities of each working group are as follows:

1. Governance Working Group: Convened by the Secretary General. Members include representatives from Secretariat Office, General Accounting Office, Personnel Office, Office of Library and Information Services, College of Informatics, and Institutional Research Center. This group is responsible for deliberating sustainability management, university governance, information security management, and sustainable finance, in order to enhance overall the University’s governance effectiveness.

2. Social Working Group: Convened by the Dean of Student Affairs. Members include representatives from Office of Academic Affairs, Office of Global Affairs, Office of Student Affairs, College of Humanities and Social Sciences, College of General Studies, International Academy, and Arts Center. This group is responsible for deliberating social inclusion, teaching innovation, and international exchange, and for strengthening the University’s social responsibility and global engagement.

3. Environmental Working Group: Convened by the Dean of General Affairs. Members include representatives from the Office of Student Affairs, Office of General Affairs, Environmental Protection and Occupational Safety and Health Center, College of Engineering, College of Electrical and Communication Engineering, Physical Education Office, and Military Education Office. This group is responsible for deliberating energy management, smart campus initiatives, environmental management, and safety management, with the aim of creating a sustainable and safe campus environment.

4. Economic Working Group: Convened by the Dean of Research and Development. Members include representatives from Office of Research and Development,

College of Management, College of Medicine and Nursing, Industrial Academy, and School of Lifelong Education. This group is responsible for deliberating industryacademic research collaboration, local revitalization, and extension education, thereby promoting co-prosperity between the University, industry, and the community.

Article 6 These Regulations shall be promulgated and implemented upon approval by the Executive Council Meeting. The same procedure shall apply to any amendments.

In case of any discrepancy between the Chinese and English versions, the Chinese version shall prevail
```

```markdown
August 23, 2023 Approved at the 2nd Executive Council Meeting of the 2023 Academic Year
```

```markdown
# Yuan Ze University Regulations for Institutional Research Incentives

February 12, 2025. Approved by the 10th Executive Council Meeting of the 2024 Academic Year

## Article 1

To establish the distinctive features of institutional research at the University, encourage faculty and staff to participate in institutional research, enhance the professional management capabilities of various administrative units, and recognize and reward faculty and staff for their dedication and contributions to institutional research, these " Regulations for Institutional Research Incentives" (hereinafter referred to as "These Regulations") are formulated.

## Article 2 Eligibility:

Faculty and staff who participated in institutional research projects in the preceding year and completed all required data and report submissions in accordance with the Institutional Research Center’s guidelines are eligible to apply for this incentive.

## Article 3 Application Procedures:

Applicants shall submit the application form and relevant documents to the Institutional Research Center under the Secretariat Office after obtaining approval from their unit supervisor. The Institutional Research Center may also proactively recommend units or faculty members for application based on project execution.

## Article 4 Evaluation Criteria:

## 1. Research Design (40%)

## (1) Significance and Innovation of the Research Topic:

The research topic and content must align with the operational needs of the unit, the University’s development plan, or the Ministry of Education’s competitive grant programs. The research should also reflect educational trends, demonstrate forward-thinking perspectives, and contribute to advancing Institutional Research, University development, and competitiveness.

## (2) Research Framework and Empirical Validity:

The appropriateness of the research subjects and methodology, the scientific rigor of data collection and sampling methods, the robustness of the theoretical foundation, and the suitability of empirical analysis methods.

## 2. Research Outcomes (60%)

## (1) Feasibility of Research Recommendations:

The recommendations must be specific, practical, and valuable, addressing current academic or administrative challenges while considering potential execution difficulties and limitations.

## (2) Contribution of Research Implementation:

The practical application value of the research findings, contributions to resolving related issues, the effectiveness of proposed solutions, and the long-term benefits and continuous improvement potential after implementation.

Article 5 An "Institutional Research Incentive Review Committee" shall be established, comprising five committee members of the Institutional Research Advisory Committee appointed by the Principal for the current academic year and the Director of the Institutional Research Center.

## Article 6 Review Procedures:

Based on the application materials and final reports, a document review process will be conducted. If necessary, applicants may be invited for an oral presentation. If no applications meet the required standards, the award will be withheld.

## Article 7 Incentive Awards:

1. Outstanding Award: A prize of NT\$30,000 and a certificate of merit.

2. Excellence Award: A prize of NT\$20,000 and a certificate of merit.

3. Honorable Mention Award: A prize of NT\$10,000 and a certificate of merit. All awards are subject to tax deductions in accordance with government tax regulations.

## Article 8 Obligations:

Faculty members receiving Institutional Research incentives shall serve as exemplary figures in Institutional Research and support institutional development initiatives. They will be given priority invitations to present or share their findings at internal and external institutional research conferences.

## Article 9 Funding Source:

The Ministry of Education’s Higher Education Sprout Project will fund these incentives. Any matters not covered herein will be handled in accordance with the relevant regulations of the Ministry of Education and the University.

Article 10 These regulations take effect upon approval by the Administrative Meeting. The same procedure applies to any amendments.

In case of any discrepancy between the Chinese and English versions, the Chinese version shall prevail.
```
