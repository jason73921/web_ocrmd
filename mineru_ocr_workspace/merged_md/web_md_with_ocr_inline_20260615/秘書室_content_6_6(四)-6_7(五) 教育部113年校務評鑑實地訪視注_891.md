I hope this e-mail finds you well.

We would like to inform you that the Evaluation Committee for the Ministry of Education"s "2024th Annual University Campus Evaluation" will be visiting our campus on 6/6 (Thursday) and 6/7 (Friday) for an on-site evaluation. Regarding the meeting arrangements, we kindly ask for your assistance in the following matters:

According to the schedule planned by the MOE, the committee members will listen to a briefing on the current situation of our school"s affairs and teaching, review relevant materials, and tour the school facilities. The MOE will randomly select faculty, staff and students for meetings in advance. The time and location of the meetings will be individually notified by the secretariat. We kindly request that the selected faculty, staff and students cooperate and report to the designated time and place. We also ask teachers to kindly grant permission for students selected for meetings to take official leave (please see the explanation below) to attend the meetings.

We have a lot of experience in various kinds of evaluations in the past, and our performance in the previous evaluation (2018) was also very good. However, the indicators and operation mode of the evaluation have been slightly adjusted this time, and the result of the evaluation is even more important to the reputation of the university and the allocation of the relevant Ministry of Education awards and subsidies for the next six years, so we hope that all staff members and students will work together in order to facilitate the smooth conduct of the two-day evaluation visit.

Your cooperation and support in ensuring the success of this visit are greatly appreciated. Should you have any questions or need further clarification, please feel free to contact the secretariat: Ms. Sally Lu (ext. 2211), Ms. Haruna Liao (ext. 2213) and Ms. Josie Chang (ext. 2219).

Sincerely,

Secretary"s Office

Student Application for Official Leave Explanation

Regarding students requesting official leave for the "2024 Campus Affairs Evaluation On-site Survey or Interview," please follow the “Regulations Related to Absence for Students” stated in Article 2, Section 6:

Official Leave: Must be registered in the leave system one week in advance.

Students are requested to apply for official leave through the personal Portal and inform their assigned teachers of this message on the day of absence. The secretary"s office will provide proof documents (including a list of attending students) to the students.

Article 1 In order to cultivate students’ self-discipline and self-respect at Yuan Ze University, and in consideration of circumstances in which students are unable to participate in course learning activities or attend to matters related to their rights and interests due to major incidents, illness, or other significant reasons, these Regulations are hereby enacted.

## Article 2 Types of Leave:

## 1. Personal Leave

Leave for marriage, celebrations, or general personal matters. Applications shall be submitted through the leave application system within one week prior to the leave date (inclusive of the leave date).

## 2. Sick Leave

Applications shall be submitted through the leave application system within one week before or after the leave date.

## 3. Maternity Leave

Leave for pregnancy, childbirth, or miscarriage. Applications shall be submitted within one week before or after the leave date, with supporting medical certification attached. 56 days of maternity leave shall be granted following childbirth. For miscarriage after 3 months of pregnancy, 28 days of maternity leave shall be granted. For miscarriage before 3 months of pregnancy, 7 days of maternity leave shall be granted. The above leave days include weekends and holidays.

## 4. Prenatal Leave

Prior to childbirth, students may apply for a total of 7 days of prenatal leave.

Applications shall be submitted within one week before or after the leave date, with supporting documentation attached. Prenatal Leave may be taken in multiple installments.

## 5. Menstrual Leave

Female students who experience difficulty attending classes due to menstruation may apply for 1 day of menstrual leave per month (with an interval of at least 25 days between two applications). No supporting documentation is required. Applications shall be submitted within one week after the leave date.

## 6. Mental Health Leave

(1) Students who experience psychological or mental discomfort that affects their ability to attend classes may apply for leave. No supporting documentation is required. Applications shall be submitted within one week after the leave date. Leave is limited to 3 days per semester.

(2) When the cumulative number of mental health leave days reaches 2 days within the same semester, the student’s academic advisor shall be notified to provide priority care and, if necessary, refer the student to the Counseling and Career Services Division of the Office of Student Affairs.

## 7. Bereavement Leave

Students whose blood relatives within the second degree of kinship die may apply for bereavement leave by submitting an obituary or death certificate. For each case, the cumulative leave period shall not exceed 10 days within 100 days. Applications shall be submitted within one week before or after the leave date.

## 8. Official Leave

Applications shall be submitted through the leave application system within one week prior to the leave date.

(1) Students officially appointed to represent the University in off-campus or intercollegiate activities or competitions shall attach certification issued by the relevant unit or organizer.

(2) Students officially appointed to attend meetings at various levels of the University shall attach meeting notices as proof.

(3) Students officially appointed to support major on-campus events shall attach certification issued by the first-level supervisor of the appointing unit.

(4) Leave related to military service matters shall be supported by certification from the military authority.

(5) Indigenous students applying for leave to observe traditional festivals (as announced by the Council of Indigenous Peoples, Executive Yuan) shall attach a household registration transcript or household registration book, along with certification issued by a government authority.

9. Post–Natural Disaster Recovery and Reconstruction Leave

(1) Students whose families are affected by natural disasters such as typhoons, floods, earthquakes, or landslides, and who need to return home to assist with recovery or resettlement, may apply for leave based on actual needs. No supporting documentation is required. Applications shall be submitted within one week after the leave date, with a brief description of the disaster and the reason for returning home.

(2) The academic advisor shall be notified to provide priority care. The department chair shall coordinate with course instructors to provide make-up classes or online learning resources. Depending on the circumstances, the Student Life Counseling Division may provide living assistance or emergency aid, the Counseling and Career Services Division may provide psychological counseling, and the Campus Safety Center shall conduct follow-up monitoring.

## 10. Multicultural Leave

Leave taken due to the student’s nationality, ethnicity, traditional culture, local customs, religious practices, or ceremonial observances. Applications shall be submitted through the leave application system within one week prior to the leave date, with supporting documentation attached.

## Article 3 Approval Procedures:

1. For leave of 1 to 2 days, students shall submit applications through the leave application system for confirmation and filing.

2. For official leave and leave of 3 days or more (inclusive), students shall submit the application through the system and, within 3 days thereafter, submit supporting documentation to the Student Life Counseling Division for approval. Failure to submit documentation within the prescribed period shall be deemed not approved.

3. Upon approval, the leave application system shall notify relevant faculty members and the student by email.

4. In the event of emergencies that prevent advance application, students shall submit supporting documentation to the Student Life Counseling Division within one week after the incident.

Article 4 In respect of instructors’ autonomy in teaching activities, students who are unable to attend classes shall, in accordance with the principle of honesty, submit leave applications through the system and inform the course instructor. The course instructor may, at their discretion, approve or deny the leave and shall have full discretion to determine the extent to which such leave affects the student’s academic evaluation and grade.

Article 5 If any leave application or supporting documentation is found to be falsified or untrue, disciplinary action shall be taken in accordance with the Student Discipline Regulations and relevant procedures, depending on the severity of the offense.

Article 6 For leave taken during semester examinations, students shall submit an application through the leave application system and attach supporting documentation. Subsequent make-up examination arrangements may be processed only after approval by the course instructor.

Article 7 Any matters not covered herein shall be handled in accordance with the University Academic Regulations.

Article 8 These Regulations shall take effect upon approval by the Student Affairs Meeting. Amendments shall follow the same procedure.
