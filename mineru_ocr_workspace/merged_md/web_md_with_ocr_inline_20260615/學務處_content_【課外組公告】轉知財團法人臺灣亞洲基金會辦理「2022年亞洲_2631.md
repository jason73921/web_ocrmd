## About

The goalofSEASATYouthCamp is to enrich30-40 young people, ages18 to24,in their familiarity with their ASEAN,South Asian,and Taiwan neighbors with the hopes of promoting better understanding and cooperation among international youth.In collaboration with the American Institute in Taiwan, the three-day session will expose participants to lectures on contemporary issues affecting the region, cultural and educational tours, seminars, and a crash course on social innovation. TheCamp will also double as a networking platform to further connectSEASATYouth.

## Theme

Organizedby theAmerican Institutein Taiwanandthe Taiwan-Asia Exchange Foundation, the 2022 SEASAT Youth Camp aims to bring together young representatives in Taiwan to investigate howto enhance youth engagement in Asian democracies, explore ways in which the younger generation has responded to urgent issues both locally and globally through innovative projects, and design theirown practical agendas in achieving a more democratic and livable future where social discrimination is diminished, economic inequality reduced, and youth voices heard.

From the Milk Tea Alliance toMyanmar's civil disobidience movement, to feminist andLGBTQadvocacy,we have witnessed that youthhave been instrumental in initiating and sustaining socialtransformation across Asia.In addition,more youth are become actively engaged in politics, ensuring better institutional transition.While student and youth activism has separate historical roots and trajectories in Asia,their methods of critical engagement and coalition politics have significantly changed in response to recent developments in digital technology, international challenges, and the changing nature of democracy.

Contact MR. CHENG-HSIEN, CHANG Taiwan-Asia Exchange Foundation +886-2-2700-2367 ext.27 jol1382001@taef.org

## Topic

The2022YouthCamp identifies nine core elements that constitute the future of Asian democracies, namely "digital," "education,""migrants," "ocean,""culture,""resilience,""activism,""communication,"and "youth"— with the initial letters of these keywords constitute spelling democracy:

• Digital governance in the new Indo-Pacific order

•Educational reforms for the future of work

Migrants participation and integration for future democracy

Reframing maritime cooperation across Indo-Pacific oceans

Culture as the driving force in regional revitalization

Building city resilience and achieving SDGs

Youth in action for gender equality and social justice

New media communication and new ways of storytelling

• Fostering different futures for youth in Asia
