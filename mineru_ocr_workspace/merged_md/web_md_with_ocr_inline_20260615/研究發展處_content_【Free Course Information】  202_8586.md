Host Organization: Office of Academic Integrity, National Cheng Kung University (NCKU)

Date & Time: May 24, 2026 (Sunday), 14:10 – 16:00

Venue: Online via Webex

Topic: Responsible AI Application and Citations in Academic Ethics

Speaker: Lucien C.H. Lin (CC Taiwan Chapter Lead / Legal Consultant at G-Infor Intellectual Property Law Office)

Course Introduction:


Generative AI has become an essential tool in research, teaching, and professional work. Understanding "Responsible AI Application" is crucial for protecting rights and mitigating compliance risks. This lecture will begin with relevant legal frameworks and fundamental principles, analyzing recent legal controversies and copyright issues. It will also explain how to effectively utilize legitimate materials such as Creative Commons (CC) licensed works and open data. Furthermore, the speaker will provide practical examples of compliance and academic ethics regarding web scraping and the labeling of AI-generated outputs.
