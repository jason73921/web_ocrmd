# 全球環境教育夥伴(GEEP)亞太中心

# 2025 年環境教育國際工作坊簡章

## 一、 前言

全球環境教育夥伴亞太中心（以下簡稱亞太中心）自西元（下同）2021 年起辦理亞太地區環境教育國際交流活動，透過工作坊與研討會平臺，邀請亞太各國專家學者及青年行動者分享其環境教育經驗，促進跨國對話與知識共享。2025年亞太中心持續透過線上模式，串聯各地夥伴關注環境與永續議題，並以具體行動實踐聯合國永續發展目標，推動區域環境教育能量之交流與合作。

## 二、 活動緣起

我國環境部、美國環保署及北美環境教育學會（NAAEE）自2014 年共同推動全球環境教育夥伴計畫（Global Environmental Education Partnership, GEEP），2019 年在臺灣設立亞太區域中心（Asia-Pacific Regional Center, APRC），由臺灣承擔亞太區域環境教育推動角色。亞太中心持續整合各國資源與專家網絡，並辦理人才培訓與國際論壇，致力建構亞太地區環境永續發展之合作平臺。

## 三、 活動主題

【邁向永續的公民參與 Civic Engagement Towards Sustainable Development】

2025年度工作坊邀請臺灣及亞太地區國家（如不丹、韓國、日本、菲律賓、越南、馬來西亞、泰國等）環境教育專家、青年行動者與實務推動者，共同探討公民參與於循環經濟、淨零生活、氣候行動、生物多樣性等面向的實踐模式與創新策略，以及有關臺灣環境部環境教育成果分享，深化跨國夥伴合作與行動對話。

## 四、活動目的

1. 探討亞太地區各國於永續環境議題中公民參與之經驗與挑戰。

2. 分享我國推動綠生活、氣候行動及環境教育之成果，提升國際能見度。

3. 鼓勵青年參與，促進亞太地區彼此交流學習及未來合作。

## 五、辦理單位

主辦單位：環境部

• 執行單位：全球環境教育夥伴亞太中心（GEEP APRC）（由環境部委託邑沅有限公司維運）

## 六、活動時間與地點

本活動開放全球對環境教育有興趣的夥伴參加，於全球環境教育夥伴(GEEP)亞太中心網站（https://geepaprc.org/zh-tw）進行線上報名。

## 十、國際工作坊主題介紹

## （一） 主題背景

面對氣候變遷、生物多樣性危機與資源耗竭等全球性環境挑戰，唯有透過跨國合作與公民行動，才能共同邁向永續未來。本次由 GEEP APRC主辦的2025年國際環境教育工作坊，以「公民參與邁向永續發展」為主題，邀集亞太地區多國專家學者、政府單位、青年代表及民間組織，共同探討環境教育如何成為推動永續發展的核心動力。

議程涵蓋「循環回收」、「淨零綠生活」、「氣候行動」、「生物多樣性」、「臺灣環境教育分享」及「青年參與環境教育」等重要議題。透過兩日的深度交流與實務分享，工作坊期盼促進區域合作網絡，培育具國際視野與行動力的環境公民，為亞太地區及全球環境永續注入更多創新思維與集體力量。

## （二） 主題內容重點說明

1. 循環回收：面對資源有限與廢棄物激增的環境壓力，循環經濟成為全球永續政策的重要方向。本主題將介紹如何透過環境教育，推動大眾理解「從搖籃到搖籃」的理念，鼓勵產品設計源頭減廢、回收再利用與社會創新應用。透過來自越南、日本及臺灣環境部資源循環署等單位的實例分享，呈現亞太地區在政策、產業與教育現場的多元實踐。

2. 淨零綠生活：淨零碳排是全球面對氣候變遷的重要承諾，而實現淨零的關鍵，來自全民的綠色生活行動。此主題著重於個人與社區層面的節能減碳實踐，探討綠建築、綠色消費、交通與飲食習慣的轉變如何透過教育與政策引導發生改變。來自泰國、南韓、馬來西亞等國家的實務經驗，以及臺灣環境部綜合規劃司政策推動介紹，提供多樣的行動策略與跨國合作機會。

3. 氣候行動：氣候變遷已不再是遙遠的威脅，而是全球社會迫切應對的當前危機。本主題將探討地方氣候行動如何連結國際倡議與永續發展目標（SDGs），透過社區倡議、青年參與與創新溝通等策略，提升公民氣候素養與行動力。除臺灣環境部氣候變遷署說明臺灣推動氣候行動現況，並邀請來自菲律賓、不丹、越南等國家的分享，彰顯不同文化背景下的在地行動力量。

4. 生物多樣性：自然生態系統是人類賴以生存的基礎，而生物多樣性的喪失正迅速威脅全球糧食安全、水資源與健康發展。本主題將聚焦於保育教育如何結合科學研究、在地知識與社會參與，促進大眾對生態系統服務的理解與尊重。來自泰國、馬來西亞及臺灣農業部的專家將共同探討亞洲地區的保育成果與未來挑戰。

5. 臺灣環境教育分享：臺灣長期致力於推動法制化、社區化與國際化的環境教育政策與行動，成為亞太地區重要的環教合作夥伴。本主題將由環境部代表與學者分享臺灣在外籍青年實踐計畫、環境教育創新設計競賽、青年永續領袖培訓等方面的豐富經驗，呈現如何透過制度支持與多元參與，打造全齡共學的環教生態系。

6. 青年參與環境教育：青年是改變的推動者，亦是永續的關鍵角色。本主題透過青年座談方式，邀請來自臺灣與馬來西亞的歷屆青年領袖得主分享他們在社區行動、國際實習與倡議活動中的實踐歷程。探討如何透過環境教育賦能青年，讓他們從參與者轉化為發起者，進一步帶動同儕與政策對話，展現青年世代在環境轉型中的主體性與創造力。

透過這 6 大主題的探討，跨足政策、行動、教育與青年層面，不僅呈現亞太地區多元的永續實踐，也為參與者提供對話平臺與合作契機，期待共同建構一個更有韌性與包容力的永續未來。

## Global Environmental Education Partnership Asia-Pacific Regional Center (GEEP APRC)

## 2025 International Workshop on Environmental Education

## I. Preface

The Asia-Pacific Center of the Global Environmental Education Partnership (herein referred to as the Asia-Pacific Regional Center) has been organizing international exchange activities on environmental education in the Asia-Pacific region since 2021. Through workshops and seminars, experts, scholars and youth activists from various countries in the Asia-Pacific region have shared their environmental education knowledge and experiences, and promoted cross-border dialogue. In 2025, the Asia-Pacific Regional Center will continue to connect partners from all over the world online to focus on environmental and sustainable issues, implement the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals through concrete actions, and promote the exchange and cooperation in environmental education in the region.

## II. Origin of the Activity

Taiwan's Ministry of Environment, the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency, and the North American Association for Environmental Education (NAAEE) have been jointly promoting the Global Environmental Education Partnership (GEEP) since 2014, and established the Asia-Pacific Regional Center (APRC) in Taiwan in 2019 with Taiwan taking up the task of promoting environmental education in the Asia-Pacific region. The Asia-Pacific Regional Center continues to integrate resources and expert networks from various countries, organize talent training and international forums, and is committed to building a cooperation platform for environmental sustainable development in the Asia-Pacific region.

## III. Activity Theme

## 【Civic Engagement Towards Sustainable Development】

The 2025 workshop invites environmental education experts, youth activists, and practitioners from Taiwan and Asia-Pacific countries (such as Bhutan, South Korea, Japan, the Philippines, Vietnam, Malaysia, Thailand, etc.) to jointly discuss practical models and innovative strategies for citizen participation in circular economy and recycling, net-zero and green life, climate action, and biodiversity. Taiwan will also share its environmental education achievements to deepen cross-border partnership cooperation and dialogue.

## IV. Purpose

1. To explore the experiences and challenges of citizen participation in sustainable environmental issues in Asia-Pacific countries.

2. To share Taiwan’s achievements in promoting green lifestyle, climate action and environmental education, and enhance international visibility.

3. To encourage youth participation and promote mutual exchange, learning and future cooperation in the Asia-Pacific region.

## X. About this year’s theme

## i. Background

As we face global environmental challenges such as climate change, biodiversity crisis and resource depletion, only through transnational cooperation and citizen action can we move towards a sustainable future together. The 2025 International Environmental Education Workshop, hosted by GEEP APRC, is themed "Citizen Participation towards Sustainable Development." Experts, scholars, government agencies, youth representatives and non-governmental organizations from many countries in the Asia-Pacific region are invited to discuss how environmental education can become the driving force for promoting sustainable development.

The agenda covers important topics such as "Circular Economy & Recycling," "Net-Zero and Green Life," "Climate Action," "Biodiversity," "Sharing Achievements of Taiwan's Environmental Education," and "Youth Engagement in Environmental Education." Through two days of in-depth exchanges and experience sharing, the workshop is expected to promote regional cooperation networks, cultivate environmental citizens with international vision and action, and inject more innovative thinking and collective strength into environmental sustainability in the Asia-Pacific region and the world.

## ii. Key points of the subject content

1. Circular Economy & Recycling: In the face of limited resources and surging waste, the circular economy has become an important direction for global sustainable policies. This theme will introduce ways to promote public understanding of the "cradle to cradle" concept through environmental education, and encourage waste reduction at the source, recycling and reuse, and social innovation applications in product design. Through case studies from Vietnam, Japan, and Taiwan's Resource Circulation Administration, the event presents diverse practices in the industry, government policy and education in the Asia-Pacific region.

2. Net-Zero and Green Life: Net-zero carbon emissions is an important global commitment in the face of climate change, and the key to achieving net-zero lies in the green life actions of the entire citizenry. This theme focuses on energy conservation and carbon reduction practices at the individual and community levels, and explores how changes in green buildings, green consumption, transportation and eating habits can be achieved through education and policy guidance. Practical experience from countries such as Thailand, South Korea, and Malaysia, as well as policy promotion by the Department of Comprehensive Planning of Taiwan’s Ministry of Environment provide a wide range of strategies and international cooperation opportunities.

3. Climate Action: Climate change is no longer a distant threat, but a present crisis that the global community must address without delay. This theme will explore how local climate action can link international initiatives and the UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), and enhance citizens’ climate literacy and action through strategies such as community initiatives, youth participation and innovative communication. In addition to a report by the Climate Change Administration of Taiwan’s Ministry of Environment on the current status of Taiwan's promotion of climate action, speakers from countries such as the Philippines, Bhutan, and Vietnam will share their experiences, highlighting the power of local action with different cultural backgrounds.

4. Biodiversity: Natural ecosystems are the foundation of human survival, and the loss of biodiversity is rapidly threatening global food security, water resources and the development of good health. This theme will focus on how conservation education can combine scientific research, local knowledge and social participation to promote public understanding and respect for the ecosystem as well as ways to serve for a better ecosystem. Experts from the Ministry of Agriculture of Thailand, Malaysia and Taiwan will discuss conservation achievements and future challenges in Asia.

5. Sharing Achievements of Taiwan's Environmental Education: Taiwan has long been committed to promoting legislation, community-based and internationalized environmental education policies and actions, becoming an important environmental education partner in the Asia-Pacific region. This theme will be shared by representatives and scholars from the Ministry of Environment about Taiwan's rich experience in foreign youths’ programs, environmental education innovation design competitions, and training of youth leadership for sustainability. It will show how to create an environmental education ecosystem for all ages through institutional support and diverse participation.

6. Youth Engagement in Environmental Education: Young people are agents of change and key players in sustainability. This theme will be highlighted in the form of youth forums. Former Young Leaders winners from Taiwan and Malaysia are invited to share their practical experiences in community action, international internships and advocacy activities. This event will explore how to empower young people through environmental education, transform them from participants to initiators, further promote dialogue between peers and policies, and demonstrate the subjectivity and creativity of the younger generation in environmental transformation.

Through the discussion of these six major topics, spanning the levels of policy, action, education and youth, the diverse sustainable practices in the Asia-Pacific region will be presented. A dialogue platform and cooperation opportunities for participants will be provided, jointly building a more resilient and inclusive sustainable future.
