1. Campus Streetlights

Q1: What should I do if a campus streetlight is not working?


Faculty, staff, and students may either call the Construction and Maintenance Section directly or report the issue via [Personal Portal → Customer Service → Report an Issue → Engineering and Maintenance].

Visitors or external parties may contact the Construction and Maintenance Section directly or leave a message through the Office of General Affairs Feedback webpage.


2. Telephone Installation

Q1: How do I apply for the installation, relocation, or removal of an office telephone extension?


Please call or report through [Personal Portal → Customer Service → Report an Issue → Engineering and Maintenance].


3. Construction and Maintenance Announcements

Q1: How are scheduled construction, water outages, or power outages announced?


The Construction and Maintenance Section will notify all faculty, staff, and students in advance via email.


Q2: How are water or power outages handled?


For scheduled outages, the Construction and Maintenance Section will remain on standby to promptly restore supply and conduct system checks once service resumes.

For unexpected power outages, the Section will activate emergency power generators and provide updates after troubleshooting and resolving the issue.


4. Public Facilities and Campus Building Maintenance

Q1: What maintenance services does the Office of General Affairs provide, and how can I report an issue?


Public facilities: Include external main pipelines for water supply and drainage, water leaks, outages, and basement sewage/water pumps.

Campus buildings: Include roof or exterior wall leaks and falling tiles.

Reporting method: Call or report through [Personal Portal → Customer Service → Report an Issue → Engineering and Maintenance].


Q2: How are faulty lighting fixtures or consumables replaced in each department?


The Office of General Affairs provides only basic consumable items—fluorescent tubes and starters—for faculty, staff, and students to replace on their own.

Used or damaged tubes should be collected at the recycling area, and cleaning personnel in each building will handle their proper disposal.


Q3: How should emergency or safety-related maintenance issues be handled?


During office hours: Immediately contact the Construction and Maintenance Section.

After office hours: Notify the security guards at the campus gate, who will promptly contact the relevant units for emergency response.
