🔵 About the Program

 

1.What is the “Interdisciplinary Bachelor’s Program？


The Interdisciplinary Bachelor’s Program allows students to design their own study pathway by combining courses across departments and colleges. It offers maximum flexibility beyond the constraints of the original major and leads to the degree Bachelor of Interdisciplinary Studies.



2.Why does YZU offer this program?


The program aims to help students achieve both disciplinary depth and interdisciplinary breadth, strengthening adaptability, creativity, and career competitiveness.



3.How is this different from a double major or a minor？

A double major adds another full major, and a minor supplements your main major.
 The Interdisciplinary Bachelor’s Program redefines your entire degree, allowing you to construct a fully customized academic focus from campus-wide resources.

4.What degree will I receive?？


You will earn the degree Bachelor of Interdisciplinary Studies.



 


🔵 Application & Eligibility



1.Who is eligible to apply?


Undergraduate students in the second semester of Year 1 or in Year 2.



2.How many students are admitted each year?


Approximately 30 students (around 2% of the Year‑2 cohort), subject to official announcements.



3.When is the application period?


According to the application schedule announced each year.



4.What documents are required?


Academic transcript


Extracurricular or skill-related experience




Course-taking record




Study Plan (most important—should include motivation, course plan, and career goals)




5. Do I need approval from my home department?


No. Approval from your original department is not required.



6. How long does the review take, and how will I be informed?


Results will be announced by the Interdisciplinary Bachelor’s Office after review.



7. Can I reapply if I am not accepted?


Yes. You may revise and resubmit your application anytime before Year 3.



8. What are the key criteria for review?



Clarity of learning motivation and goals




Coherence between goals and course plan

 


Relevance of internships, projects, or capstone plans




Distinction from the student’s original major





🔵 Curriculum & Coursework

 

1. How many credits are required for graduation?


128 credits—the same as any undergraduate program.



2. What is the curriculum structure?



University/College Core: 31 credits




Free Electives: 30–40 credits




Program Modules: 40–50 credits




Independent Study / Internship / Capstone / Overseas Study: 15–25 credits




3. Can Free Electives include courses from my original major?


Yes. However, at least 10 credits must come from your chosen specialty area (AI, Semiconductor, Innovation, Sustainability).



4. Do students need to take courses outside their home college?


Yes. At least one cross-college course is required.



5. Will scheduling conflicts occur?


Exploration courses are usually offered on Wednesday or Friday afternoons to avoid clashes.



6. What are Exploration Courses?


Introductory interdisciplinary courses worth 1–2 credits.



7. Can the same course count toward multiple modules?


A course may fulfill multiple module requirements, but its credits may only be counted once toward the 128-credit total.



 


🔵 Interdisciplinary & Specialty Modules

 

1. What is an Interdisciplinary Module?


A 12–15 credit module combining courses across departments or colleges.



2. What are the four key domains?



Artificial Intelligence (AI)




Semiconductor




Innovation & Entrepreneurship




Sustainability




3. What is a Specialty Module?


A module based on existing departmental curricula focusing on professional depth.



4. Can I complete only interdisciplinary modules without specialty modules?


Yes. Students are free to mix and match modules.



 


🔵 Advising & Support

 

1. Do students have advisors?


Yes. Students are assigned two advisors:





A Professional Advisor (academic planning)




A Guidance Advisor (well‑being and career support)




2. Is there a dedicated administrative office?


Yes—the Interdisciplinary Bachelor’s Office provides full administrative and academic support.



3. What if I encounter course selection issues?


Contact your advisors or the program office for assistance with scheduling or add‑requests.



 


🔵 Degree Completion

 

1. How will the degree appear on my diploma?


Your diploma will state Bachelor of Interdisciplinary Studies and list your specialty area (e.g., Artificial Intelligence).



2. How do I qualify for a specialty notation?


You must complete:





At least 10 credits of Free Electives in the same field




At least 20 credits from modules in that field




3. Can I graduate with both my original major and the interdisciplinary degree?


Yes. It works similarly to a double major.



4. Can I drop my original major and graduate only with the interdisciplinary degree?


Yes, if you choose to withdraw from your home major.



5. Can I withdraw from the program midway?


Yes. You may return to your original major at any time, as long as you meet its graduation requirements.



 


🔵 Other Questions

 

1. Will joining this program delay graduation?


Not if you plan well—most students graduate on time.



2. What are the key benefits of this program?



Build your own customized academic pathway




Access resources across multiple colleges




Two‑advisor support system




High flexibility and freedom




Clear specialty notation on the diploma
