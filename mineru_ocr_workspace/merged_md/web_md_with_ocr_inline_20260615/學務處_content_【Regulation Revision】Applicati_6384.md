# Application Guidelines of Yuan Ze University Student Scholarship for Donations from Different Sectors

April 14, 2023 Approved by the 2nd Scholarship Review Committee of the 2022 Academic Year

October 31, 2025 Amended and Approved by the 1st Scholarship Review Committee of the 2025 Academic Year

1. YZU has established these guidelines to take care of and encourage outstanding students in straitened circumstances to concentrate on their studies, and effectively manage and coordinate non-specified purpose scholarships which is donated by Different Sectors.

2. If the scholarships donated by Different Sectors have specified purposes, they will be handled according to the mentioned purpose. It cannot be changed the purposes without the written consent of the donors. The non-specified purposes scholarships will be managed and utilized in accordance with these guidelines.

3. All enrolled undergraduate students with ROC (Taiwan) nationality who meet all of the following conditions may submit an application. Priority will be given to applicants who did not receive any other scholarships or grants in the previous semester, except for the Yu-Ziang Scholarship.

(1) Scholarship and Grant for Low-Income Students:

a. Students who are in straitened circumstances or need an Emergency Assistance.

b. Students achieved an average academic score of 75 or above with no fail subjects, and conduct score of 85 without disciplinary actions in the previous semester.

(2) Scholarship and Grant for Outstanding Low-Income Students:

a. Students who are in straitened circumstances or need an Emergency Assistance.

b. Students achieved an average academic score of 90 or above with no fail subjects, and conduct score of 85 without disciplinary actions in the previous semester.

4. The Low-Income Student Grant is generally awarded at NT\$5,000 to NT\$10,000 per student per semester. The Outstanding Low-Income Student Scholarship is generally awarded at NT\$10,000 to NT\$20,000 per student. The quotas and amount of scholarships are determined by the amount of donations received in the current year.

5. After new semester started, relevant information regarding the scholarships application will be announced by the Office of Student Affairs (OSA). Applicants should complete the online questionnaire and submit the following documents:

(1) Application form.

(2) Academic Transcript (Including Conduct Grades).

(3) Low-Income Household Certificate, Family in Hardship Certificate, Underprivileged Certificate (issued by the Village Leader) or Evidence of Suddenly Domestic Misfortune.

(4) Other relevant supporting documents.

6. The OSA will prepare a list of students applying for this scholarship in accordance with the aforementioned guidelines. It will be announced after review by the Scholarship Review Committee (SRC).

7. Matters not covered in these guidelines shall be handled in accordance with the "YZU Measures for the Management of Educational Resources Development Fund."

8. These guidelines shall be implemented upon approval by the SRC Meeting, Any amendments shall be processed accordingly

In case of any discrepancy between the Chinese and English versions, the Chinese version shall prevail.
