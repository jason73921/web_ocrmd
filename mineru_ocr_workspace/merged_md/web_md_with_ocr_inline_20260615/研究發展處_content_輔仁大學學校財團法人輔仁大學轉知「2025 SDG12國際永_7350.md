## SCOPE

As climate change intensifies and global issues like food distribution inequality gain attention, the United Nations' Sustainable Development Goal 12 (SDG 12) - “Ensure sustainable consumption and production patterns" — has become a key focus. This goal is also a central priority for Taiwan's industry, government, and academic sectors.

To address challenges such as wealth inequality, climate change, and gender equality, the UN launched the 2030 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) in 2015, outlining 17 core objectives. Among them, SDG 12 emphasizes the importance of green economies and sustainable production and consumption. The following research themes have been identified as key areas of focus, aligned with SDG 12's specific targets:

Promote sustainable consumption and production.

Encourage companies to adopt and report sustainable practices.

Ensure awareness of sustainability by 2030.

Remove fossil fuel subsidies and protect vulnerable communities.

Use resources efficiently and sustainably.

Manage chemicals and waste to protect health and the environment.

Reduce waste through prevention, recycling, and reuse.

Create tools to monitor sustainable tourism and support local culture and jobs.

Promote sustainable public procurement practices.

Adopting sustainable consumption and production practices.
