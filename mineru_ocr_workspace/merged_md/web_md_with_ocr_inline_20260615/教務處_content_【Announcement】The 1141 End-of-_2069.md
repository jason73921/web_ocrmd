## 一、文章基本資料

- **文章ID**: 2069
- **文章標題**: 【Announcement】The 1141 End-of-Semester Learning Questionnaire will begin at 12:00 PM on Dec. 4, 2025. Please log in to your personal Portal and actively participate in the survey!
- **文章網址**: https://www.yzu.edu.tw/admin/aa/index.php/tw/aa-202511281201-02
- **文章簡介**: Starting from the 2025-2026 Academic Year, the Learning Questionnaire has been updated to better reflect students’ learning experiences. The goal is to help students develop self-reflection, adopt proper learning attitudes and methods, and assist instructors in understanding students’ learning difficulties in order to enhance teaching effectiveness.

Your feedback is crucial for improving the quality of teaching at our university. In addition, your feedback helps encourage outstanding instructors to continue their efforts in teaching. Once the questionnaire is completed, instructors can review the results only after final grades have been submitted, ensuring that students’ responses do not influence their grades.

🕒【Survey Period】

From: 12:00 PM, Thursday, Dec. 4, 2025
 To: 12:00 PM, Saturday, Dec. 20, 2025

🧭【How to Access the Survey】

Path: Personal Portal → New Course Survey → My Learning Survey

🎁【Priority Course Selection for 1151 Semester】

Complete both the:


End-of-semester survey for 1141 semester

Beginning-of-semester survey for 1142 semester


You’ll have a chance to gain priority course selection rights for the 1151 semester!

Applicable to: Elective courses only 
 Reserved quotas for priority selection rights:


Courses with ≤ 35 students: 5 quotas

Courses with 36–70 students: 10 quotas

Courses with ≥ 71 students: 15 quotas


⚠️【Important Notes】


Please be mindful of the survey period and complete it on time through the Portal.

Before the survey begins, any courses you have applied to withdraw from but are still pending instructor approval must also be included in the survey!

The survey is anonymous. Please answer honestly, but do not include inappropriate remarks that could violate the law.


Thank you!
 Teaching Services Section, Office of Academic Affairs
