- **文章全文**: Secretariat office is mainly responsible for managing council affairs, official document processing, general administrative affairs, official ceremonies and activities, and general service counter. It also supports the office of President, as well as Vice President, by coordinating between various colleges, divisions and administrative councils





Since Secretariat Office carries on its mission to manage directors’ various affairs 
 and promote holistic university administration and significant projects, it is duty-bound to 
 play the roles of “administrative planner,” “negotiatory communicator,” “plan executor,” 
 and “emergency handler,” contriving attainable schemes, negotiating with interdisciplinary 
 divisions and departments and coping with problems of different sorts.

Currently there are 8 members and there is no division into groups in the office. Every member is mission-oriented.

Council Affairs

In terms of council affairs, the office is in charge of meetings, agendas and drafting of by-laws for the following councils:
 1) The University Council, 
 2) The Executive Council, 
 3) The Budget Planning and Auditing Committee
 4) The Administrative Quality Committee

   

General Administrative Affairs

General administrative affairs managed by the Secretariat Office include monitoring the implementation of 
 important university resolutions. The office is also responsible for reviewing the important plans and resolutions 
 submitted by various divisions of the university and recording important University events. In addition, it is also 
 in charge of collecting and compiling materials related to the history of the university.

Job Assignment





Briefly, Secretariat Office is in charge of the following tasks:

1.Planning for administrative development, budget and funds examination, and results assessment (evaluations of university affairs, faculty achievement, etc.)

2.Implementing quality system to upgrade holistic administrative services.

3.Preserving official documents and records of council meetings; compiling university history.

4.Hosting anniversary ceremony, the commencement of honorary doctoral degree and discussion for faculty and students;

5.Receiving honored guests; handling university’s public affairs; providing one-stop services.

6.Administering annual primary projects and university merging; reinforcing internationalization of administrations.
