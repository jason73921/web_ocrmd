- **文章簡介**: Influenza is an acute respiratory infection caused primarily by influenza viruses (types A and B). It is highly contagious, develops rapidly, and can easily lead to cluster infections. Seasonal outbreaks commonly occur during the autumn–winter and spring months each year.

▲symptoms

Usually comes on suddenly, and common symptoms include：

1、High fever (often > 38°C)

2、Cough (often dry)

3、Sore throat

4、Runny nose

5、headache and muscle aches。

6、General fatigue

7、Some patients may experience nausea, vomiting, or diarrhea (more common in children).

▲Mode of transmission

Main transmission routes:

1、Droplet transmission (when coughing or sneezing)

2、Contact transmission (touching virus-contaminated objects and then touching the nose or mouth)

After infection, symptoms appear within 1–4 days. The person is contagious one day before symptoms appear, and the contagiousness is strongest 5–7 days after onset.

▲Prevention Methods:

1、Get a flu vaccine.

2、Wash hands frequently (with soap for more than 20 seconds).

3、Wear a mask when symptoms appear.

4、Avoid touching eyes, nose, and mouth.

5、Maintain good indoor ventilation.

6、Wear a mask in crowded places.

7、Avoid going to public places, work, or school when sick.

【Flu Vaccine Information】

1、Before receiving a publicly funded flu or COVID-19 vaccine, it is recommended to make an appointment with a nearby contracted clinic or call ahead to confirm vaccine availability. This helps avoid long waits or unnecessary trips.

2、Clinic opening information is sourced from the National Health Insurance Administration and is for reference only. Since schedules may change unexpectedly, it is advised to call the clinic to confirm before getting vaccinated.
